# Lesson: The Aorist Optative — Active Voice (ending ‑ω)

**Goal:** By the end of this lesson you will be able to recognise, form, and translate the aorist optative active of regular verbs and understand its special contracted form that ends in **‑ω**.

---

## 1. What is the Aorist Optative?

| Term | Meaning in Ancient Greek |
|------|--------------------------|
| **Aorist** | A “simple past” aspect: the action is viewed as a whole, without reference to its internal duration. |
| **Optative** | A mood that expresses wishes, potential (what might happen), or polite/indirect requests. In Classical Greek it is used mainly in indirect speech, in conditional clauses, and after certain verbs of fearing, wishing, or commanding. |
| **Active Voice** | The subject of the verb performs the action (as opposed to passive where the subject receives it). |

The **aorist optative active** therefore expresses a *potential* or *wish* concerning a *simple‑past* action performed by the subject.

> **Example of use** – *“May he have won the race.”*  
> Greek: **νικήσῃ** (present optative) → **νικήσῃ** (aorist optative would be **νικήσῃ**? actually aorist optative of νικάω is **νικήσω**? We'll give correct forms later.)

## 2. Forms and Patterns

### 2.1 Regular Aorist Optative Active Endings

The aorist optative active is built from the **aorist active stem** (the same stem used in the aorist indicative without the augment) plus the following endings:

| Person | Ending | Example with **λύω** (to loosen) |
|--------|--------|-----------------------------------|
| 1st sg | **‑οιμι** → contracted **‑ω** in many verbs | λυ‑σ‑οιμι → **λῦσω** (contracted) |
| 2nd sg | **‑οις** | λυ‑σ‑οις → **λῦσῃς** |
| 3rd sg | **‑οι** | λυ‑σ‑οι → **λῦσῃ** |
| 1st pl | **‑οιμεν** | λυ‑σ‑οιμεν → **λῦσῃμεν** |
| 2nd pl | **‑οιτε** | λυ‑σ‑οιτε → **λῦσῃτε** |
| 3rd pl | **‑οιεν** | λυ‑σ‑οιεν → **λῦσῃεν** |

> **Note:** The **‑σ‑** is the aorist “thematic” consonant that appears in most aorist active stems (e.g., λυ‑σ‑). Verbs whose aorist stem already ends in a vowel (e.g., **ἔλαβ‑** from λαμβάνω) simply attach the endings directly: **ἔλαβ‑οιμι → ἔλαβοι** (rare, see § 3).

### 2.2 The Contracted Form **‑ω**

In many verbs the sequence **‑σ‑οιμι** contracts to **‑ω** (omega with a circumflex in the classical period). This is the most common way the aorist optative active appears in the 1st person singular.

| Verb (aorist stem) | Full form | Contracted form (1st sg) |
|--------------------|-----------|--------------------------|
| λυ‑σ‑ (from λύω)   | λυ‑σ‑οιμι | **λῦσω** |
| διδ‑ (from διδάσκω) | διδ‑σ‑οιμι | **διδῶ** |
| ἀγ‑ (from ἀγγέλλω) | ἀγ‑σ‑οιμι | **ἀγῶ** |
| ἔλαβ‑ (from λαμβάνω) | ἔλαβ‑οιμι | **ἔλαβοι** (no contraction) |

The contracted **‑ω** is therefore **not a separate ending**; it is the result of phonological contraction of **‑σ‑οιμι**. When you see **‑ω** in an aorist optative, think “‑σ‑οιμι” behind it.

### 2.3 Full Paradigm (Regular Verb)

Below is the complete paradigm for a regular aorist‑active‑stem verb **λῦσιν** (to loosen). The stem is **λῦσ‑**.

|          | **Active Aorist Optative** |
|----------|----------------------------|
| **1st sg** | **λῦσω** (contracted) |
| **2nd sg** | λῦσῃς |
| **3rd sg** | λῦσῃ |
| **1st pl** | λῦσῃμεν |
| **2nd pl** | λῦσῃτε |
| **3rd pl** | λῦσῃεν |

> The circumflex on the omega (ῶ) marks the contracted form. In later Greek the circumflex may be omitted in prose.

| Verb | Aorist Stem | Optative 1st sg (contracted) | Comment |
