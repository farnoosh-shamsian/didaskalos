# Lesson: The Present System, the Present Infinitive, and Middle/Passive (Deponent) Forms

*Target audience: beginner–intermediate students who know the Greek alphabet, basic noun declension, and the concept of a "stem."*

---

## 1. What the Present System Expresses

| Category | Meaning | Typical English equivalents |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Present indicative (active)** | Ongoing or habitual action, general truth | "I love," "the sun rises" |
| **Present indicative (middle)** | The subject acts on or for itself | "I wash myself" |
| **Present indicative (passive)** | The subject receives the action | "the city is being built" |
| **Present infinitive** | The non-finite "to-verb" form; after verbs of wishing and commanding, and in noun-like constructions | "to loose," "to be loosed" |
| **Deponent verbs** | Have only middle/passive forms but an **active** meaning | "I fear," "I come" |

> **Why study them together?** All these forms are built on one **present stem**; learn the pattern once and you can recognize them all.

---

## 2. The Present Stem

The present stem is found by removing the ending of the dictionary form: ‑ω for active verbs, ‑ομαι for deponents.

| Dictionary entry | Meaning | Present stem |
|---------|------------------|--------------|
| λύω | "to loose" | λυ‑ |
| παίζω | "to play" | παιζ‑ |
| ἀποκρίνομαι | "to answer" (deponent) | ἀποκριν‑ |
| φημί | "to say" (irregular, μι-verb) | φη‑/φα‑ |

*Irregular verbs (φημί, εἰμί, and the like) have their own special paradigms and are learned separately.*

---

## 3. Endings of the Present System

### 3.1 Active Endings (Indicative)

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | ‑ω | ‑ομεν |
| 2nd | ‑εις | ‑ετε |
| 3rd | ‑ει | ‑ουσι(ν) |

### 3.2 Middle Endings (Indicative)

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | ‑ομαι | ‑όμεθα |
| 2nd | ‑ῃ / ‑ει | ‑εσθε |
| 3rd | ‑εται | ‑ονται |

*The 2nd person singular goes back to older ‑εσαι: the σ dropped out between vowels and the vowels contracted.*

### 3.3 Passive Endings (Indicative)

In the present, the passive uses **the same endings as the middle**:

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | ‑ομαι | ‑όμεθα |
| 2nd | ‑ῃ / ‑ει | ‑εσθε |
| 3rd | ‑εται | ‑ονται |

In the **aorist** and **future** the passive has its own marker (‑θη‑), but in the present it is identical to the middle.

### 3.4 Present Infinitive Endings

| Voice | Ending |
|-------|--------|
| Active | ‑ειν |
| Middle | ‑εσθαι |
| Passive | ‑εσθαι (identical to middle) |

> **Result:** the infinitive ‑εσθαι can be either middle or passive; context decides.

---

## 4. Full Paradigm Example

The complete present indicative and infinitive of the regular verb **παίζω** "to play":

| **Active** | Singular | Plural |
|------------|----------|--------|
| 1st | **παίζω** | **παίζομεν** |
| 2nd | **παίζεις** | **παίζετε** |
| 3rd | **παίζει** | **παίζουσι(ν)** |

| **Middle/Passive** | Singular | Plural |
|----------------------|----------|--------|
| 1st | **παίζομαι** | **παιζόμεθα** |
| 2nd | **παίζῃ / παίζει** | **παίζεσθε** |
| 3rd | **παίζεται** | **παίζονται** |

| **Infinitive** | Form |
|----------------|------|
| Active | **παίζειν** |
| Middle/Passive | **παίζεσθαι** |

## 5. Deponent Verbs

### 5.1 What Is a Deponent?

- **Morphology:** only middle/passive forms; active endings never appear.
- **Semantics:** translated with an **active** meaning.

| Dictionary entry | Meaning |
|---------|------------------|
| φοβέομαι (φοβοῦμαι) | "to fear" |
| ἀποκρίνομαι | "to answer" |
| δέχομαι | "to receive, accept" |

### 5.2 A Contract Deponent Paradigm: **φοβοῦμαι**

| **Middle/Passive** (active meaning) | Singular | Plural |
|----------------------------------------|----------|--------|
| 1st | **φοβοῦμαι** | **φοβούμεθα** |
| 2nd | **φοβῇ / φοβεῖ** | **φοβεῖσθε** |
| 3rd | **φοβεῖται** | **φοβοῦνται** |

| **Infinitive** | Form |
|----------------|------|
| Middle/Passive | **φοβεῖσθαι** |

*The present infinitive of a deponent ends in ‑εσθαι (in contract verbs: ‑εῖσθαι) and is still translated actively: φοβεῖσθαι "to fear."*

### 5.3 Common Deponent Verbs (for reference)

| Dictionary entry | Meaning | Present infinitive |
|-------------------|---------|---------|
| ἀποκρίνομαι | to answer | ἀποκρίνεσθαι |
| βούλομαι | to want, wish | βούλεσθαι |
| γίγνομαι | to become, happen | γίγνεσθαι |
| δέχομαι | to receive, accept | δέχεσθαι |
| ἔρχομαι | to come | ἔρχεσθαι |
| μάχομαι | to fight | μάχεσθαι |
| αἰσθάνομαι | to perceive | αἰσθάνεσθαι |
| πορεύομαι | to travel, set out | πορεύεσθαι |
| ἡγέομαι | to lead; to consider | ἡγεῖσθαι |

## 6. Key Rules & Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **1. Present stem + ending** | The present stem is the base for all present-system forms. | λυ‑ + ‑ω → λύω |
| **2. Contraction** | In contract verbs the stem vowel merges with the ending. | φοβε‑ + ‑ομαι → φοβοῦμαι |
| **3. One form, two voices** | Middle and passive are identical in the present; context decides. | λύεται "is being loosed / looses for himself" |
| **4. Deponents never take active endings** | Translate the middle/passive form actively. | φοβοῦμαι = "I fear," not "I am feared" |
| **5. The infinitive ‑εσθαι is two-faced** | Context decides between middle and passive. | ἔρχεσθαι "to come" (middle); γράφεσθαι "to be written" (passive) |
| **6. Negate the infinitive with μή** | The infinitive normally takes μή, not οὐ. | μὴ φοβεῖσθαι "not to fear" |
| **7. Irregular verbs** | εἰμί and φημί have special stems and endings. | εἰμί → εἶναι; φημί → φάναι |

## 7. Example Sentences

| Greek | Translation | Notes |
|------------------------------|-------------|-------|
| **λύω τὸν δεσμόν.** | I loose the bond. | Present indicative active. |
| **ὁ παῖς λούεται.** | The boy washes himself. | Middle (reflexive). |
| **δεῖ τὸν ἵππον λύεσθαι.** | The horse must be untied. | Present infinitive passive (‑εσθαι). |
| **φοβοῦμαι τὰ θηρία.** | I fear the beasts. | Deponent: middle form, active meaning. |
| **ἔρχομαι εἰς τὴν πόλιν.** | I am coming into the city. | Deponent of motion. |
| **οἱ πολῖται βούλονται ἀκούεσθαι ὑπὸ τῶν ἀρχόντων.** | The citizens want to be heard by their rulers. | Passive infinitive after a verb of wishing. |

---

## 8. Usage & Context

1. **Narrative prose** — the present indicative is used for vivid narration (the historical present).
2. **The infinitive** — appears after verbs of wishing, commanding, and perceiving (βούλομαι, κελεύω) and behaves like a noun with the article τό.
3. **Philosophical and legal language** — deponents are frequent here (ἡγέομαι "to consider," ἀποκρίνεσθαι "to answer").
4. **Middle vs. passive** — middle means acting for or on oneself; passive means the subject receives the action. In many sentences one form can carry either sense; the dictionary and the context are your guides.

---

## 9. Historical Development & Etymology

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Proto-Indo-European origin** | The Greek present system continues the present stems of the parent language; the middle voice arose from its reflexive constructions and was later grammaticalized. |
| **Development within Greek** | Homeric Greek preserves some older forms and endings; in Classical Attic ‑ω became standard for the active and ‑ομαι for the middle/passive. |
| **Convergence of middle and passive** | In the present system the two voices were formally identical from the start; Greek created a separate passive only in the aorist and future. |
| **The fate of deponents** | Many classical deponents lived on in the Koine (γίνομαι, ἔρχομαι, δέχομαι), and some gradually acquired passive-like forms. |
