# Lesson: The Imperfect Indicative Middle/Passive of **‑μι** Verbs

---

## 1. What the Imperfect Middle/Passive Is

| Term | Meaning | Use |
|------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Imperfect** | The past aspect of "in progress" or "repeated": an action or state that continued in the past. | "he was saying," "they used to go." |
| **Indicative** | The "real" mood — statement, question, ordinary narration. | Not a wish, command, or possibility. |
| **Middle/Passive** | In the imperfect, middle and passive have **identical endings**: middle = for oneself; passive = done to the subject. | "he was washing himself" / "he was being washed." |
| **(μι)** | This lesson concerns the athematic verbs: δίδωμι, τίθημι, ἵστημι, and archaic deponents such as δύναμαι and κεῖμαι. | — |

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns

### 2.1 The Secondary Middle Endings (Athematic)

In μι-verbs the endings attach **directly to the stem, without a thematic vowel**:

| Person | Ending | Note |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| 1st sg. | **‑μην** | — |
| 2nd sg. | **‑σο** | The σ survives, since it is not between two vowels: ἐτίθε-σο |
| 3rd sg. | **‑το** | — |
| 1st pl. | **‑μεθα** | — |
| 2nd pl. | **‑σθε** | — |
| 3rd pl. | **‑ντο** | — |

*Compare the thematic verbs: in ἐλύου (from ἐλύε-σο) the σ dropped and contraction followed; in ἐτίθεσο it remains.*

### 2.2 Construction: Augment + Short Present Stem + Ending

In the middle/passive imperfect, μι-verbs use the **short stem** (the same as in the active plural): τιθε‑, διδο‑, ἱστα‑.

### 2.3 Full Paradigms

**τίθημι "to place" → ἐτιθέμην "I was placing for myself / was being placed":**

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|------|--------|
| 1st | **ἐτιθέμην** | **ἐτιθέμεθα** |
| 2nd | **ἐτίθεσο** | **ἐτίθεσθε** |
| 3rd | **ἐτίθετο** | **ἐτίθεντο** |

**δίδωμι "to give" → ἐδιδόμην:**

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|------|--------|
| 1st | **ἐδιδόμην** | **ἐδιδόμεθα** |
| 2nd | **ἐδίδοσο** | **ἐδίδοσθε** |
| 3rd | **ἐδίδοτο** | **ἐδίδοντο** |

**ἵστημι "to set up" → ἱστάμην (middle: "I was standing"):**

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|------|--------|
| 1st | **ἱστάμην** | **ἱστάμεθα** |
| 2nd | **ἵστασο** | **ἵστασθε** |
| 3rd | **ἵστατο** | **ἵσταντο** |

### 2.4 High-Frequency Athematic Deponents

| Verb | Meaning | Imperfect (1st/3rd sg.) |
|------|------|------------------|
| **δύναμαι** | to be able | **ἐδυνάμην / ἐδύνατο** |
| **κεῖμαι** | to lie, be situated | **ἐκείμην / ἔκειτο** |
| **ἐπίσταμαι** | to know | **ἠπιστάμην / ἠπίστατο** |

*These three verbs are extremely common in prose: ἐδύνατο "he could," ἔκειτο "it lay."*

## 3. Key Rules & Common Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **1. Augment** | ἐ‑ before a consonant; lengthening of an initial vowel. | τίθημι → **ἐ**τιθέμην; ἐπίσταμαι → **ἠ**πιστάμην |
| **2. The short stem** | The middle/passive always uses the short stem: τιθε‑, διδο‑, ἱστα‑. | ἐτίθετο (not *ἐτίθητο) |
| **3. The σ of the 2nd person survives** | Because after the consonant-free athematic stem the σ is not intervocalic. | ἐδίδοσο, ἵστασο |
| **4. Middle or passive? Context!** | ἐδίδοτο = "was given (for himself)" or "was being given"; with ὑπό + genitive it is definitely passive. | ἐδίδοτο ὑπὸ τοῦ βασιλέως |
| **5. εἰμί has no middle/passive** | Its imperfect (ἦν) has only one set of forms. | — |
| **6. Do not confuse with the aorist middle** | ἐτίθετο (imperfect) but ἔθετο (aorist): the reduplication τι‑ marks the present system. | ἐδίδοτο / ἔδοτο |

---

## 4. Example Sentences

| Greek (imperfect middle/passive) | Transliteration | English |
|-----------------------------------|----------------|---------|
| **οἱ νόμοι ἐτίθεντο ὑπὸ τῶν πολιτῶν.** | hoi nómoi etíthento… | The laws were being enacted by the citizens. |
| **τὰ δῶρα ἐδίδοτο τοῖς θεοῖς.** | ta dôra edídoto… | The gifts were being given to the gods. |
| **ὁ στρατὸς ἵστατο πρὸ τῶν πυλῶν.** | ho stratòs hístato… | The army was taking its stand before the gates. |
| **οὐκ ἐδύνατο λέγειν.** | ouk edýnato légein | He was unable to speak. |
| **τὸ ξίφος ἔκειτο ἐπὶ τῆς τραπέζης.** | to xíphos ékeito… | The sword lay on the table. |

---

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Narrative background** — ongoing states and processes: ἔκειτο, ἵστατο, ἐδύνατο build the scene.
2. **Institutional/repeated action** — lawgiving, continual giving: ἐτίθεντο νόμοι "laws were being enacted."
3. **Passive with agent** — ὑπό + genitive: ἐδίδοτο ὑπὸ… "was being given by…"
4. **Deponents always translate actively** — ἐδύνατο "he could," ἠπίστατο "he knew."

---

## 6. Historical Development & Etymology

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **The oldest layer** | Attaching the ending directly to the stem (without a thematic vowel) is an intact inheritance from Proto-Indo-European. |
| **The middle endings** | ‑μην, ‑σο, ‑το… have parallels in Sanskrit and the other related languages. |
| **κεῖμαι and δύναμαι** | Among the oldest athematic verbs; κεῖμαι functions practically as the "passive" of τίθημι: ἔκειτο "it had been placed / it lay." |
| **Development** | In the Koine these formations were rebuilt on the analogy of thematic verbs, and in Modern Greek only relics of the athematics survive. |
