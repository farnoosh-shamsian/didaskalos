# Lesson: The Aorist – Indicative – Active (Irregular Forms)

*Target level: Beginner‑Intermediate*  
*Prerequisites: Greek alphabet, basic noun declension, present‑tense verb forms.*

---

## 1. What the Aorist Indicative Active Is  

| Term | Meaning in Greek grammar | Rough English equivalent |
|------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Aorist** | A past‑time aspect that presents an action as a simple, completed whole, without reference to duration or repetition. | “did”, “went”, “saw” (simple past) |
| **Indicative** | The mood used for statements of fact or reality. | – |
| **Active** | The subject performs the action (as opposed to middle or passive). | – |

The **aorist indicative active** therefore tells us *who* performed *what* in the past, as a single, finished event.

> **Contrast** – The imperfect (continuous past) says “was doing”, the aorist says “did”.  
> **Example**:  
> - *ἔλυον* = “I was loosening” (imperfect)  
> - *ἔλυσα* = “I loosened / I have loosened” (aorist)

## 2. Regular vs. Irregular Aorist Stems  

### 2.1 The regular (‑σα) aorist  

Most verbs form the aorist active with the **‑σα** suffix added to the verb stem:

| Person | Ending | Example with **λύω** (to loosen) |
|--------|--------|-----------------------------------|
| 1 sg | ‑α | ἔλυσα |
| 2 sg | ‑ας | ἔλυσας |
| 3 sg | ‑ε(ν) | ἔλυσε(ν) |
| 1 pl | ‑αμεν | ἐλύσαμεν |
| 2 pl | ‑ατε | ἐλύσατε |
| 3 pl | ‑αν | ἔλυσαν |

The **augment** (ε‑) is added at the beginning of the aorist stem in the indicative (except in the 3 sg where the augment may be omitted in poetry).  

### 2.2 Irregular aorist stems  

A relatively small group of very common verbs does **not** use the ‑σα suffix. Their aorist stems are formed by:

* a change of the vowel or consonant (ablaut)  
* a different suffix (‑ον, ‑α, ‑αν, etc.)  
* sometimes a completely different root

These verbs are called **irregular aorists**. They must be memorised individually, but they all share the same set of **indicative active endings** (‑ον, ‑ας, ‑ε(ν), ‑αμεν, ‑ατε, ‑αν) **except where the verb supplies its own ending** (e.g., ἔδωκα).

Below is a **paradigm table** that shows the six personal endings for the irregular aorist active indicative. In each verb’s paradigm the stem is highlighted in **bold**.

| Person | Regular‑aorist ending | Irregular‑aorist ending (most verbs) |
|--------|------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| 1 sg | **‑α** (e.g., ἔλυ**σα**) | **‑ον** (e.g., ἔλαβ**ον**) |
| 2 sg | **‑ας** (ἔλυ**σας**) | **‑ας** (ἔλαβ**ας**) |
| 3 sg | **‑ε(ν)** (ἔλυ**σε(ν)**) | **‑ε(ν)** or **‑α** (ἔδω**κ**α) |
| 1 pl | **‑αμεν** (ἐλύσ**αμεν**) | **‑αμεν** (ἔλαβ**αμεν**) |
| 2 pl | **‑ατε** (ἐλύσ**ατε**) | **‑ατε** (ἔλαβ**ατε**) |
| 3 pl | **‑αν** (ἔλυσ**αν**) | **‑αν** (ἔλαβ**αν**) |

> **Note:** The augment ε‑ is prefixed to the stem in all persons except the 3 sg of some poetic forms.
