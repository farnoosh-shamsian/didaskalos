# Lesson: The Imperfect Indicative Active of μι-Verbs

**Target:** Beginner–intermediate students of Ancient Greek
**Focus:** Understanding, forming, and using the imperfect indicative active of the μι-verb class.

---

## 1. What the Imperfect Indicative Active Means

| Term | Meaning | Greek function |
|------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Imperfect** | A past action that was **ongoing, repeated, or incomplete** ("was doing," "used to do"). | A continuous or habitual past situation. |
| **Indicative** | The "real" mood — statements of fact. | Ordinary narration. |
| **Active** | The subject performs the action. | — |
| **(μι)** | This lesson concerns the archaic μι-verbs: εἰμί, φημί, δίδωμι, τίθημι, ἵστημι. | — |

The imperfect answers the question *"What was going on?"* It is not the simple past (that is the aorist) but the **past-in-progress**.

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns

### 2.1 A Thematic Verb for Comparison (λύω "to loose")

**Augment + present stem + thematic vowel + secondary endings:**

| Person | Form | Gloss |
|--------|------|-------|
| 1 sg | ἔλυον | I was loosing |
| 2 sg | ἔλυες | you were loosing |
| 3 sg | ἔλυε(ν) | he/she/it was loosing |
| 1 pl | ἐλύομεν | we were loosing |
| 2 pl | ἐλύετε | you (pl.) were loosing |
| 3 pl | ἔλυον | they were loosing |

### 2.2 The Principle of μι-Verbs

μι-Verbs attach the secondary endings **directly to the present stem (with its reduplication), without the thematic vowel**; the stem usually has a long vowel in the singular and a short vowel in the plural:

**δίδωμι "to give" → ἐδίδουν:**

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| 1st | ἐδίδουν | ἐδίδομεν |
| 2nd | ἐδίδους | ἐδίδοτε |
| 3rd | ἐδίδου | ἐδίδοσαν |

**τίθημι "to place" → ἐτίθην:**

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| 1st | ἐτίθην | ἐτίθεμεν |
| 2nd | ἐτίθεις | ἐτίθετε |
| 3rd | ἐτίθει | ἐτίθεσαν |

**ἵστημι "to set up" → ἵστην:**

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| 1st | ἵστην | ἵσταμεν |
| 2nd | ἵστης | ἵστατε |
| 3rd | ἵστη | ἵστασαν |

*(In ἵστημι the augment has merged with the initial ἱ‑ and shows only in the length of the vowel.)*

### 2.3 The Verb φημί "to say"

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| 1st | **ἔφην** | **ἔφαμεν** |
| 2nd | **ἔφησθα / ἔφης** | **ἔφατε** |
| 3rd | **ἔφη** | **ἔφασαν** |

*ἔφη "he said" is among the most frequent verb forms in narrative texts.*

### 2.4 The Verb εἰμί "to be"

| Person | Form | Transliteration | Gloss |
|--------|------|----------------|-------|
| 1 sg | ἦν (older: ἦ) | ên | I was |
| 2 sg | ἦσθα | êstha | you were |
| 3 sg | ἦν | ên | he/she/it was |
| 1 pl | ἦμεν | êmen | we were |
| 2 pl | ἦτε | ête | you (pl.) were |
| 3 pl | ἦσαν | êsan | they were |

*The ἦ‑ is the augment fused with the root ἐσ‑ (ἐ + ἐσ → ἠσ): the apparently "augmentless" look is deceptive.*

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **1. The augment is always there** | ἐ‑ before a consonant; vowel lengthening in vowel-initial verbs. | δίδωμι → **ἐ**δίδουν |
| **2. No thematic vowel** | The endings attach directly to the stem: ἐτίθε‑σαν, not *ἐτιθέ‑ο‑σαν. | ἐδίδοσαν |
| **3. Long singular, short plural** | ἐτίθην / ἐτίθεμεν; ἵστην / ἵσταμεν. | — |
| **4. 3rd plural in ‑σαν** | The familiar signature of the μι-class. | ἐδίδοσαν, ἐτίθεσαν, ἦσαν |
| **5. The singular ἐδίδουν follows the contract verbs** | ἐδίδουν, ἐδίδους, ἐδίδου are formed by analogy with ἐποίουν. | — |
| **6. Imperfect ≠ aorist** | ἐτίθην "I was placing" but ἔθηκα "I placed"; ἵστην "I was setting up" but ἔστην "I stood." | — |
| **7. The present reduplication remains** | The imperfect is built on the present stem: δι‑, τι‑, ἱ‑ are kept. | ἐδίδουν (not *ἔδουν) |

## 4. Example Sentences

| Greek (imperfect) | Transliteration | English |
|-------------------|----------------|---------|
| **ἦν ὁ ἀνὴρ ἐν τῇ ἀγορᾷ.** | ên ho anḕr en têi agorâi | The man was in the marketplace. |
| **ἐδίδου τοῖς πτωχοῖς ἀεί.** | edídou toîs ptōkhoîs aeí | He always used to give to the poor. |
| **ἔφη τοὺς ξένους ἥκειν.** | éphē tous xénous hḗkein | He said that the strangers had come. |
| **ἵστασαν τρόπαιον.** | hístasan trópaion | They were setting up a trophy. |
| **ἦσαν οἱ παῖδες ἐν τῷ κήπῳ.** | êsan hoi paîdes en tôi kḗpōi | The children were in the garden. |

---

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Narrative background** — the imperfect sets the scene: "While X was going on, Y happened."
2. **Repeated and habitual actions** — "he used to give every day" (ἐδίδου).
3. **Simultaneity** — two simultaneous past actions both stand in the imperfect.
4. **Contrast with the aorist** — the aorist reports the event, the imperfect the flow: ἦν... καὶ ἐγένετο "it was so… and then it happened."
5. **The frequency of εἰμί and φημί** — ἦν and ἔφη occur several times on every page of narrative prose; memorize these first.

---

## 6. Historical Development & Etymology

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Indo-European formation** | Imperfect = augment *e‑ + present stem + secondary endings — a formation Sanskrit shares exactly. |
| **The augment** | The relic of an ancient past-marking particle; in Greek it survived only in the indicative of past tenses. |
| **μι-Verbs** | The oldest layer of conjugation: endings attached directly to the stem, before the thematic vowel arose. |
| **εἰμί** | Root *h₁es‑ "to be"; ἦν results from the fusion of augment and root (ἐ + ἐσ). |
| **Aftermath** | Modern Greek keeps a living imperfect (παρατατικός), but the μι-verbs have migrated into the ω-pattern. |
