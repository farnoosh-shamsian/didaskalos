# Lesson: The Aorist Middle Infinitive of ω-Verbs

*Target level: Beginner–Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Aorist indicative middle, present middle infinitive, middle voice basics.*

---

## 1. What Is the Aorist Middle Infinitive?

The **infinitive** is the non-finite form of the verb: no person, no number.
The **aorist** presents an action as a single complete whole. In the infinitive it carries **no reference to past time** — only the aspect "once and done."
The **middle** voice means the subject acts **for itself, on itself, or in its own interest**.

So **λύσασθαι** is not "to have loosed" but "to ransom (someone, for oneself)" — a single act, seen as done in the subject's own interest.

| Form | Aspect | Sense |
|---|---|---|
| λύεσθαι (present middle) | ongoing / repeated | "to be ransoming, to keep ransoming" |
| **λύσασθαι** (aorist middle) | single, complete | "to ransom (once)" |

Remember that many verbs shift meaning in the middle: αἱρέω "take" → αἱροῦμαι "choose"; παύω "stop (someone)" → παύομαι "cease".

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns

The formation depends on the verb's **aorist stem type** — exactly as in the indicative.

### 2.1 First (sigmatic) aorist — ending **‑σασθαι**

Aorist stem in ‑σα‑ + **‑σθαι**:

| Verb | Aorist middle indicative | Aorist middle infinitive | Sense |
|---|---|---|---|
| λύω "loose" | ἐλυσάμην | **λύσασθαι** | to ransom |
| παύω "stop" | ἐπαυσάμην | **παύσασθαι** | to cease |
| βουλεύω "plan" | ἐβουλευσάμην | **βουλεύσασθαι** | to deliberate |
| τιμάω "honour" | ἐτιμησάμην | **τιμήσασθαι** | to value, esteem |
| φυλάττω "guard" | ἐφυλαξάμην | **φυλάξασθαι** | to be on guard |

### 2.2 Second (thematic) aorist — ending **‑έσθαι**

Second-aorist stem + the thematic ending, always accented on the penult:

| Verb | Aorist middle indicative | Aorist middle infinitive | Sense |
|---|---|---|---|
| λαμβάνω "take" | ἐλαβόμην | **λαβέσθαι** | to take hold of |
| αἱρέω "take" | εἱλόμην | **ἑλέσθαι** | to choose |
| γίγνομαι "become" | ἐγενόμην | **γενέσθαι** | to become, happen |
| πυνθάνομαι "inquire" | ἐπυθόμην | **πυθέσθαι** | to learn by inquiry |
| πείθω "persuade" | ἐπιθόμην | **πιθέσθαι** | to obey |

### 2.3 The Three Voices Side by Side (λύω)

| Voice | Aorist infinitive | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Active | λῦσαι | to loose |
| **Middle** | **λύσασθαι** | to ransom (for oneself) |
| Passive | λυθῆναι | to be loosed |

---

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **1. No augment** | The augment belongs to the indicative only. Strip it before forming the infinitive. | ἐλυσάμην → λύσασθαι (not *ἐλύσασθαι*) |
| **2. Stem type decides the ending** | Sigmatic aorists take ‑σασθαι; second aorists take ‑έσθαι. | παύσασθαι vs. γενέσθαι |
| **3. Accent** | ‑σασθαι infinitives accent the penult (λύσασθαι); second-aorist middle infinitives accent the penult too, on the ε: γενέσθαι, λαβέσθαι. | |
| **4. Aspect, not time** | The aorist infinitive says nothing about past time except in indirect discourse. | βούλεται παύσασθαι "he wants to stop" |
| **5. In indirect discourse it does mark time** | After verbs of saying/thinking, the aorist infinitive represents a past indicative. | ἔφη παύσασθαι "he said that he had ceased" |
| **6. Middle ≠ passive** | The aorist keeps them apart: λύσασθαι (middle) vs. λυθῆναι (passive). Only the present and imperfect merge them. | |

---

## 4. Example Sentences

| Greek | Translation |
|---|---|
| ἐβούλοντο **παύσασθαι** τοῦ πολέμου. | "They wished to cease from the war." |
| ἔδοξεν αὐτοῖς **ἑλέσθαι** στρατηγούς. | "They resolved to choose generals." |
| χαλεπόν ἐστι τὴν ἀλήθειαν **πυθέσθαι**. | "It is hard to learn the truth." |
| ἀνάγκη ἦν τοῖς νόμοις **πιθέσθαι**. | "It was necessary to obey the laws." |
| φησὶ ταῦτα **γενέσθαι**. | "He says that this happened." |

---

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Complement of a verb** — after βούλομαι, ἐθέλω, δύναμαι, δοκεῖ: ἐβουλήθη λύσασθαι τὸν υἱόν "he wished to ransom his son."
2. **With impersonals** — δεῖ, χρή, ἀνάγκη ἐστί: δεῖ παύσασθαι "one must stop."
3. **Indirect discourse** — after φημί, λέγω, νομίζω, the aorist infinitive stands for an aorist indicative of the direct speech: this is the one place where it does imply past time.
4. **Articular infinitive** — τὸ γενέσθαι "the happening, the fact of coming to be"; very common in Thucydides' abstract prose.
5. **After ὥστε** — expressing actual result: ὥστε πάντας πυθέσθαι "so that everyone learned of it."

Thucydides is especially fond of γενέσθαι and πυθέσθαι in reported speech and in his analyses of cause.

---

## 6. Historical Development & Etymology

- The middle ending **‑σθαι** is the regular infinitive marker of the middle voice throughout the Greek verb (present λύεσθαι, perfect λελύσθαι, aorist λύσασθαι) — a stable formation from Proto-Greek onwards.
- The **‑σα‑** of the first aorist reflects the Indo-European sigmatic aorist suffix *‑s‑, seen also in Latin *dīxī* and Sanskrit *ábhārṣam*.
- The **second aorist** preserves the older athematic-root pattern; its infinitive ‑έσθαι shows the thematic vowel that the sigmatic type lacks.
- In Koine the middle voice steadily lost ground to the passive, and in Modern Greek the infinitive disappeared altogether, replaced by να-clauses.
