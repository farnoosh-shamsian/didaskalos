# Lesson: The Aorist Active Participle — Irregular Forms

*Target level: Beginner–Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Greek alphabet, basic verb endings, the concept of the aorist tense.*

---

## 1. What Is the Aorist Active Participle?

| Term | Meaning in Greek | Rough English equivalent |
|------|------------------|--------------------------|
| **Aorist** | A "punctual," unitary action, without reference to duration. | "did / having done" |
| **Active** | The subject performs the action. | "he/she does …" |
| **Participle** | A verbal adjective declined like a noun/adjective. | "the one who did," "having done," "after doing" |

The aorist active participle describes a **completed** action performed by the noun it modifies, and like an adjective it has gender, number, and case.

> **Example** — *ἐλθών* "having come / the one who came."

---

## 2. Forming the Aorist Active Participle

### 2.1 The Two Basic Patterns

1. **First aorist (sigmatic):** stem + **‑σας/‑σασα/‑σαν**: λύσας, γράψας.
2. **Second aorist (thematic):** stem + **‑ών/‑οῦσα/‑όν** with the accent on the ending: λαβών, ἐλθών.

Irregular verbs build the participle on **their own aorist stem** — never on the present stem; so the aorist stem must be memorized first.

### 2.2 Irregular Aorist Stems and Their Participles

| Verb (present) | Aorist indicative | Aorist stem | Participle (masc. nom. sg.) |
|----------------|----------------------------|-------------|-----------------------------------|
| **ἔρχομαι** "to come" | ἦλθον | ἐλθ‑ | **ἐλθών** |
| **λαμβάνω** "to take" | ἔλαβον | λαβ‑ | **λαβών** |
| **ὁράω** "to see" | εἶδον | ἰδ‑ | **ἰδών** |
| **λέγω** "to say" | εἶπον | εἰπ‑ | **εἰπών** |
| **δίδωμι** "to give" | ἔδωκα | δο‑ | **δούς** (δοῦσα, δόν) |
| **τίθημι** "to place" | ἔθηκα | θε‑ | **θείς** (θεῖσα, θέν) |
| **ἵστημι** "to stand" | ἔστην | στα‑ | **στάς** (στᾶσα, στάν) |
| **γιγνώσκω** "to recognize" | ἔγνων | γνο‑ | **γνούς** (γνοῦσα, γνόν) |
| **βαίνω** "to go" | ἔβην | βα‑ | **βάς** (βᾶσα, βάν) |
| **φέρω** "to carry" | ἤνεγκα | ἐνεγκ‑ | **ἐνεγκών / ἐνέγκας** |

> **Note:** γίγνομαι "to become" has a middle aorist; its participle is **γενόμενος** (middle) and belongs to the middle participle lesson. εἰμί "to be" has no aorist at all.

### 2.3 Full Paradigm (Example: **δούς** from *δίδωμι*)

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|
| **Singular** | | | |
| Nominative | **δούς** | **δοῦσα** | **δόν** |
| Genitive | **δόντος** | **δούσης** | **δόντος** |
| Dative | **δόντι** | **δούσῃ** | **δόντι** |
| Accusative | **δόντα** | **δοῦσαν** | **δόν** |
| **Plural** | | | |
| Nominative | **δόντες** | **δοῦσαι** | **δόντα** |
| Genitive | **δόντων** | **δουσῶν** | **δόντων** |
| Dative | **δοῦσι(ν)** | **δούσαις** | **δοῦσι(ν)** |
| Accusative | **δόντας** | **δούσας** | **δόντα** |

The pattern of endings is the same for all aorist active participles; only the **stem** changes.

## 3. Key Rules & Common Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **1. The aorist stem, not the present** | The participle is never built on the present stem. | *ἔρχομαι* → stem **ἐλθ‑**, not *ἔρχ‑* |
| **2. No augment** | The augment belongs to the indicative alone. | ἦλθον but **ἐλθών** |
| **3. Second aorist: accent on the ending** | ‑ών/‑οῦσα/‑όν always carries the accent — the mark that distinguishes it from the present participle. | λαβών ≠ λαμβάνων |
| **4. Root aorists: ‑άς/‑είς/‑ούς** | Vowel stems take the endings directly. | στάς, θείς, δούς, γνούς |
| **5. Memorize the suppletion** | The aorist root may be entirely different. | ὁράω → ἰδών; λέγω → εἰπών |
| **6. Full agreement** | Like any adjective: gender, number, case. | τῇ δούσῃ γυναικί "to the woman who gave" |
| **7. εἰμί has no aorist participle** | Because it has no aorist; its present participle is ὤν. | — |

## 4. Example Sentences

| Greek (with participle) | Transliteration | Translation |
|--------------------------|-------|-------------|
| **ἐλθὼν εἰς τὴν πόλιν, ὁ ἄνθρωπος ἀπῆλθεν.** | elthṑn eis tēn pólin… | Having come into the city, the man went away. |
| **δοὺς τὸ δῶρον, ὁ παῖς χαίρει.** | doùs to dôron… | Having given the gift, the boy is glad. |
| **ἰδόντες τοὺς πολεμίους, ἔφυγον.** | idóntes tous polemíous… | When they saw the enemy, they fled. |
| **δοῦσα τὴν ἐπιστολήν, ἡ γυνὴ ἀπῆλθεν.** | doûsa tēn epistolḗn… | Having given the letter, the woman left. |
| **ταῦτα εἰπὼν ἀπέθανεν.** | taûta eipṑn apéthanen | Having said this, he died. |

*In narrative prose, the aorist active participle usually works like an English "after …ing" or "having …ed" clause.*

---

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Temporal / circumstantial** — an action prior to the main verb: ἐλθὼν εἶπεν "he came and said / when he had come, he said."
2. **Causal** — γνοὺς τὸν κίνδυνον ἔφυγεν "realizing the danger, he fled."
3. **Substantive with the article** — οἱ ἰδόντες "those who saw; the witnesses."
4. **Very high frequency** — these ten or so irregular forms (ἐλθών, ἰδών, εἰπών, λαβών, δούς…) make up a large share of the participles on any page of text; memorizing them is the best possible investment.

---

## 6. Historical Note

The suffix ‑ντ‑ (visible in the oblique cases: δόντος, στάντος) is the ancient Indo-European active participle suffix. The apparently irregular nominatives (δούς from *δοντς, θείς from *θεντς) result from the simplification of consonant clusters. These root participles are among the language's oldest inheritance and survived regularization precisely because of their high frequency.
