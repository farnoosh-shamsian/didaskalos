# Lesson: The Aorist Participle in the Middle Voice (‑ω / ‑αμεν‑)

**Goal:**  
By the end of this lesson you will be able to:

* Explain what an aorist participle is and how the middle voice works.  
* Recognize and form the middle‑voice aorist participle (the “‑ω”‑type forms).  
* Apply the participle in simple sentences and understand its nuances of meaning.  

---

## 1. What Is an Aorist Participle?

| Term | Meaning in Greek Grammar | Rough English Approximation |
|------|--------------------------|------------------------------|
| **Aorist** | A past‑time aspect that presents an action as a simple, completed whole, without reference to its duration. | “did / have done” |
| **Participle** | A verbal adjective that can modify a noun (like an adjective) or stand in a clause (like a subordinate clause). | “having done …”, “the one who …” |
| **Middle Voice** | The subject is both the doer and the (often) beneficiary or is acting for its own interest. Many middle meanings are reflexive, reciprocal, or “for oneself”. | “I wash **myself**”, “He fights **for himself**” |

The **aorist middle participle** therefore expresses a *completed* action performed *by the subject for its own benefit (or with a middle nuance)*, and it behaves like an adjective that agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case.

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns

### 2.1. The Basic Shape

The aorist middle participle is built from the **aorist stem** of a verb plus the middle‑voice participial suffix **‑αμεν‑** (or **‑σάμεν‑** after a stem ending in ‑σ). The resulting masculine nominative singular ends in **‑αμεν‑ω** (often written **‑αμεν‑ος** → **‑αμεν‑ω** in older editions). Modern textbooks usually give the ending as **‑αμεν‑** with the regular adjective endings.

| Voice | Masculine Nom. Sing. | Feminine Nom. Sing. | Neuter Nom. Sing. |
|-------|----------------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Aorist Middle** | ‑αμεν‑ **‑ος** → **‑αμεν‑ω** (e.g., *πράξαμεν‑ω*) | ‑αμεν‑ **‑η** (e.g., *πράξαμεν‑η*) | ‑αμεν‑ **‑ον** (e.g., *πράξαμεν‑ον*) |

> **Note:** In many printed editions the masculine ending appears as **‑αμεν‑ος**; the **‑ω** you see in older sources is simply the contracted form of **‑αμεν‑ος** after the stem vowel.

### 2.2. Full Declension Table (Masculine Example)

Below is the complete paradigm for the aorist middle participle of the verb **πράσσω** “to do, to accomplish”. The aorist stem is **πράξ‑**.

| Case | Singular | Dual | Plural |
|------|----------|------|--------|
| **Nominative** | **πράξαμεν‑ος** (‑ω) | **πράξαμεν‑ώ** | **πράξαμεν‑οί** |
| **Genitive** | **πράξαμεν‑οῦ** | **πράξαμεν‑ώ** | **πράξαμεν‑ῶν** |
| **Dative** | **πράξαμεν‑ῳ** | **πράξαμεν‑ώ** | **πράξαμεν‑οῖς** |
| **Accusative** | **πράξαμεν‑ον** | **πράξαμεν‑ώ** | **πράξαμεν‑ούς** |
| **Vocative** | **πράξαμεν‑ε** | **πράξαμεν‑ώ** | **πράξαμεν‑οί** |

> The dual forms are rarely used in Attic prose but are shown for completeness.

### 2.3. Verbs with a Stem Ending in ‑σ

When the aorist stem ends in **‑σ**, the participial suffix appears as **‑σάμεν‑**.

| Verb | Aorist Stem | Middle Participle (Masc. Nom.) |
|------|-------------|--------------------------------|
| **λύω** “to loosen” | **λυσ‑** | **λυσάμεν‑ω** (often written **λυσάμεν‑ος**) |
| **ἀκούω** “to hear” | **ἀκουσ‑** | **ἀκουσάμεν‑ω** |

The rest of the paradigm follows the same pattern as above, simply replacing **‑αμεν‑** with **‑σάμεν‑**.

### 2.4. Comparison with Other Voices

| Voice | Active Ending | Middle Ending | Passive Ending |
|-------|---------------|---------------|----------------|
| **Aorist Participle** | ‑ων, ‑ουσα, ‑ον | ‑αμεν‑ (‑αμεν‑ος/‑ω) | ‑θῆς, ‑θῆ, ‑θός (or ‑η, ‑ον) |

The middle participle is the only aorist participle that uses **‑αμεν‑/‑σάμεν‑**; the active uses **‑ων/‑ουσα/‑ον**, and the passive uses **‑θῆς/‑θῆ/‑θός** (or the contracted **‑η/‑ον**).

---

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **1. Use the aorist stem** | Take the same stem that appears in the aorist indicative (e.g., *πράξ‑* from *πράξα*). | *πράξ‑* + ‑αμεν‑ → *πράξαμεν‑ος* |
| **2. Add ‑αμεν‑ (or ‑σάμεν‑)** | If the stem ends in ‑σ, insert **‑σάμεν‑**; otherwise **‑αμεν‑**. | *λυσ‑* → *λυσάμεν‑ος* |
| **3. Decline like a regular adjective** | After the suffix, attach the normal first‑/second‑declension adjective endings. |
