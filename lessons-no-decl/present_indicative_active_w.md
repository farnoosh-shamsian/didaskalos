# Lesson: The Present‑Indicative‑Active (‑ω) in Ancient Greek  

*Target audience: beginner‑intermediate students who already know the Greek alphabet and basic noun declension.*  

---

## 1. What the Present‑Indicative‑Active Is  

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Present** | The action is happening **now** or habitually, or it expresses a timeless truth. |
| **Indicative** | The “statement” mood – used for ordinary factual or neutral statements (as opposed to subjunctive, optative, imperative, etc.). |
| **Active** | The subject **does** the action (as opposed to middle or passive). |
| **‑ω** | The **first‑person singular** ending of the present‑indicative‑active for most verbs. All other persons are built from this stem. |

Thus, the present‑indicative‑active is the “default” verb form you will meet most often in prose and poetry: *λέγω* “I say”, *γράφω* “I write”, *παιδεύω* “I educate”, etc.

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns  

Ancient Greek verbs fall into three major **conjugation classes** in the present system:

1. **Thematic (vowel‑stem) verbs** – stem ends in a vowel (‑α, ‑ε, ‑ο, ‑ι, ‑υ).  
2. **Contract verbs** – the vowel of the stem contracts with the personal ending.  
3. **A‑thematic (consonant‑stem) verbs** – stem ends in a consonant (often ‑ν, ‑ρ, ‑σ, ‑τ, ‑κ, ‑γ, ‑δ, ‑β, ‑μ, ‑λ).  

Below are the **regular paradigms** for each class.  Irregular verbs (e.g., *εἰμί*, *ἔρχομαι*, *ἵστημι*) are treated in the “Exceptions” section.

### 2.1 Thematic Verbs (e.g., **φιλέω** “to love”)  

| Person | Ending | Form | Gloss |
|--------|--------|------|-------|
| 1st sg. | ‑ω | **φιλέω** | I love |
| 2nd sg. | ‑εις | **φιλεῖς** | you love |
| 3rd sg. | ‑ει | **φιλεῖ** | he/she/it loves |
| 1st pl. | ‑ομεν | **φιλοῦμεν** | we love |
| 2nd pl. | ‑ετε | **φιλεῖτε** | you (pl.) love |
| 3rd pl. | ‑ουσι(ν) | **φιλοῦσι(ν)** | they love |

*Note:* The thematic vowel **ο** appears before the personal endings; in the 2nd sg. and 3rd sg. the **ει** is a contraction of **ε + ι** (or **ο + ι**).  

### 2.2 Contract Verbs  

Contract verbs have a stem ending in **α, ε, or ο** that merges with the ending. Three sub‑types:

| Sub‑type | Example | Stem | Present‑Indicative‑Active Forms |
|----------|---------|------|---------------------------------|
| **‑α‑** (α‑contract) | **πράττω** “to do, practice” (stem *πρα‑*) | *πρα‑* | 1sg **πράττω**, 2sg **πράττεις**, 3sg **πράττει**, 1pl **πράττομεν**, 2pl **πράττετε**, 3pl **πράττουσι(ν)** |
| **‑ε‑** (ε‑contract) | **δείκνυμι** → **δείκνῡμι** “to show” (stem *δείκνυ‑*) – actually a mixed case, but for pure ε‑contract: **τιμάω** “to honor” (stem *τιμα‑*) | *τιμα‑* | 1sg **τιμῶ**, 2sg **τιμᾷς**, 3sg **τιμᾷ**, 1pl **τιμῶμεν**, 2pl **τιμᾶτε**, 3pl **τιμῶσι(ν)** |
| **‑ο‑** (ο‑contract) | **βούλομαι** (middle) – for active: **βούλομαι** is middle; a pure active example: **προσαγορεύω** “to call” (stem *προσαγορε‑*) – actually a thematic verb. A classic o‑contract active is **γίγνομαι** (middle). For active we use **ἀγοράζω** (no contraction). The true o‑contract active is rare; most o‑contract verbs are middle/passive. |

*The most common contract pattern you will meet is the **α‑contract** (‑α‑ + ‑ω → ‑ω, ‑α + ‑εις → ‑εις, etc.).*  

### 2.3 A‑thematic Verbs (e.g., **ἔρχομαι** is middle; a true active a‑thematic: **πίνω** “to drink”)  

| Person | Ending | Form | Gloss |
|--------|--------|------|-------|
| 1sg | ‑ω | **πίνω** | I drink |
| 2sg | ‑εις | **πίνεις** | you drink |
| 3sg | ‑ει | **πίνει** | he/she/it drinks |
| 1pl | ‑ομεν | **πίνομεν** | we drink |
| 2pl | ‑ετε | **πίνετε** | you (pl.) drink |
| 3pl | ‑ουσι(ν) | **πίνουσι(ν)** | they drink |

The a‑thematic pattern is identical to the thematic pattern **except** that there is **no thematic vowel** before the endings; the stem ends directly in a consonant.

## 3. Key Rules & Common Exceptions  

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **1. The 1st‑person singular always ends in ‑ω** | This is the “base” from which the other forms are built. | *γράφω* “I write” |
| **2. The 2nd‑sg. ending is ‑εις** (or ‑ᾷς/‑ᾷ for contract‑α) | The vowel before ‑εις may be long (‑ᾷ) after contraction. | *φιλεῖς* vs. *τιμᾷς* |
| **3. The 3rd‑sg. ending is ‑ει** (or ‑ᾷ after α‑contract) | Same vowel length rule as above. | *πράττει* vs. *τιμᾷ* |
| **4. The 1st‑pl. ending is ‑ομεν** | The thematic vowel (ο) appears only in thematic verbs; a‑thematic verbs have the same ending because the stem already ends in a consonant. | *γράφουμεν* (thematic) vs. *πίνουμεν* (a‑thematic) |
| **5. The 2nd‑pl. ending is ‑ετε** | No contraction occurs here. | *γράφετε* |
| **6. The 3rd‑pl. ending is ‑ουσι(ν)** (or ‑ουσι for contract‑α) | The final **ν** is optional in prose; it appears more often in poetry. | *γράφουσι(ν)* |
| **7. Contract verbs shorten the vowel before the ending** | α‑contract: ‑α + ‑ω → ‑ω, ‑α + ‑εις → ‑ᾷς, etc.  ε‑contract: ‑ε + ‑ω → ‑ῶ, ‑ε + ‑εις → ‑ῆς, etc. | *τιμῶ* (from *τιμα‑* + ‑ω) |
| **8. Verbs with a **‑σ‑** in the stem often insert **‑σ‑** before the ending in the 2nd sg. and 3rd pl.** | This is the “‑σ‑” augment for present? Actually present does not have augment; the **‑σ‑** appears in the 2nd sg. of many a‑thematic verbs (e.g., *ἔρχομαι* is middle). For active a‑thematic verbs, the **‑σ‑** appears in the 2nd sg. of some verbs like **ἔχω** → **ἔχεις** (but that is thematic). The rule is more relevant for **future**. So we skip. |
| **9. Irregular verbs** | Some of the most common verbs have unique present stems or endings. | *εἰμί* “I am”, *ἔχω* “I have”, *ἔρχομαι* (middle), *ὁράω* “I see” (contract‑ο) |
| **10. The “‑ν” in 3rd‑pl. is often omitted in poetry** | Poets drop the **ν** for metrical reasons. | *γράφουσι* vs. *γράφουσιν* |

### 3.1 Irregular Verbs Worth Knowing  

| Verb | Present‑Indicative‑Active (1sg.) | Paradigm (selected) | Note |
|------|--------------------------------|---------------------|------|
| **εἰμί** “to be” | **εἰμί** | 1sg εἰμί, 2sg εἶ, 3sg ἐστί(ν), 1pl ἐσμέν, 2pl ἐστέ, 3pl εἰσίν | Suppletive stem; irregular forms. |
| **ἔχω** “to have” | **ἔχω** | 1sg ἔχω, 2sg ἔχεις, 3sg ἔχει, 1pl ἔχομεν, 2pl ἔχετε, 3pl ἔχουσι(ν) | The stem ends in **χ**, but the endings are regular. |
| **ὁράω** “to see” (ο‑contract) | **ὁρῶ** | 1sg ὁρῶ, 2sg ὁρᾷς, 3sg ὁρᾷ, 1pl ὁρῶμεν, 2pl ὁρᾶτε, 3pl ὁρῶσι(ν) | The long **ω** comes from contraction of **ο + ω**. |
| **δίδωμι** “to give” (mixed) | **δίδωμι** | 1sg δίδωμι, 2sg δίδως, 3sg δίδωσι(ν), 1pl διδόμεν, 2pl διδόμετε, 3pl διδόασι(ν) | Shows a mixture of a‑thematic and thematic patterns. |
| **ἔρχομαι** “to come” (middle) – not active, but appears often in texts. | — | — | Use the middle paradigm; not covered here. |

## 4. Example Sentences  

| Greek (present‑indicative‑active) | Transliteration | English |
|-----------------------------------|----------------|---------|
| **Λέγω τὸν νόμον.** | Légō tòn nómón. | I speak the law. |
| **Φιλεῖς τὸν φίλον σου.** | Phileîs tòn phílon sou. | You love your friend. |
| **Πίνουσιν οἶνον ἐν τῇ ἀγορᾷ.** | Pínousin oînón en têi agorâi. | They drink wine in the market. |
| **Τιμῶ τὸν πατέρα μου.** | Timô tòn patéra mou. | I honor my father. |
| **Ἐγὼ ἔχω τὰ βιβλία.** | Egṓ ékhō tà bíblia. | I have the books. |
| **Ὁ ἀνήρ ὁρᾷ τὸν οὐρανὸν.** | Ho anḗr ho̱râi tòn ouranòn. | The man sees the sky. |

*Notice the verb endings:* **-ω**, **-εις**, **-ει**, **-ομεν**, **-ετε**, **-ουσι(ν)**.

## 5. Usage & Context  

1. **Narrative prose** – The present‑indicative‑active is the default tense for describing actions that are happening **as the story is told** (e.g., *Ἀλέξανδρος ἰσχύει* “Alexander is strong”).  
2. **Historical present** – In historiography, the present is often used to make past events feel immediate (*Ἀθῆναι ἐπὶ τῇ μάχῃ νικῶσι* “Athens wins the battle”).  
3. **General truths & scientific statements** – Present expresses timeless facts (*ὁ ἥλιος ἀνατέλλει* “the sun rises”).
