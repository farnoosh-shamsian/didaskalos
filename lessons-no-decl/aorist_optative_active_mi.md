# Lesson: The Aorist Optative Active of μι-Verbs

*Target level: Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Aorist indicative active of μι-verbs (ἔδωκα, ἔθηκα, ἔστην), optative mood basics*

---

## 1. What This Form Is

The great μι-verbs — **δίδωμι** "give", **τίθημι** "put", **ἵστημι** "set/stand", **ἵημι** "send, let go" — form their aorist optative active from the **bare root**, with the optative suffix **-ιη-/-ι-** added directly. The result is a set of short, striking forms that beginners often fail to recognize:

> **δοίη** "may he give" · **θείη** "may he put" · **σταίη** "may he stand" · **(ἀφ)είη** "may he let go"

These forms carry wishes, prayers, and polite possibilities: δοῖεν οἱ θεοί "may the gods grant ...".

## 2. Morphology

Root + **-ιη-** (singular) / **-ι-** (plural) + secondary endings — the same rhythm as the present optative of these verbs, but on the short aorist root:

### 2.1 δίδωμι (aorist root δο-)

| Person | Form | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| 1 sg | **δοίην** | may I give |
| 2 sg | **δοίης** | may you give |
| 3 sg | **δοίη** | may he give |
| 1 pl | **δοῖμεν** | may we give |
| 2 pl | **δοῖτε** | may you (pl.) give |
| 3 pl | **δοῖεν** | may they give |

### 2.2 τίθημι (root θε-), ἵστημι (root στα-), ἵημι (root ἑ-)

| Person | τίθημι | ἵστημι | ἵημι (compound ἀφίημι) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 sg | θείην | σταίην | (ἀφ)είην |
| 2 sg | θείης | σταίης | (ἀφ)είης |
| 3 sg | **θείη** | **σταίη** | **(ἀφ)είη** |
| 1 pl | θεῖμεν | σταῖμεν | (ἀφ)εῖμεν |
| 2 pl | θεῖτε | σταῖτε | (ἀφ)εῖτε |
| 3 pl | **θεῖεν** | σταῖεν | (ἀφ)εῖεν |

> **Note on ἵστημι:** these optatives come from the **intransitive root aorist ἔστην** "I stood". The transitive first aorist ἔστησα "I set up" makes στήσαιμι, στήσειε like an ω-verb.

## 3. Key Rules & Common Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Short root, no reduplication** | The present reduplication (δι-, τι-, ἱ-) disappears: διδοίη (present) vs. δοίη (aorist). | |
| **2. -ιη- in singular, -ι- in plural** | δοίην / δοῖμεν; the longer plurals (δοίημεν, δοίησαν) occur but are rarer. | |
| **3. Compounds are everywhere** | ἀφίημι → ἀφείη "may he release"; μεθίημι → μεθείη "may he let go"; παρίστημι → παρασταίη. The prefix does not change the optative. | |
| **4. φημί behaves differently** | Its aorist is the sigmatic ἔφησα, so the optative is φήσειε(ν) — like an ω-verb — beside the present optative φαίη. | |
| **5. ὄλλυμι and δείκνυμι also go sigmatic** | -νυμι verbs form the aorist active optative from their sigmatic aorist: ὀλέσειε, δείξειε. Only the root aorists use -ιη-. | |

## 4. Example Sentences

- δοῖεν οἱ θεοὶ ὑμῖν πάντα τὰ ἀγαθά. — "May the gods give you every blessing."
- εἰ δοίη μοι ταύτην τὴν χάριν, εὐδαίμων ἂν εἴην. — "If he should grant me this favor, I would be happy."
- ηὔξαντο τὸν θεὸν τῇ πόλει εἰρήνην θεῖναι· θείη δὲ ταχέως. — "They prayed the god to establish peace for the city — and may he establish it soon."
- οὐκ ἂν σταίη τὸ στράτευμα ἄνευ ἡγεμόνος. — "The army would not stand its ground without a leader."
- ἐδεήθησαν αὐτοῦ ὅπως ἀφείη τοὺς αἰχμαλώτους. — "They begged him to release the prisoners." (optative in past sequence)

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Prayer formulas:** δοίη / δοῖεν with the gods as subject is the classic blessing shape — common in oratory, comedy, and inscriptions.
2. **Potential optative with ἄν** for polite assertions: θείη τις ἄν "one might suppose/lay it down".
3. **Past-sequence subordination:** purpose clauses and indirect petitions (ὅπως ἀφείη) take the optative after past main verbs.
4. Recognition tip: a two-syllable optative beginning δο-, θε-, στα-, or -ει- (from ἵημι) with -ίη-/-ῖ- is almost always one of these aorists. Read the compound prefix off first: μετα-δοίη, ἐπι-θείη, κατα-σταίη, ἀφ-είη.
