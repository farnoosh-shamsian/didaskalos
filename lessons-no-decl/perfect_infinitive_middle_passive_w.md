# Lesson: The Perfect Middle/Passive Infinitive of ω-Verbs

*Target level: Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Perfect indicative middle/passive, present middle infinitive.*

---

## 1. What This Form Is

The **perfect middle/passive infinitive** expresses a **completed action whose result still stands**, in the middle or passive voice:

> **λελύσθαι** "to have been loosed / to be in a loosed state"
> **γεγράφθαι** "to have been written / to stand written"

It is not primarily about past time. It describes a **condition**, and in indirect discourse it is the natural way to report that something *is now the case as a result of what was done*.

| Infinitive | Force |
|---|---|
| λύεσθαι (present) | to be being loosed (process) |
| λυθῆναι (aorist passive) | to be loosed (single event) |
| **λελύσθαι** (perfect) | **to stand loosed (resulting state)** |

Thucydides reaches for it constantly in reported claims about treaties, agreements, and fortifications — things that *have been done* and therefore now hold.

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns

### 2.1 Formation

**Reduplication + perfect middle stem + ‑σθαι.** The ending attaches directly to the stem, with no thematic vowel:

| Verb | Perfect indicative m/p | Perfect m/p infinitive |
|---|---|---|
| λύω "loose" | λέλυμαι | **λελύσθαι** |
| παύω "stop" | πέπαυμαι | **πεπαῦσθαι** |
| ποιέω "make" | πεποίημαι | **πεποιῆσθαι** |
| τιμάω "honour" | τετίμημαι | **τετιμῆσθαι** |

*Note the accent: it falls on the syllable before ‑σθαι and is a circumflex when that syllable is long — πεπαῦσθαι, πεποιῆσθαι.*

### 2.2 Consonant Stems

When the stem ends in a consonant it collides with the σ of the ending, and the two adjust — the same sound changes as in the perfect indicative:

| Verb | Perfect indicative | Infinitive | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| γράφω "write" | γέγραμμαι | **γεγράφθαι** | φ + σθαι → φθαι |
| τάττω "arrange" | τέταγμαι | **τετάχθαι** | γ + σθαι → χθαι |
| πείθω "persuade" | πέπεισμαι | **πεπεῖσθαι** | θ + σθαι → σθαι |
| ἀγγέλλω "report" | ἤγγελμαι | **ἠγγέλθαι** | λ‑stem |
| τειχίζω "fortify" | τετείχισμαι | **τετειχίσθαι** | σ‑stem |

### 2.3 The Whole Infinitive System of λύω

| Tense/Voice | Infinitive |
|---|---|
| Present middle/passive | λύεσθαι |
| Future middle | λύσεσθαι |
| Aorist middle | λύσασθαι |
| Aorist passive | λυθῆναι |
| **Perfect middle/passive** | **λελύσθαι** |

The reduplication is what marks the perfect off from all the others.

---

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Reduplication is obligatory** | It is the mark of the perfect system and never drops in the infinitive. | λε‑λύσθαι |
| **2. No augment** | The augment belongs to the indicative past tenses only; the infinitive never takes it. | |
| **3. Accent before ‑σθαι** | λελύσθαι, πεπαῦσθαι — not recessive as in finite forms. | |
| **4. Consonant adjustments** | Labials → φθαι, velars → χθαι, dentals → σθαι. | γεγράφθαι, τετάχθαι, πεπεῖσθαι |
| **5. Middle or passive?** | One form serves both; context decides. πεπαῦσθαι = "to have ceased" (middle) or "to have been stopped" (passive). | |
| **6. State, not event** | Translate with "to stand …" or "to have been … (and still be)". | |
| **7. Common in indirect discourse** | Especially after verbs of saying and knowing, reporting a settled state of affairs. | |

---

## 4. Example Sentences

| Greek | Translation |
|---|---|
| ἔφασαν τὰς σπονδὰς **λελύσθαι**. | "They said that the treaty had been broken." |
| ταῦτα ἐν ταῖς συνθήκαις **γεγράφθαι** ἔλεγον. | "They said that this stood written in the agreements." |
| ὁ στρατηγὸς ἠξίου τὸ στράτευμα **τετάχθαι**. | "The general demanded that the army be drawn up." |
| δοκεῖ ἡ πόλις καλῶς **τετειχίσθαι**. | "The city seems to have been well fortified." |
| οὐκ ἔφη ὑπ' οὐδενὸς **πεπεῖσθαι**. | "He said he had been persuaded by no one." |

---

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Indirect discourse** — after φημί, λέγω, οἶδα, δοκεῖ. This is by far the commonest use: reporting that something *has been done and so now holds*.
2. **Complement of verbs of demanding and seeming** — ἀξιόω, δοκεῖ + perfect infinitive.
3. **With impersonals** — δεῖ, χρή, when the requirement is that a state be in place: δεῖ τὰ τείχη τετειχίσθαι.
4. **Articular infinitive** — τὸ γεγράφθαι "the fact of its standing written."
5. **Choosing the tense** — use the perfect when the *result* matters, the aorist when the *event* matters: λυθῆναι "to be released" vs. λελύσθαι "to be free, released."

---

## 6. Historical Development & Etymology

- The infinitive ending **‑σθαι** is invariant for the middle across all tenses of the Greek verb; the tense is signalled entirely by the stem — here, by reduplication.
- The **reduplication** of the perfect is an Indo-European inheritance, originally marking a stative formation, which is why the Greek perfect means a *state* rather than a past event.
- The **consonant assimilations** (γεγράφθαι, τετάχθαι) follow the ordinary Greek rules for stops before σ/θ and are worth learning once as a set, since they recur throughout the perfect middle system.
- As the perfect merged with the aorist during the Koine period, this infinitive lost its distinct force and disappeared along with the infinitive itself in later Greek.
