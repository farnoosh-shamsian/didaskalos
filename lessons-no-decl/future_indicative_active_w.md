# Future Indicative Active (ω-Verbs)
*Ancient Greek — Beginner–Intermediate Lesson*

---

## 1. What the Future Indicative Active Is

The **future indicative active** expresses an action that **will happen** (or is intended to happen).
It is built on the **future stem + the same personal endings as the present indicative active** (‑ω, ‑εις, ‑ει, ‑ομεν, ‑ετε, ‑ουσι(ν)).

The characteristic element of the Greek future is the suffix **‑σ‑** (the "future marker").

> **Future = present stem + ‑σ‑ + present endings**

The first person singular always ends in **‑σω**, which is why the whole tense is often called the "‑σω form."

---

## 2. Forming the Future Stem

| Verb type | How the future stem is built | Example (present stem) | Future stem |
|-----------|-----------------------------|------------------------|-------------|
| **Thematic ω-verbs** (e.g., *λύω*) | Add **‑σ‑** to the present stem | λυ‑ | **λυσ‑** |
| **Contract verbs** in ‑άω, ‑έω, ‑όω | Lengthen the vowel (α/ε → η, ο → ω) and add **‑σ‑** | τιμα‑ | **τιμησ‑** |
| **μι-Verbs** (e.g., *φημί*) | Add **‑σ‑** to the root | φη‑ | **φησ‑** |
| **Irregular verbs** | Special forms (see § 3.4) | — | — |

*Note: consonant-final stems merge with σ: labial + σ → ψ (γράφω → γράψω); velar + σ → ξ (πράττω → πράξω); dentals drop (πείθω → πείσω).*

## 3. Full Paradigm Tables

### 3.1 Regular Thematic Verb (λύω "to loose")

| Person | Form | Transliteration | Gloss |
|--------|------|----------------|-------|
| 1 sg. | λύσω | **lýsō** | I will loose |
| 2 sg. | λύσεις | **lýseis** | you (sg.) will loose |
| 3 sg. | λύσει | **lýsei** | he/she/it will loose |
| 1 pl. | λύσομεν | **lýsomen** | we will loose |
| 2 pl. | λύσετε | **lýsete** | you (pl.) will loose |
| 3 pl. | λύσουσι(ν) | **lýsousi(n)** | they will loose |

*Construction: λυ‑ + **σ** + the present endings — the future conjugates exactly like the present, only with σ.*

### 3.2 Contract Verb (τιμάω "to honor")

| Person | Form | Transliteration | Gloss |
|--------|------|----------------|-------|
| 1 sg. | τιμήσω | **timḗsō** | I will honor |
| 2 sg. | τιμήσεις | **timḗseis** | you will honor |
| 3 sg. | τιμήσει | **timḗsei** | he/she/it will honor |
| 1 pl. | τιμήσομεν | **timḗsomen** | we will honor |
| 2 pl. | τιμήσετε | **timḗsete** | you (pl.) will honor |
| 3 pl. | τιμήσουσι(ν) | **timḗsousi(n)** | they will honor |

*Notice the lengthened **η** before the **‑σ‑** — there is no contraction in the future.*

### 3.3 μι-Verb (φημί "to say")

| Person | Form | Gloss |
|--------|------|-------|
| 1 sg. | φήσω | I will say |
| 2 sg. | φήσεις | you will say |
| 3 sg. | φήσει | he/she/it will say |
| 1 pl. | φήσομεν | we will say |
| 2 pl. | φήσετε | you (pl.) will say |
| 3 pl. | φήσουσι(ν) | they will say |

The **‑σ‑** attaches directly to the root *φη‑*; in the future, μι-verbs behave like ω-verbs.

### 3.4 Irregular Futures (selected)

| Verb | Present | Future | Gloss |
|------|---------|--------|-------|
| εἰμί "to be" | εἰμί | **ἔσομαι** | I will be |
| ἔρχομαι "to come" | ἔρχομαι | **ἐλεύσομαι** (Attic: εἶμι) | I will come |
| ὁράω "to see" | ὁράω | **ὄψομαι** | I will see |
| δίδωμι "to give" | δίδωμι | **δώσω** | I will give |
| γίγνομαι "to become" | γίγνομαι | **γενήσομαι** | I will become |
| ἀκούω "to hear" | ἀκούω | **ἀκούσομαι** | I will hear |

*Many of these futures are **middle in form** (ἔσομαι, ὄψομαι, ἀκούσομαι) with active meaning — a common phenomenon in verbs of perception and motion. They must be memorized.*

## 4. Key Rules & Common Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **‑σ‑ before the endings** | The future marker sits between stem and ending. | λυ-**σ**-ω → λύσω |
| **No augment** | The future takes no augment — the augment belongs to past indicatives. | λύσω, not *ἐλύσω |
| **Contract verbs lengthen the vowel** | α/ε → η, ο → ω before σ. | ποιέω → ποιήσω; δηλόω → δηλώσω |
| **Liquid verbs have no ‑σ‑** | Stems in λ, ρ, μ, ν form a "contracted" future in ‑ῶ. | ἀγγέλλω → ἀγγελῶ; μένω → μενῶ |
| **The look-alike trap** | λύσω = future **or** aorist subjunctive; context decides. | ἐὰν λύσω (subjunctive) / λύσω αὔριον (future) |
| **Accent** | Recessive, as in other finite verb forms. | λύσομεν |
| **Future middle/passive** | Middle: ‑σομαι; passive: ‑θήσομαι — in separate lessons. | λύσομαι; λυθήσομαι |

## 6. Usage & Context

1. **Simple future** — a straightforward future action: *γράψω τὴν ἐπιστολήν* "I will write the letter."
2. **Intention and promise** — the future often conveys will and promise: *δώσω* "I will give (I promise)."
3. **In the future more vivid condition** — as the apodosis of ἐάν + subjunctive: *ἐὰν ἔλθῃς, δώσω σοι τὸ βιβλίον* "if you come, I will give you the book."
4. **Relative clause of purpose** — the future indicative after a relative pronoun expresses purpose: *πέμπει ἄνδρα ὃς ἀγγελεῖ* "he sends a man who will carry the news."
5. **Prophecy** — in oracular and epic texts it lends a tone of inevitability.
6. **Do not confuse with the future perfect** — λελύσομαι "I shall have been freed" is a separate tense.

---

## 7. Historical Development & Etymology

* **Indo-European origin** — the future marker ‑σ‑ is connected with the desiderative suffix *‑s‑ of Proto-Indo-European (compare Sanskrit *‑sya‑*).
* **Phonological development** — in liquid verbs the σ dropped between vowels and contraction produced the ‑ῶ future (ἀγγελῶ) — the same pattern as the contract presents.
* **Kinship with the subjunctive** — the ancient affinity of future and subjunctive (both "not yet realized") explains the formal identity of λύσω.
* **Later fate** — in the Koine the sigmatic future declined, and Modern Greek forms its future with the particle θα (θα λύσω) — a remnant of θέλω ἵνα "I want that."
