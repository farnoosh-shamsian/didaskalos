# Lesson: The Aorist Active Infinitive of ‑μι Verbs

*Target level: Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Aorist indicative active of ‑μι verbs (ἔδωκα, ἔθηκα, ἔστην), present active infinitive of ‑μι verbs.*

---

## 1. What This Form Is

The **aorist active infinitive** of a ‑μι verb expresses a single, complete action in the active voice, without person or number and — outside indirect discourse — without reference to past time.

These infinitives are short, sharp, and extremely common:

> **δοῦναι** "to give" · **θεῖναι** "to place" · **στῆναι** "to stand" · **ἀφεῖναι** "to release"

The central thing to master is the **contrast with the present infinitive**, which is built on the reduplicated stem:

| Present (ongoing) | Aorist (single act) |
|---|---|
| διδόναι "to be giving" | **δοῦναι** "to give (once)" |
| τιθέναι "to be placing" | **θεῖναι** "to place (once)" |
| ἱστάναι "to be setting up" | **στῆναι** "to stand" |
| ἱέναι "to be sending" | **εἷναι** "to send" |

The difference is **aspect**, not time.

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns

### 2.1 The Root Aorist Infinitives

Take the bare aorist root and add **‑ναι**, with a circumflex on the root vowel:

| Verb | Aorist indicative | Aorist active infinitive | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| **δίδωμι** "give" | ἔδωκα | **δοῦναι** | to give |
| **τίθημι** "place" | ἔθηκα | **θεῖναι** | to place |
| **ἵστημι** "stand" | ἔστην | **στῆναι** | to stand |
| **ἵημι** "send" | ἧκα | **εἷναι** | to send |
| **γιγνώσκω** "know" | ἔγνων | **γνῶναι** | to recognise |
| **βαίνω** "go" | ἔβην | **βῆναι** | to go |

*γιγνώσκω and βαίνω are ω-verbs in the present, but their aorists are athematic root aorists, so they form their infinitive exactly like the ‑μι verbs.*

### 2.2 Compounds — Where You Will Actually Meet Them

Compounds are far commoner than the simple verbs, especially in prose:

| Compound | Aorist infinitive | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| ἀποδίδωμι | **ἀποδοῦναι** | to give back, pay |
| παραδίδωμι | **παραδοῦναι** | to hand over, surrender |
| ἐπιτίθημι | **ἐπιθεῖναι** | to impose, set upon |
| ἀνατίθημι | **ἀναθεῖναι** | to dedicate |
| ἀφίστημι | **ἀποστῆναι** | to revolt, stand away |
| ἀφίημι | **ἀφεῖναι** | to let go, release |

> **Reading tip:** strip the prefix first, then recognise the root. παρα‑δοῦναι, ἀπο‑στῆναι, ἀφ‑εῖναι. ἀποστῆναι "to revolt" is one of the defining words of Thucydides' narrative.

### 2.3 ἵστημι Has Two Aorists

This is the one genuine trap in the lesson:

| Aorist | Type | Infinitive | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| ἔστην | root (athematic), **intransitive** | **στῆναι** | "to stand, to come to a halt" |
| ἔστησα | sigmatic (first), **transitive** | **στῆσαι** | "to set up, to station (something)" |

στῆναι and στῆσαι differ by one letter and by their entire syntax: στῆναι takes no object, στῆσαι does.

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **1. No reduplication** | Present διδόναι keeps δι‑; aorist δοῦναι drops it. This is the single best recognition cue. | διδόναι vs. δοῦναι |
| **2. No augment** | The augment (ἔ‑δωκα) belongs to the indicative only. | ἔδωκα → δοῦναι |
| **3. Ending ‑ναι with circumflex** | δοῦναι, θεῖναι, στῆναι, γνῶναι — the accent sits on the root vowel and is a circumflex. | |
| **4. Accent stays put in compounds** | ἀποδοῦναι, παραδοῦναι — the prefix does not draw the accent back. | not *ἀπόδουναι* |
| **5. δοῦναι vs. δοῦσι** | Do not confuse the infinitive δοῦναι with the participle forms (δούς, δόντος). | |
| **6. Aspect, not time** | βούλεται δοῦναι "he wants to give (now)" — no past sense. | |
| **7. In indirect discourse it is past** | After verbs of saying, the aorist infinitive stands for an aorist indicative: ἔφη δοῦναι "he said he had given." | |

---

## 4. Example Sentences

| Greek | Translation |
|---|---|
| ἐβούλοντο τὴν πόλιν **παραδοῦναι**. | "They wished to surrender the city." |
| ἔδοξεν αὐτοῖς **ἀποστῆναι** ἀπὸ Ἀθηναίων. | "They resolved to revolt from the Athenians." |
| δεῖ τοὺς νόμους **θεῖναι** δικαίους. | "One must lay down just laws." |
| χαλεπὸν ἦν **γνῶναι** τὴν αἰτίαν. | "It was hard to discern the cause." |
| ἠξίουν αὐτοὺς τοὺς αἰχμαλώτους **ἀφεῖναι**. | "They demanded that they release the prisoners." |

---

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Complement of verbs of wishing and resolving** — βούλομαι, ἐθέλω, δοκεῖ, ψηφίζομαι: the standard environment in historical prose.
2. **With impersonals** — δεῖ, χρή, ἀνάγκη ἐστί: δεῖ δοῦναι δίκην "he must pay the penalty."
3. **Indirect discourse** — after φημί, λέγω, νομίζω the aorist infinitive represents a past event.
4. **After ὥστε** — result: ὥστε τὴν πόλιν ἀποστῆναι "with the result that the city revolted."
5. **Articular infinitive** — τὸ δοῦναι "the giving," τὸ ἀποστῆναι "the act of revolting"; Thucydides uses these abstractions constantly in his causal analysis.

---

## 6. Historical Development & Etymology

- The ending **‑ναι** is the athematic infinitive marker, the same one seen in the present διδόναι and in εἶναι. The aorist and present differ only by stem, not by ending.
- The **root aorist** (ἔδων/ἔστην type) is the oldest aorist formation in Indo-European, preserving a bare root with secondary endings. Sanskrit *ádāt* "he gave" is the exact counterpart of Greek ἔδω‑.
- **δοῦναι** derives from *δο‑εναι with contraction; the circumflex is the trace of that lost vowel.
- As the ‑μι class eroded in later Greek, these infinitives were among the last athematic forms to survive, precisely because the compounds (παραδοῦναι, ἀποστῆναι) were so heavily lexicalised in legal, military, and religious language.
