# Lesson: The Present Subjunctive Active of ‑μι Verbs

*Target level: Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Present indicative of ‑μι verbs, present subjunctive active of ω-verbs.*

---

## 1. What This Form Is

The **present subjunctive active** of a ‑μι verb expresses a possible, intended, or indefinite action with imperfective aspect: "that he may (keep) giving."

The good news is that ‑μι verbs are **not irregular here**. In the subjunctive they take the same long thematic vowel as every other verb; the endings contract with the stem vowel, and the result looks like a contract verb.

The essential thing to fix in your mind is the **contrast between present and aorist**, which is carried entirely by the **reduplication**:

| Present subjunctive | Aorist subjunctive |
|---|---|
| **διδῶ** "that I may be giving" | **δῶ** "that I may give" |
| **τιθῶ** | **θῶ** |
| **ἱστῶ** | **στῶ** |

Lose the reduplication and you have changed the aspect. This pair is one of the commonest points of confusion in reading Greek prose.

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns

### 2.1 δίδωμι "to give" (stem διδο‑)

The short stem vowel ο contracts with the subjunctive's long vowel:

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | **διδῶ** | **διδῶμεν** |
| 2nd | **διδῷς** | **διδῶτε** |
| 3rd | **διδῷ** | **διδῶσι(ν)** |

### 2.2 τίθημι "to place" (stem τιθε‑) and ἵστημι "to set up" (stem ἱστα‑)

| Person | τίθημι | ἵστημι |
|---|---|---|
| 1 sg | **τιθῶ** | **ἱστῶ** |
| 2 sg | **τιθῇς** | **ἱστῇς** |
| 3 sg | **τιθῇ** | **ἱστῇ** |
| 1 pl | **τιθῶμεν** | **ἱστῶμεν** |
| 2 pl | **τιθῆτε** | **ἱστῆτε** |
| 3 pl | **τιθῶσι(ν)** | **ἱστῶσι(ν)** |

*Note the pattern: δίδωμι (ο‑stem) gives ῷ/ῶ, while τίθημι and ἵστημι (ε‑ and α‑stems) give ῇ/ῶ — exactly as ο‑contract and ε/α‑contract verbs behave.*

### 2.3 εἰμί "to be"

εἰμί's present subjunctive is the bare set of long endings, with no stem at all:

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | **ὦ** | **ὦμεν** |
| 2nd | **ᾖς** | **ἦτε** |
| 3rd | **ᾖ** | **ὦσι(ν)** |

These tiny forms are extremely common: ἐὰν σοφὸς ᾖς "if you are wise"; ἵνα ἐλεύθεροι ὦμεν "so that we may be free."

---

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Reduplication distinguishes present from aorist** | διδῶ (present) vs. δῶ (aorist). The single most important point of this lesson. | ἵνα διδῷ "so that he may keep giving" vs. ἵνα δῷ "so that he may give" |
| **2. The short stem vowel is used** | διδο‑, τιθε‑, ἱστα‑ — not the long singular stem διδω‑, τιθη‑. | |
| **3. Contraction, then circumflex** | The contracted syllable carries a circumflex: διδῶ, τιθῶμεν. | |
| **4. ο‑stems give ῷ; ε/α‑stems give ῇ** | διδῷς but τιθῇς, ἱστῇς. | |
| **5. No augment** | The subjunctive never takes the augment, in any tense. | |
| **6. 3 pl ‑ῶσι(ν)** | Identical in shape to the 3 pl indicative of contract verbs; context decides. | διδῶσι |
| **7. εἰμί is the highest-frequency member** | Learn ὦ, ᾖς, ᾖ, ὦμεν, ἦτε, ὦσι cold. | |

---

## 4. Example Sentences

| Greek | Translation |
|---|---|
| ἐὰν οἱ θεοὶ νίκην **διδῶσιν**, εὐτυχήσομεν. | "If the gods grant victory, we shall prosper." |
| ἵνα τοὺς νόμους δικαίως **τιθῶμεν** … | "So that we may lay down laws justly …" |
| ὅταν τὸ στράτευμα ἐπὶ τῷ ποταμῷ **ἱστῆται** … | "Whenever the army takes up position at the river …" |
| ἐὰν σοφὸς **ᾖς**, οὐκ ἀδικήσεις. | "If you are wise, you will do no wrong." |
| μὴ φοβώμεθα, ἵνα ἐλεύθεροι **ὦμεν**. | "Let us not be afraid, so that we may be free." |

---

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Purpose clauses** — ἵνα, ὅπως + subjunctive after a primary tense.
2. **Future more vivid conditions** — ἐάν + subjunctive: ἐὰν διδῷ …
3. **Indefinite clauses** — ὅταν, ὃς ἄν, ἐπειδὰν + subjunctive.
4. **Hortatory and deliberative** — first person: διδῶμεν "let us give"; τί τιθῶμεν; "what are we to establish?"
5. **Aspect matters more than usual here** — because the reduplicated and unreduplicated stems sit side by side, Greek authors use διδῶ / δῶ deliberately to mark ongoing versus single action. Read the stem carefully.

---

## 6. Historical Development & Etymology

- In the subjunctive the athematic verbs adopt the **thematic long vowel**, so the ‑μι class is already partly assimilated to the ω‑verbs here — an early stage of the drift that eventually dissolved the class entirely.
- The **ι‑reduplication** of the present system (δί‑δωμι, τί‑θημι, ἵ‑στημι) is inherited from Indo-European, where it marked the imperfective stem; its survival in the subjunctive is what keeps the aspectual contrast visible.
- **ὦ, ᾖς, ᾖ** continue *h₁es‑ with the subjunctive vowel and the loss of intervocalic σ — the same root as εἰμί, Latin *sim/sit*, Sanskrit *ásāni*.
- The ἵνα + subjunctive construction, so common with these verbs, is the direct ancestor of the Modern Greek να-clause.
