# Lesson: The Aorist Indicative Active (Irregular Forms)

*Target level: Beginner–Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Greek alphabet, basic noun declension, present-tense verb forms.*

---

## 1. What the Aorist Indicative Active Is

| Term | Meaning in Greek grammar | Rough English equivalent |
|------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Aorist** | An aspect that presents a past action as a simple, complete whole, without reference to duration or repetition. | "did," "went," "saw" (simple past) |
| **Indicative** | The mood of statements of fact. | — |
| **Active** | The subject performs the action (as opposed to middle or passive). | — |

The **aorist indicative active** therefore tells us *who* did *what* in the past — as a single, finished event.

> **Contrast** — the imperfect says "was doing"; the aorist says "did."
> **Example:**
> - *ἔλυον* = "I was loosening" (imperfect)
> - *ἔλυσα* = "I loosened" (aorist)

---

## 2. Regular vs. Irregular Aorists

### 2.1 The Regular (‑σα) Aorist

Most verbs form the aorist active with the suffix **‑σα**:

| Person | Ending | Example with **λύω** "to loose" |
|--------|--------|-----------------------------------|
| 1 sg | ‑α | ἔλυσα |
| 2 sg | ‑ας | ἔλυσας |
| 3 sg | ‑ε(ν) | ἔλυσε(ν) |
| 1 pl | ‑αμεν | ἐλύσαμεν |
| 2 pl | ‑ατε | ἐλύσατε |
| 3 pl | ‑αν | ἔλυσαν |

The **augment** ἐ‑ is prefixed to the stem in the indicative (it is sometimes omitted in poetry).

### 2.2 Irregular Aorist Stems

A relatively small group of **very common** verbs does not take the ‑σα suffix. Their aorist stems arise by:

* a change in the root vowel or consonant (ablaut): λαμβάνω → λαβ‑
* a different suffix (such as ‑κα): δίδωμι → ἔδωκα
* sometimes an entirely different root (suppletion): ὁράω → εἶδον, φέρω → ἤνεγκα

These verbs must be memorized individually. There are two main sets of endings:

| Person | First aorist (sigmatic) | Second aorist (thematic) |
|--------|------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| 1 sg | **‑α** (ἔλυ**σα**) | **‑ον** (ἔλαβ**ον**) |
| 2 sg | **‑ας** (ἔλυ**σας**) | **‑ες** (ἔλαβ**ες**) |
| 3 sg | **‑ε(ν)** (ἔλυ**σε(ν)**) | **‑ε(ν)** (ἔλαβ**ε(ν)**) |
| 1 pl | **‑αμεν** (ἐλύσ**αμεν**) | **‑ομεν** (ἐλάβ**ομεν**) |
| 2 pl | **‑ατε** (ἐλύσ**ατε**) | **‑ετε** (ἐλάβ**ετε**) |
| 3 pl | **‑αν** (ἔλυσ**αν**) | **‑ον** (ἔλαβ**ον**) |

> **Note:** the second-aorist endings are the same as those of the imperfect; what distinguishes the aorist from the imperfect is the **stem**: ἐλάμβανον (imperfect) vs. ἔλαβον (aorist).

## 3. Full Paradigms of the Most Common Irregular Aorists

### 3.1 λαμβάνω → **ἔλαβον** "to take"

| | Singular | Plural |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **1st** | ἔλαβον | ἐλάβομεν |
| **2nd** | ἔλαβες | ἐλάβετε |
| **3rd** | ἔλαβε(ν) | ἔλαβον |

### 3.2 δίδωμι → **ἔδωκα** "to give"

| | Singular | Plural |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **1st** | ἔδωκα | ἔδομεν |
| **2nd** | ἔδωκας | ἔδοτε |
| **3rd** | ἔδωκε(ν) | ἔδοσαν |

*The aorist stem is **δωκ‑** (with the suffix ‑κα) in the singular and **δο‑** in the plural — the "kappa aorist" peculiar to δίδωμι, τίθημι, and ἵημι.*

### 3.3 ἐσθίω → **ἔφαγον** "to eat"

| | Singular | Plural |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **1st** | ἔφαγον | ἐφάγομεν |
| **2nd** | ἔφαγες | ἐφάγετε |
| **3rd** | ἔφαγε(ν) | ἔφαγον |

*The stem **φαγ‑** comes from an entirely different root than ἐσθι‑ (suppletion).*

### 3.4 ἵστημι → **ἔστην** "to stand"

| | Singular | Plural |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **1st** | ἔστην | ἔστημεν |
| **2nd** | ἔστης | ἔστητε |
| **3rd** | ἔστη | ἔστησαν |

*This "root aorist" is intransitive: "I stood." ἵστημι also has a sigmatic first aorist — ἔστησα "I set up" — which is transitive.*

### 3.5 ὁράω → **εἶδον** "to see"

| | Singular | Plural |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **1st** | εἶδον | εἴδομεν |
| **2nd** | εἶδες | εἴδετε |
| **3rd** | εἶδε(ν) | εἶδον |

*From the root ἰδ‑ (cognate with Latin* video*); the present is from the root ὁρα‑.*

### 3.6 φέρω → **ἤνεγκα / ἤνεγκον** "to carry, bring"

| | Singular | Plural |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **1st** | ἤνεγκα / ἤνεγκον | ἠνέγκαμεν |
| **2nd** | ἤνεγκας | ἠνέγκατε |
| **3rd** | ἤνεγκε(ν) | ἤνεγκαν / ἤνεγκον |

*An entirely different root (ἐνεγκ‑); attested with both first- and second-aorist endings.*

### 3.7 ἔχω → **ἔσχον** "to have"

| | Singular | Plural |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **1st** | ἔσχον | ἔσχομεν |
| **2nd** | ἔσχες | ἔσχετε |
| **3rd** | ἔσχε(ν) | ἔσχον |

*The stem σχ‑ (zero grade of the root σεχ‑); its imperfect is εἶχον — do not confuse the two.*

---

## 4. Key Rules

1. **The augment appears in all indicative forms** — with vowel lengthening in vowel-initial verbs: ἄγω → ἤγαγον.
2. **Second aorist = altered stem + imperfect endings.** Recognition depends on the stem, not the endings.
3. **The ‑κα aorists** (ἔδωκα, ἔθηκα, ἧκα) have ‑κα in the singular and the short stem in the plural.
4. **These verbs are the most frequent in the language** — memorizing their principal parts pays the highest dividends.

---

## 5. Example Sentences

| Greek | Translation |
|---------------------------|-------------|
| **ἔλαβον τὰ ὅπλα.** | I/they took the weapons. |
| **ἔδωκε τὴν χώραν τοῖς πολίταις.** | He gave the land to the citizens. |
| **εἶδον τοὺς πολεμίους.** | I/they saw the enemy. |
| **ἔσχε τὴν ἀρχήν.** | He took hold of the rule. |
| **ἤνεγκαν τὰ δῶρα.** | They brought the gifts. |

---

## 6. Usage & Context

Irregular aorists appear in texts **more often than the regular ones**, because they belong to the language's most basic verbs (take, give, see, eat, have, bring). When reading narrative, whenever you meet an augmented form that does not end in ‑σα, think of the list of second and root aorists and reconstruct the dictionary verb from the stem.
