# Lesson: The Future Indicative Middle of ‑μι Verbs

*Target level: Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Present indicative of ‑μι verbs, future indicative middle of ω-verbs.*

---

## 1. What This Form Is

The **future indicative middle** states that something **will happen**, with the subject acting for itself or in its own interest.

For the ‑μι verbs there is a decisive simplification to grasp at once: **in the future they abandon the athematic pattern entirely.** The long/short stem alternation, the ‑μι ending, the ‑ασι(ν) third plural — all of it disappears. The future is built with **‑σ‑ plus the ordinary thematic middle endings**, exactly like an ω-verb.

> δίδωμι → **δώσομαι** · τίθημι → **θήσομαι** · ἵστημι → **στήσομαι**

And one verb dominates this category in real texts: **εἰμί "to be," whose future ἔσομαι is middle in form but simply means "I shall be."**

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns

### 2.1 εἰμί "to be" — **ἔσομαι** (learn this first)

εἰμί has no future active at all; its future is deponent:

| Person | Form | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| 1 sg | **ἔσομαι** | I shall be |
| 2 sg | **ἔσῃ** (also ἔσει) | you will be |
| 3 sg | **ἔσται** | he/she/it will be |
| 1 pl | **ἐσόμεθα** | we shall be |
| 2 pl | **ἔσεσθε** | you (pl.) will be |
| 3 pl | **ἔσονται** | they will be |

> **The one irregularity that matters:** the 3rd singular is **ἔσται**, not *ἔσεται* — the thematic vowel is missing. ἔσται is one of the most frequent verb forms in all of Greek prose; learn it as a fixed word.

### 2.2 The Great ‑μι Verbs

| Verb | Present | Future middle | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| **δίδωμι** "give" | δίδωμι | **δώσομαι** | I shall give (for myself) |
| **τίθημι** "place" | τίθημι | **θήσομαι** | I shall place (for myself) |
| **ἵστημι** "set up" | ἵστημι | **στήσομαι** | I shall set up for myself / I shall stand |
| **ἵημι** "send" | ἵημι | **ἥσομαι** | I shall send (for myself) |
| **δείκνυμι** "show" | δείκνυμι | **δείξομαι** | I shall show (for myself) |

Full paradigm of **δώσομαι**:

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | **δώσομαι** | **δωσόμεθα** |
| 2nd | **δώσῃ** (δώσει) | **δώσεσθε** |
| 3rd | **δώσεται** | **δώσονται** |

*Note the contrast with εἰμί: δώσ‑ε‑ται keeps the thematic vowel, but ἔσ‑ται does not.*

### 2.3 Where the Long Vowel Comes From

The future stem uses the **long-grade root** — the same vowel you see in the singular of the present:

| Root | Present singular | Future |
|---|---|---|
| δο‑/δω‑ | δίδωμι | δώσω / δώσομαι |
| θε‑/θη‑ | τίθημι | θήσω / θήσομαι |
| στα‑/στη‑ | ἵστημι | στήσω / στήσομαι |

The present reduplication (δι‑, τι‑, ἱ‑) is **dropped** in the future: δώσομαι, not *διδώσομαι*.

## 3. Key Rules & Common Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **1. The future is thematic** | No ‑μι verb keeps athematic endings in the future. If you can conjugate λύσομαι, you can conjugate δώσομαι. | δώσεται like λύσεται |
| **2. No reduplication** | Present δί‑δωμι but future δώσομαι. | |
| **3. Long-grade root + σ** | δω‑σ‑, θη‑σ‑, στη‑σ‑. | |
| **4. ἔσται is athematic and irregular** | The single most important exception in this lesson. | ῥᾴδιον ἔσται "it will be easy" |
| **5. 2 sg in ‑ῃ or ‑ει** | From ‑ε‑σαι with loss of σ; both spellings occur in manuscripts. | ἔσῃ / ἔσει |
| **6. Middle form, active sense** | For εἰμί the middle form carries no middle meaning at all — it is simply "will be." | |
| **7. ἵστημι's future is two-faced** | στήσω (active) "I shall set up" is transitive; στήσομαι can be "I shall set up for myself" or intransitive "I shall stand." Context decides. | |

## 4. Example Sentences

| Greek | Translation |
|---|---|
| **ἔσται** μεγάλη ἡ μάχη. | "The battle will be great." |
| οὐκ **ἔσεσθε** ἐλεύθεροι, ἐὰν μὴ μάχησθε. | "You will not be free unless you fight." |
| **δώσομαι** ὑμῖν τὰ ὅπλα. | "I shall hand over the arms." |
| ταῦτα τὰ χρήματα εἰς τὸν πόλεμον **θησόμεθα**. | "We shall devote this money to the war." |
| τὸ στράτευμα ἐπὶ τῷ ποταμῷ **στήσεται**. | "The army will take its stand at the river." |

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Prediction and consequence** — the future indicative is the ordinary tense of forecast; Thucydides' speeches are full of ἔσται in arguments about what will follow.
2. **In conditional apodoses** — ἐὰν + subjunctive … future indicative: ἐὰν ταῦτα ποιῆτε, ἐλεύθεροι ἔσεσθε.
3. **Future for polite command** — a future indicative can stand for an imperative: ταῦτα ποιήσετε "you will do this" (= do it).
4. **ἔσται in impersonal expressions** — δῆλον ἔσται "it will be clear," ῥᾴδιον ἔσται "it will be easy": a workhorse of expository prose.
5. **Aspect note** — Greek has only one future stem; unlike the present/aorist pair, the future does not distinguish ongoing from single action.

---

## 6. Historical Development & Etymology

- The Greek future in **‑σ‑** continues an Indo-European desiderative formation in *‑sye/o‑ ("be about to, want to"), which is why a future so often shades into intention.
- **ἔσομαι** is built on the root *h₁es‑ "to be" — the same root as εἰμί, Latin *sum/erō*, Sanskrit *ásmi*. The 3rd singular **ἔσται** preserves an athematic ending directly on the root, a genuine archaism frozen into the paradigm.
- The migration of the ‑μι verbs into thematic patterns, complete in the future already in Homer, is the same drift that eventually dissolved the whole ‑μι class in later Greek.
- ἔσται survives into Modern Greek in the descendants of εἶμαι, and the future itself was eventually rebuilt with the particle θα (from θέλω ἵνα).
