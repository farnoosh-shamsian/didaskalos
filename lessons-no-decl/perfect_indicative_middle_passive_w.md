# Lesson: The Perfect Indicative Middle/Passive (ω-Verbs)

*Target level: Beginner → Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Greek alphabet, basic verb concepts (present, aorist), the idea of voice.*

---

## 1. What the Perfect Middle/Passive Is

| Term | Meaning | Typical English equivalent |
|------|-------------------|----------------------------|
| **Perfect** | A past action whose **result still holds now**. | "I have written," "it is (now) written." |
| **Indicative** | The ordinary "statement" mood. | — |
| **Middle/Passive** | Middle = for oneself; passive = done to the subject. In the perfect the two are **identical in form**, and the passive sense is very common. | "it has been bound." |

**Key idea:** the perfect middle/passive says that a completed action has left the subject in a **new state**:

- **λέλυμαι** — "I have been set free (and am now free)."
- **γέγραπται** — "it is written (and stands written)" — the familiar formula for citing texts!

---

## 2. Forming the Perfect Middle/Passive

### 2.1 The Perfect Stem

1. **Reduplication** — the first consonant is repeated with ε: λύω → **λελυ‑**.
2. **Vowel-initial verbs** lengthen the vowel: ἀγγέλλω → **ἠγγελ‑**.
3. The κ of the active perfect is **absent** here: λέλυκα (active) but λέλυμαι (middle/passive).

### 2.2 The Endings — Directly on the Stem

The primary middle endings attach to the stem **without a thematic vowel**:

| Person | Ending | Example with **λέλυμαι** |
|--------|------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| 1 sg. | **‑μαι** | **λέλυμαι** "I have been freed" |
| 2 sg. | **‑σαι** | **λέλυσαι** "you have been freed" |
| 3 sg. | **‑ται** | **λέλυται** "he/she/it has been freed" |
| 1 pl. | **‑μεθα** | **λελύμεθα** "we have been freed" |
| 2 pl. | **‑σθε** | **λέλυσθε** "you (pl.) have been freed" |
| 3 pl. | **‑νται** | **λέλυνται** "they have been freed" |

> **Note:** since there is no thematic vowel, ‑σαι survives intact (λέλυσαι) — unlike the present (λύῃ from *λύεσαι).

### 2.3 Consonant-Final Stems: Assimilation

When the stem ends in a consonant, that consonant assimilates to the ending — example: **γράφω → γέγραμμαι**:

| Person | Form | Process |
|--------|------|--------------------------------------------|
| 1 sg. | **γέγραμμαι** | φ + μ → μμ |
| 2 sg. | **γέγραψαι** | φ + σ → ψ |
| 3 sg. | **γέγραπται** | φ + τ → πτ |
| 1 pl. | **γεγράμμεθα** | — |
| 2 pl. | **γέγραφθε** | φ + σθ → φθ |
| 3 pl. | **γεγραμμένοι εἰσί(ν)** | periphrasis: participle + εἰσί |

*The 3rd person plural of consonant stems is formed with the **participle + εἰσί**, because ‑νται after a consonant was unpronounceable.*

## 3. Full Paradigm (λύω "to loose")

| | Singular | Plural |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **1st** | λέλυμαι | λελύμεθα |
| **2nd** | λέλυσαι | λέλυσθε |
| **3rd** | λέλυται | λέλυνται |

*The active perfect for comparison:*

| | Singular | Plural |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **1st** | λέλυκα | λελύκαμεν |
| **2nd** | λέλυκας | λελύκατε |
| **3rd** | λέλυκε(ν) | λελύκασι(ν) |

---

## 4. Key Rules & Common Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|----------------------|
| **A. Reduplication stays** | In every mood and form of the perfect. | Infinitive: λελύσθαι |
| **B. No κ and no thematic vowel** | The ending sits directly on the stem. | λέλυ-ται |
| **C. Consonant assimilation** | Labial + μ → μμ; velar + μ → γμ; dental + μ → σμ. | γέγραμμαι; πέπραγμαι; πέπεισμαι |
| **D. The 3rd plural of consonant stems is periphrastic** | Participle in ‑μένοι + εἰσί. | γεγραμμένοι εἰσί |
| **E. Middle or passive?** | Context; ὑπό + genitive → passive. The passive sense is very common in the perfect. | λέλυται ὑπὸ τοῦ βασιλέως |
| **F. Deponents** | Have active meaning: μέμνημαι "I remember." | — |

## 5. Example Sentences

| Greek (perfect middle/passive) | Transliteration | English |
|-----------------------------|----------------|---------|
| **ὁ ἵππος λέλυται.** | ho híppos lélytai | The horse has been set loose. |
| **γέγραπται ἐν τῷ νόμῳ.** | gégraptai en tôi nómōi | It is written in the law. |
| **ἡ πόλις καλῶς πεπαίδευται.** | hē pólis kalôs pepaídeutai | The city has been well educated. |
| **μέμνημαι τῶν λόγων σου.** | mémnēmai tôn lógōn sou | I remember your words. |
| **οἱ νόμοι γεγραμμένοι εἰσίν.** | hoi nómoi gegramménoi eisín | The laws stand written. |

---

## 6. Usage & Context

1. **Abiding state** — the core use: "it has happened and so it remains": λέλυται, γέγραπται.
2. **Citing texts and documents** — γέγραπται "it is written" is the formula for referring to law and scripture.
3. **Perfect deponents** — μέμνημαι "I remember," κέκτημαι "I possess (have acquired)" — present in meaning and very common.
4. **The pluperfect middle/passive** — with the augment: ἐλελύμην "I had been freed" — the same formation set in the past.

---

## 7. Historical Note

The direct attachment of the endings to the stem makes the perfect middle/passive one of the oldest and most regular formations in Greek; the consonant assimilations (γέγραπται) transparently display the language's phonological rules, and the periphrastic third plural (γεγραμμένοι εἰσί) foreshadows the periphrastic conjugations of later Greek.
