# Lesson: The Aorist Infinitive Active **‑ω**

---

## 1. What is the Aorist Infinitive Active?

The **aorist** is one of the three basic tenses of the Ancient Greek verb (present, aorist, perfect).  
The aorist expresses a **simple, completed action** without reference to its duration or repetition.  

The **infinitive** is a non‑personal verb form that can function as a noun (subject, object, complement, etc.).  

When the aorist infinitive is **active**, it denotes that the subject of the infinitive **does the action** (as opposed to a middle or passive infinitive).

In the **first aorist** (the “‑ω” infinitive) the ending is **‑ω**.  
This form is the counterpart of the first‑aorist indicative (‑α, ‑ε, ‑ον) and of the aorist participle (‑σας, ‑σᾱ).

> **Typical meaning:** “to do (something)”, “to have done”, “to have done it”.  
> The aorist infinitive is often used after verbs of saying, thinking, fearing, commanding, etc., and in purpose clauses.

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns  

### 2.1. The Basic Paradigm  

The aorist infinitive active **‑ω** is built from the **aorist stem** (the same stem that appears in the aorist indicative).  
The pattern is:

| Person/Number | Ending | Example with **λύω** (to loose) |
|---------------|--------|---------------------------------|
| 1st sg. | ‑ω | **λῦσαι** → **λῦσαι** (to loose) – note that the infinitive has no person; the table shows the ending only |
| 2nd sg. | ‑ειν | **λῦσαι** (rare) – actually the infinitive does **not** vary by person; the only form is **‑ω**. |

*In Ancient Greek the infinitive is **non‑personal**; there is only one form per verb.*  

Thus the paradigm reduces to a single cell:

| Form | Ending | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| Aorist Inf. Active | **‑ω** | **λῦσαι** → **λῦσαι** (to loose) → **λῦσω** (first‑aorist infinitive) |

> **Important:** The infinitive **does not** change for person or number. The table above is only to illustrate the ending.

### 2.2. How to Form the Aorist Infinitive Active **‑ω**

1. **Identify the aorist stem** (usually the same as the aorist indicative stem).  
2. **Add the ending ‑ω**.  

The aorist stem can be obtained in three main ways:

| Verb Type | Aorist Stem Formation | Example |
|-----------|----------------------|---------|
| **First‑aorist (‑α/‑ε)** – regular | Add **‑α/‑ε** to the present stem, then drop the **‑α/‑ε** for the infinitive and add **‑ω**. | λυ‑ (present) → **λῦσ‑** (aorist stem) → **λῦσω** |
| **Second‑aorist (‑σ‑)** – “‑σ‑” aorist | Add **‑σ‑** to the present stem (or a modified stem). | παίδ‑ → **παιδ‑** + **‑σ‑** → **παιδ‑σ‑** → **παιδ‑σ‑ω** → **παιδεσθαι** (passive) – active: **παιδεσθαι** is passive; active: **παιδεσθαι** not relevant. Use **παιδεσθαι** for passive; active: **παιδεσθαι** not needed. |
| **Irregular / Suppletive** | Memorize the aorist stem. | εἰμί → **ἦ** (aorist stem) → **ἦν** (imperfect) – not relevant. For **εἰμί** the aorist infinitive is **εἶναι** (present infinitive) because there is no aorist infinitive. |

Below are the most common patterns with concrete examples.

#### 2.2.1. First‑Aorist (‑α/‑ε) – Regular Verbs  

| Verb (present) | Aorist Stem | Aorist Inf. Active **‑ω** |
|----------------|-------------|---------------------------|
| **λύω** “to loose” | λῦσ‑ | **λῦσω** |
| **γράφω** “to write” | γραφ‑ | **γράψω** |
| **παιδεύω** “to educate” | παιδευσ‑ | **παιδεύσω** |
| **ἀκούω** “to hear” | ἀκουσ‑ | **ἀκούσω** |
| **βασιλεύω** “to be king” | βασιλευσ‑ | **βασιλεύσω** |

#### 2.2.2. Second‑Aorist (‑σ‑) – “‑σ‑” Aorist  

| Verb (present) | Aorist Stem (‑σ‑) | Aorist Inf. Active **‑ω** |
|----------------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| **λαμβάνω** “to take” | λήφ‑ (suppletive) | **λῆψις** (noun) – infinitive **λῆψαι** (active) |
| **ἔρχομαι** “to come” | ἐλθ‑ | **ἐλθῶ** |
| **διδάσκω** “to teach” | διδάξ‑ | **διδάξω** |
| **πίνω** “to drink” | πίν‑ (no ‑σ‑) – irregular | **πίνειν** (present) – aorist infinitive **πίνειν** does not exist; use **πίνειν** (present) or **πίνειν** (aorist) – actually aorist infinitive **πίνειν** is rare; better example: **βούλομαι** → **βούλεσθαι** (passive). |

*Note:* The second‑aorist often shows a **‑σ‑** in the aorist indicative (e.g., ἔλαβον → **ἔλαβον** is irregular). For the infinitive, the **‑σ‑** is retained: **λῆψαι**, **διδάξαι**, **ἐλθῶ**.

#### 2.2.3. Irregular / Suppletive Verbs  

| Verb (present) | Aorist Stem | Aorist Inf. Active **‑ω** |
|----------------|-------------|---------------------------|
| **εἰμί** “to be” | — (no aorist) | No aorist infinitive; use **εἶναι** (present) |
| **ἔχω** “to have” | ἔσχ‑ | **ἔσθαι** (aorist infinitive) |
| **γίγνομαι** “to become” | ἐγένε‑ | **γενέσθαι** (aorist infinitive, middle/passive) – active infinitive does not exist; use **γίνεσθαι** (present) |
| **φημί** “to say” | εἶπ‑ | **εἰπεῖν** (present infinitive) – aorist infinitive **εἰπῆν** does not exist; use **εἰπεῖν**. |

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions  

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **1. Use the aorist stem** | The infinitive is built on the same stem that appears in the aorist indicative (e.g., λῦσ‑, γραφ‑). | λῦσ‑ + ‑ω → **λῦσω** |
| **2. First‑aorist adds ‑ω directly** | For regular first‑aorist verbs, simply attach **‑ω** to the aorist stem. | γράφ‑ + ‑ω → **γράψω** |
| **3. Second‑aorist retains the ‑σ‑** | If the aorist stem contains **‑σ‑**, keep it before adding **‑ω**. | διδάξ‑ + ‑ω → **διδάξω** |
| **4. Contract verbs** | Verbs whose present stem ends in a vowel contract before the aorist suffix; the infinitive reflects the contracted form. | τιμ- (present) → **τῑμ‑** (aorist) → **τῑμῶ** |
| **5. Suppletive stems** | Some verbs change the root entirely in the aorist; you must memorize the aorist infinitive. | ἔχω → **ἔσθαι** |
| **6. No aorist infinitive for **εἰμί**** | The verb *to be* lacks an aorist infinitive; use the present infinitive **εἶναι**. |
| **7. Middle/Passive infinitives** | The aorist infinitive active ends in **‑ω**; the middle/passive ends in **‑σθαι** (e.g., **λῦσθαι**). Do not confuse them. |
| **8. Spelling changes** | Before **‑ω**, a final **σ** may become **ς** (e.g., **πράσσω** → **πράξω**). |
| **9. Verbs with a vowel‑ending aorist stem** | If the aorist stem ends in a vowel, the **‑ω** attaches directly (e.g., **ἔρχομαι** → **ἐλθῶ**). |
| **10. Reduplication** | Some aorist stems show reduplication (e.g., **ἔρχομαι** → **ἐλθ‑**). The infinitive follows the same stem. |

## 4. Example Sentences  

| Greek (Aorist Inf. Active) | Transliteration | English Translation |
|----------------------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Θέλω λῦσαι τὸν δεσμὸν.** | Thélō lûsai tòn desmòn. | I want **to loose** the bond. |
| **Ἀκούειν ἐστὶν ἀγαθόν.** | Akoúein estìn agathón. | **To hear** is good. |
| **Ἔδει αὐτὸν ἀποδιδράξαι.** | Édei autòn apodidráxai. | It was necessary **for him to flee**. |
| **Διέπραξα τὸν πόλεμον ἀνέλθοντα.** | Diépráxa tòn pólemon anélthonta. | I **waged** the war **having come** (purpose clause). |
| **Φησίν εἰπεῖν τὸν λόγον.** | Phḗsín eipeîn tòn lógon. | He says **to say** the word. (Note: here the infinitive is present because *φημί* lacks an aorist infinitive.) |

## 5. Usage & Context  

1. **After verbs of saying, thinking, fearing, commanding**  
   - *λέγω ὅτι* + **infinitive** (often present, but aorist can express a single, completed act).  
   - Example: *ἔφη ἀπιέναι* “he said **to go** (once).”

2. **Purpose clauses** (especially with *ἵνα* or *ὡς*).  
   - *ἦλθε ἵνα λῦσῃ τὸν δεσμὸν* “He came **in order to loose** the bond.”

3. **After *δῆλός ἐστι* (it is clear) or *δῆλον* (clear) + infinitive**  
   - *δῆλός ἐστι λῦσαι* “It is clear **to loose** (i.e., to free).”

4. **With *ὑπέρ* + infinitive** to express “instead of” or “in place of”.  
   - *ὑπέρ λῦσαι* “instead of loosing”.

5. **In indirect statements** (accusative + infinitive).  
   - *ἔφη αὐτὸν λῦσαι* “He said that he **loosed** (i.e., would loose).”

6. **Contrast with present infinitive**  
   - Present infinitive = ongoing or habitual action.  
   - Aorist infinitive = single, completed action.  
   - *ἔφη λῦσαι* (aorist) = “He said **to loose** (once).”  
   - *ἔφη λῦειν* (present) = “He said **to be loosing** (habitually).”
