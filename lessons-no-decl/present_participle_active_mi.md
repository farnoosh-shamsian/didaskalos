# Lesson: The Present Active Participle of ‑μι Verbs

*Level: beginner–intermediate*
*Prerequisites: the Greek alphabet, basic noun declension, the present indicative active of ‑μι verbs*

---

## 1. What the Present Active Participle Is

The **present active participle** is a verbal adjective expressing an action of the subject that is **simultaneous and in progress**.

- It answers the question *"who/what is …‑ing?"* ("the one who …").
- It carries the **verbal meaning of the present** but **behaves like an adjective**: it is declined for gender, number, and case.

**‑μι** verbs form the present active participle from the **short present stem** (the stem of the plural forms) with the suffix **‑ντ‑**:

> **Example** — from *δίδωμι* "to give"
> - Short present stem: **διδο‑**
> - Masculine: **διδούς** "giving" (from *διδο‑ντ‑ς* with compensatory lengthening)
> - Feminine: **διδοῦσα**
> - Neuter: **διδόν**

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns

### 2.1 Nominatives of the Three Genders for the Main ‑μι Verbs

| Verb | Short stem | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Masc. genitive |
|------|------------|-----------|----------|--------|----------------|
| **δίδωμι** "to give" | διδο‑ | **διδούς** | **διδοῦσα** | **διδόν** | διδόντος |
| **τίθημι** "to place" | τιθε‑ | **τιθείς** | **τιθεῖσα** | **τιθέν** | τιθέντος |
| **ἵστημι** "to set up" | ἱστα‑ | **ἱστάς** | **ἱστᾶσα** | **ἱστάν** | ἱστάντος |
| **δείκνυμι** "to show" | δεικνυ‑ | **δεικνύς** | **δεικνῦσα** | **δεικνύν** | δεικνύντος |
| **ἵημι** "to send" | ἱε‑ | **ἱείς** | **ἱεῖσα** | **ἱέν** | ἱέντος |
| **εἰμί** "to be" | — | **ὤν** | **οὖσα** | **ὄν** | ὄντος |
| **εἶμι** "to go" | ἰ‑ | **ἰών** | **ἰοῦσα** | **ἰόν** | ἰόντος |

### 2.2 Full Declension (model: **διδούς** "giving")

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|
| **Singular** | | | |
| Nominative | διδούς | διδοῦσα | διδόν |
| Genitive | διδόντος | διδούσης | διδόντος |
| Dative | διδόντι | διδούσῃ | διδόντι |
| Accusative | διδόντα | διδοῦσαν | διδόν |
| **Plural** | | | |
| Nominative | διδόντες | διδοῦσαι | διδόντα |
| Genitive | διδόντων | διδουσῶν | διδόντων |
| Dative | διδοῦσι(ν) | διδούσαις | διδοῦσι(ν) |
| Accusative | διδόντας | διδούσας | διδόντα |

**Key points**

* The **masculine and neuter** follow the third declension (ντ‑stem); the **feminine** follows the first.
* In the masculine/neuter dative plural, ντ drops before σ and the vowel lengthens: διδοντ‑σι → διδοῦσι(ν).
* The pattern is that of the ‑ω verb participle (λύων, λύοντος); the difference is that ‑μι verbs attach the suffix to the short stem without a thematic vowel, and the masculine nominative takes ‑ς: διδούς (not *διδων*).

### 2.3 ὤν, the Participle of εἰμί

The commonest participle of this group belongs to εἰμί "to be":

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|
| Nom. sg. | ὤν | οὖσα | ὄν |
| Gen. sg. | ὄντος | οὔσης | ὄντος |
| Nom. pl. | ὄντες | οὖσαι | ὄντα |

τὸ ὄν "that which is, being" and τὰ ὄντα "the things that are, reality" are foundational terms of Greek philosophy.

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **1. Short stem + ‑ντ‑** | The same stem as the indicative plural. | διδο‑ → διδούς |
| **2. Masculine nominative in ‑ς with compensatory lengthening** | *διδο‑ντ‑ς → διδούς; *τιθε‑ντ‑ς → τιθείς. | ἱστάς, δεικνύς |
| **3. Agreement** | The participle agrees with its noun in gender, number, and case. | ὁ διδοὺς ἀνήρ "the man who gives" |
| **4. Present reduplication is kept** | It distinguishes the present δι‑δούς from the aorist δούς — a pair you will meet constantly. | διδούς (present) ≠ δούς (aorist) |
| **5. Accent** | Masculine and neuter forms with a monosyllabic stem throw the accent onto the ending: gen. διδόντος, dat. pl. διδοῦσι(ν). | — |

## 4. Example Sentences

| Greek | Translation |
|-------|-------------|
| **ὁ διδοὺς τὰ δῶρα φίλος ἐστίν.** | "The one who gives the gifts is a friend." |
| **ταῦτα λέγει δεικνὺς τὴν ὁδόν.** | "He says this while pointing out the road." |
| **σοφὸς ὢν ταῦτα λέγει.** | "Being wise, he says this." |
| **τοῦ βασιλέως διδόντος τὰ δῶρα, χαίρουσιν οἱ πολῖται.** | "While the king is giving the gifts, the citizens rejoice." (genitive absolute) |
| **οἱ νόμους τιθέντες τὴν πόλιν σῴζουσιν.** | "Those who lay down laws preserve the city." |

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Simultaneity** — the present participle marks an action going on **at the same time as the main verb**: ἔρχεται διδούς "he comes giving."
2. **Adverbial use** — often translated with "while" or "since"; ὤν is frequently causal: σοφὸς ὤν "since he is wise."
3. **Attributive use** — with the article it qualifies a noun like an adjective: ὁ διδοὺς ἀνήρ.
4. **Substantival use** — with the article it becomes a noun in its own right: οἱ τιθέντες τοὺς νόμους "the lawgivers"; τὰ ὄντα "the things that are."
5. **Genitive absolute** — a participle plus a noun, both in the genitive, forms an independent temporal or causal clause (see the example in §4).

## 6. Historical Development & Etymology

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Indo-European origin** | The participial suffix *‑nt‑* is a shared Indo-European inheritance: Latin *‑ns/‑ntis* (*dans* "giving"), Sanskrit *‑ant‑*. |
| **Early Greek** | ‑ντ‑ forms appear on every page from Homer onwards; ἰών and ὤν are among the most frequent. |
| **Classical period** | The participle ὤν became a pillar of Greek philosophy: τὸ ὄν "that which is" in Parmenides and Plato. |
| **Later Greek** | As the ‑μι system eroded, these participles too were drawn into the thematic patterns. |

## 7. Quick Review Summary

| Feature | Present active participle (‑μι) |
|---------|---------------------------------|
| **Meaning** | An action of the subject in progress and simultaneous |
| **Formation** | Short present stem + ‑ντ‑; masculine nominative in ‑ς with compensatory lengthening |
| **Sample forms** | διδούς/διδοῦσα/διδόν; τιθείς/τιθεῖσα/τιθέν; ὤν/οὖσα/ὄν |
| **Declension** | Masculine/neuter: third declension (ντ); feminine: first declension |
| **Main uses** | Attributive adjective, adverbial clause, genitive absolute, substantivised with the article |
| **Special note** | The present reduplication (διδούς) distinguishes it from the aorist participle (δούς) |
