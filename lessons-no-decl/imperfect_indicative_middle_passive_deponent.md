# Lesson: The Imperfect Indicative Middle/Passive — Deponent Verbs

*Target level: Beginner–Intermediate*

---

## 1. What the Imperfect Indicative Middle/Passive Is

| Term | Meaning | Greek grammatical label |
|------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| **Imperfect** | A past action that was ongoing, repeated, or incomplete. | *παρατατικός* |
| **Indicative** | The "statement" mood — ordinary facts and questions. | *ὁριστική* |
| **Middle / Passive** | Two voices that share **the same endings** in the imperfect: middle = action for oneself; passive = action done to the subject. | *μέση / παθητική* |
| **Deponent verb** | A verb that has only middle/passive endings but an active meaning. | ἀποθετικά |

Combining these gives a past form that looks middle/passive but, in deponent verbs, means "the subject was doing something": ἠρχόμην "I was coming."

---

## 2. The Building Blocks of the Form

1. **Augment** — ἐ‑ (or vowel lengthening), the mark of past time.
2. **Present stem** — the same stem as the present system.
3. **Secondary middle endings** — the six endings of the imperfect.

### 2.1 The Augment

| Stem begins with… | Augment | Example |
|----------------------------|---------------|--------------------------|
| A consonant (πυνθαν‑) | ἐ‑ | **ἐ**πυνθανόμην |
| The vowel ε (ἐρχ‑) | ε → η | **ἠ**ρχόμην |
| The vowel α | α → ῃ/η | αἰσθάνομαι → **ᾐ**σθανόμην |
| The vowel ο | ο → ω | — |

### 2.2 The Secondary Middle Endings

| Person | Ending | Transliteration |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| 1st sg. | **‑όμην** | ‑ómēn |
| 2nd sg. | **‑ου** (from *‑ε-σο) | ‑ou |
| 3rd sg. | **‑ετο** | ‑eto |
| 1st pl. | **‑όμεθα** | ‑ómetha |
| 2nd pl. | **‑εσθε** | ‑esthe |
| 3rd pl. | **‑οντο** | ‑onto |

*In the 2nd person singular the σ dropped between vowels and contraction followed: ἐπυνθάνε(σ)ο → ἐπυνθάνου.*

---

## 3. Full Paradigm

Model verb: **πυνθάνομαι** "to inquire" (deponent):

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | **ἐπυνθανόμην** | **ἐπυνθανόμεθα** |
| 2nd | **ἐπυνθάνου** | **ἐπυνθάνεσθε** |
| 3rd | **ἐπυνθάνετο** | **ἐπυνθάνοντο** |

And a few other high-frequency deponents (1st person):

| Verb | Meaning | Imperfect |
|------|------|----------------|
| ἔρχομαι | to come | **ἠρχόμην** |
| γίγνομαι | to become | **ἐγιγνόμην** |
| βούλομαι | to want | **ἐβουλόμην** (sometimes ἠβουλόμην) |
| δέχομαι | to receive | **ἐδεχόμην** |
| μάχομαι | to fight | **ἐμαχόμην** |

---

## 4. Key Rules

- **The augment is obligatory** — ἔρχομαι → ἠρχόμην (not *ἐρχόμην).
- **The meaning is active** — ἐβουλόμην "I wanted," not "I was being wanted."
- **The same present stem** — the imperfect is the past partner of the present: γίγνομαι/ἐγιγνόμην.
- **In non-deponent verbs the same form has two meanings** — ἐλυόμην = "I was loosing for myself" (middle) or "I was being loosed" (passive); context decides. In deponents there is no such ambiguity.

---

## 5. Example Sentences

1. **ἠρχόμην πρὸς τὴν πόλιν.** — I was coming toward the city.
2. **οἱ παῖδες ἐγίγνοντο σοφώτεροι.** — The children were becoming wiser.
3. **ἐβούλετο τὴν εἰρήνην.** — He wanted peace.
4. **ἐπυνθάνοντο τὰ γενόμενα.** — They kept inquiring about what had happened.

---

## 6. Usage & Context

The imperfect of deponents abounds in **narrative background**: ongoing wantings, becomings, and comings-and-goings set the scene for aoristic events: ἐβούλετο… καὶ ἐγένετο "he wanted… and it came to pass." Since these verbs are among the most frequent in the language, make their conjugation second nature.

---

## 7. Historical Note

The secondary middle endings (‑μην, ‑σο, ‑το…) are a direct inheritance from Proto-Indo-European. The word "deponent" comes from Latin *dēpōnere*, "to lay aside": verbs that have laid the active voice aside. The pattern "augment + present stem + secondary ending" is the same as in the active — only the ending set differs; so with one set of six endings you have the imperfect of every deponent.
