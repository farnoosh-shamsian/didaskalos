# Lesson: The Aorist Imperative Active (ending ‑σον / ‑σατε)

---

## 1. What the Aorist Imperative Active Is  

| Category | Meaning in English | Typical Use |
|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Tense** | **Aorist** – a “simple” past‑like aspect that presents an action as a single, completed event, without reference to its duration. | Commands that refer to a one‑time, finished action (e.g., “Write the letter!”). |
| **Mood** | **Imperative** – the mood of command, request, or prohibition. | Direct orders, instructions, legal formulas, rhetorical exhortations. |
| **Voice** | **Active** – the subject of the verb performs the action. | The speaker tells *someone* to do something. |
| **Form** | The active aorist imperative is built from the **aorist stem** + the **imperative suffix** **‑σον** (2nd sg) or **‑σατε** (2nd pl). | 2nd person singular: *λῦσον* “loosen!”  | 2nd person plural: *λῦσατε* “loosen, you all!” |

> **Why “‑σον/‑σατε” and not “‑ω”?**  
> The ending **‑σον** (and its plural **‑σατε**) is the regular aorist‑imperative suffix for most verbs. The “‑ω” you see in textbooks refers to the *present* indicative active (e.g., λύω). In the aorist imperative the vowel **‑σ‑** is inserted before the personal ending.

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns  

### 2.1. Regular (‑ω) Verbs – “Strong” Aorist  

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 2nd sg | **‑σον** (e.g., **λῦσον**) | — |
| 2nd pl | — | **‑σατε** (e.g., **λῦσατε**) |

**How to form:**  

1. Find the **aorist active stem** (the stem that appears in the aorist indicative, e.g., *λῡ‑* from *λῦσα*).  
2. Add **‑σον** for the singular or **‑σατε** for the plural.  

| Verb (present) | Aorist stem | 2nd sg Imperative | 2nd pl Imperative |
|----------------|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| λύω “loosen”   | λῡ‑        | λῦσον             | λῦσατε            |
| γράφω “write”  | γραψ‑      | γράψον            | γράψατε           |
| ἀκούω “hear”   | ἀκησ‑      | ἀκῆσον            | ἀκησάτε           |
| ἔρχομαι “come” (aorist ἦλθον) – irregular | — | — | — |

### 2.2. Contract Verbs (‑άω, ‑έω, ‑όω)  

Contract verbs have a slightly different shape because the vowel of the stem contracts with the suffix.

| Verb (present) | Aorist stem | 2nd sg Imperative | 2nd pl Imperative |
|----------------|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| τιμάω “honour” | τιμησ‑      | **τιμήσον** (or **τιμῆσον**) | **τιμήσατε** |
| διδάσκω “teach”| διδασ‑     | **διδάσον** | **διδάσατε** |
| ἀγγέλλω “announce”| ἀγγελ‑ | **ἀγγέλσον** | **ἀγγέλσατε** |

*Note:* For many contract verbs the **‑σ‑** is still present (‑σον/‑σατε). The only truly “contracted” imperatives are those whose aorist stem ends in a vowel (rare).  

### 2.3. Irregular Aorist Imperatives  

| Verb | Aorist Stem | 2nd sg Imperative | 2nd pl Imperative | Comment |
|------|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|---------|
| εἰμί “to be” | — (no aorist) | **γίνου** (present) | **γίνεσθε** | Uses present forms because there is no aorist. |
| ἔρχομαι “to come” | ἐλθ‑ | **ἔλθον** (sg) | **ἔλθετε** (pl) | The aorist imperative of *ἔρχομαι* is built from the aorist stem *ἐλθ‑*. |
| ἵστημι “to stand” | ἔστησ‑ | **στήσον** | **στήσατε** | The aorist stem *ἔστησ‑* yields the regular ‑σον/‑σατε forms. |
| ὁράω “to see” | εἶδ‑ | **ἴδε** (sg) | **ἴδετε** (pl) | Here the aorist imperative uses a **different root** (the perfect stem) – a lexical irregularity. |

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions  

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **R1. Use the aorist active stem** | The imperative is built from the same stem that appears in the aorist indicative (e.g., *λῡ‑* from *ἔλυσα*). | λῦσον (from λῡ‑). |
| **R2. Add ‑σον (sg) / ‑σατε (pl)** | The suffix ‑σ‑ marks the aorist imperative; the personal endings are ‑ον / ‑ατε. | γράψον, γράψατε. |
| **R3. Contract verbs keep the ‑σ‑** | Even though the verb is a contract verb, the aorist imperative still contains ‑σ‑ (unless the aorist stem ends in a vowel). | τιμήσον, τιμήσατε. |
| **R4. Verbs whose aorist stem ends in ‑α, ‑ε, ‑ο** | The ‑σ‑ may be omitted, giving simple ‑α / ‑ε / ‑ο endings (rare). | ἔλθον, ἔλθετε (no ‑σ‑). |
| **R5. Verbs with aorist stem in ‑μι** | The imperative uses ‑σον/‑σατε after dropping the ‑μι. | δέχομαι → δέξαι (but that is aorist subjunctive; for imperative: **δέξου** is present). |
| **R6. Irregular roots** | Some verbs use a completely different root for the aorist imperative (e.g., ὁράω → ἴδε). | ἴδε, ἴδετε. |
