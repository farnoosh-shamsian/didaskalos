# Lesson: The Future Middle Infinitive of ‑μι Verbs

*Target level: Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Future indicative middle of ‑μι verbs, future middle infinitive of ω-verbs.*

---

## 1. What This Form Is

The **future middle infinitive** of a ‑μι verb means "to be going to …":

> **δώσεσθαι** "to be about to give" · **θήσεσθαι** "to be about to place" · **ἔσεσθαι** "to be going to be"

As with all future infinitives, it expresses **time** rather than aspect, and it appears almost exclusively in **indirect discourse** and after **μέλλω**.

And as with the future indicative, the ‑μι verbs are **not irregular here**: in the future they have already gone over to the thematic pattern, so the infinitive is formed exactly as for an ω-verb.

One form dominates the category in real texts: **ἔσεσθαι**, the future infinitive of εἰμί.

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns

### 2.1 ἔσεσθαι — the form to learn first

εἰμί has no future active, so **ἔσεσθαι** is simply "to be going to be":

> ἔφη ταῦτα ῥᾴδια **ἔσεσθαι**. — "He said that this would be easy."

It is one of the most frequent infinitives in Greek prose, and in Thucydides' speeches it appears wherever a speaker predicts an outcome.

### 2.2 The Great ‑μι Verbs

Future stem (long-grade root + σ) + **‑εσθαι**:

| Verb | Future indicative | Future middle infinitive |
|---|---|---|
| **εἰμί** "be" | ἔσομαι | **ἔσεσθαι** |
| **δίδωμι** "give" | δώσομαι | **δώσεσθαι** |
| **τίθημι** "place" | θήσομαι | **θήσεσθαι** |
| **ἵστημι** "set up" | στήσομαι | **στήσεσθαι** |
| **ἵημι** "send" | ἥσομαι | **ἥσεσθαι** |
| **δείκνυμι** "show" | δείξομαι | **δείξεσθαι** |

Compounds behave predictably: ἀποδώσεσθαι, παραδώσεσθαι, ἀποστήσεσθαι, ἀφήσεσθαι.

### 2.3 The Present/Future Contrast

| Present middle infinitive | Future middle infinitive |
|---|---|
| δίδοσθαι "to be given" | **δώσεσθαι** "to be about to give" |
| τίθεσθαι | **θήσεσθαι** |
| εἶναι "to be" | **ἔσεσθαι** "to be going to be" |

Note that the present infinitive of εἰμί is **εἶναι** (athematic, ‑ναι) while the future is **ἔσεσθαι** (thematic, ‑εσθαι). The two are not built alike at all.

---

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **1. The future is thematic** | No athematic endings survive in the future; ‑εσθαι throughout. | δώσεσθαι, like λύσεσθαι |
| **2. No reduplication** | Present δίδοσθαι keeps δι‑; future δώσεσθαι drops it. | |
| **3. Long-grade root + σ** | δω‑σ‑, θη‑σ‑, στη‑σ‑. | |
| **4. εἶναι vs. ἔσεσθαι** | Present ‑ναι, future ‑εσθαι. A very common point of confusion. | |
| **5. Restricted to indirect discourse and μέλλω** | Not a complement of βούλομαι or δεῖ. | |
| **6. Middle form, active meaning** | ἔσεσθαι has no middle sense whatever. | |
| **7. Accent** | Recessive: ἔσεσθαι, δώσεσθαι. | |

---

## 4. Example Sentences

| Greek | Translation |
|---|---|
| ἔφη τὸν πόλεμον μέγαν **ἔσεσθαι**. | "He said that the war would be great." |
| ἤλπιζον τοὺς συμμάχους χρήματα **δώσεσθαι**. | "They hoped the allies would provide money." |
| ὑπέσχοντο τὴν πόλιν **παραδώσεσθαι**. | "They promised to surrender the city." |
| ἐνόμιζον οὐκ ἐλεύθεροι **ἔσεσθαι**. | "They thought they would not be free." |
| ἔμελλε τὸ στράτευμα ἐκεῖ **στήσεσθαι**. | "The army was about to take its position there." |

---

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Indirect discourse** — after φημί, λέγω, νομίζω, ἐλπίζω, ὑπισχνέομαι. The future infinitive stands for a future indicative of the direct speech.
2. **After μέλλω** — μέλλει ἔσεσθαι "it is going to be."
3. **Predictions in oratory** — ἔσεσθαι is the backbone of argument about consequences; expect it wherever a speaker says "the result will be …".
4. **Subject accusative** — the subject of the infinitive goes in the accusative when it differs from the main subject: ἔφη **τὸν πόλεμον** ἔσεσθαι.
5. **With predicate adjectives** — the predicate agrees with the accusative subject: ἔφη τὸν πόλεμον **μέγαν** ἔσεσθαι.

---

## 6. Historical Development & Etymology

- The **complete thematicisation of the ‑μι verbs in the future** was already accomplished in Homer; the future is the part of the verbal system where the athematic class surrendered earliest.
- **ἔσεσθαι** is built on the root *h₁es‑ (Latin *erō*, Sanskrit *ásmi*) with the desiderative *‑s‑ and the ordinary middle infinitive ending.
- The pairing of an athematic present infinitive (εἶναι) with a thematic future infinitive (ἔσεσθαι) is a good illustration of how the Greek verb assembled its paradigms from formations of different ages.
- With the collapse of infinitival indirect discourse in Koine, this form vanished; Modern Greek uses θα + a finite verb inside a ότι-clause instead.
