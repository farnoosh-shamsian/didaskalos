# Lesson: The Present Optative Middle/Passive of ω-Verbs

*Target level: Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Present indicative middle/passive of ω-verbs, present optative active*

---

## 1. What This Form Is

The **present optative middle/passive** of an ω-verb expresses an ongoing wish or possibility in which the subject is acted upon (passive) or acts with reference to itself (middle). One set of endings serves both voices in the present system — context tells you which is meant:

- φέροιτο (middle) — "may he carry off for himself / win"
- φέροιτο (passive) — "may he be carried"

## 2. Morphology

Present stem + theme vowel + **-οι-** + middle/passive secondary endings:

**λύω "release"** (middle "ransom"; passive "be released"):

| Person | Form | Gloss (passive) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 sg | **λυοίμην** | may I be released |
| 2 sg | **λύοιο** | may you be released |
| 3 sg | **λύοιτο** | may he/she/it be released |
| 1 pl | **λυοίμεθα** | may we be released |
| 2 pl | **λύοισθε** | may you (pl.) be released |
| 3 pl | **λύοιντο** | may they be released |

Corpus-frequent examples of the same pattern:

| Verb | 3 sg optative m/p | Middle sense | Passive sense |
|---|---|---|---|
| φέρω | **φέροιτο** | win, carry off | be carried |
| ἄγω | **ἄγοιτο** | take with one; marry | be led |
| φαίνω | **φαίνοιτο** | appear, seem | be shown |
| νέμω | **νέμοιτο** | possess, inhabit | be distributed |
| πείθω | **πείθοιτο** | obey, trust | be persuaded |

### Contract verbs

The stem vowel contracts with the theme vowel before -οι-:

| Verb | 1 sg | 3 sg |
|---|---|---|
| καλέω "call" | καλοίμην | καλοῖτο (epic uncontracted: καλεοίμην) |
| τιμάω "honor" | τιμῴμην | τιμῷτο |
| χράομαι "use" | χρῴμην | **χρῷτο** |
| δηλόω "show" | δηλοίμην | δηλοῖτο |

## 3. Key Rules & Common Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Middle and passive share one form in the present** | Only the aorist and future distinguish them. Translate by context. | φαίνοιτο "would seem" (mid.) vs. "would be shown" (pass.) |
| **2. 2 sg -οιο** | From -οι-σο with loss of σ. | λύοιο, πείθοιο |
| **3. α- and ο-contracts take ῳ** | τιμῴμην, χρῷτο — the iota goes subscript. | |
| **4. Epic/Ionic 3 pl -οίατο** | πειθοίατο = πείθοιντο (Homer, Herodotus). | |
| **5. φαίνοιτο ἄν is everywhere in Plato** | "it would appear" — the standard hedged assertion of philosophical prose. | |

## 4. Example Sentences

- ταῦτα δίκαια ἂν φαίνοιτο. — "These things would appear just." (potential)
- εἰ πείθοιο τοῖς νόμοις, εὖ πράττοις ἄν. — "If you obeyed the laws, you would fare well."
- εἴθε ἡ πόλις καλῶς νέμοιτο. — "May the city be well governed!"
- ἤροντο εἰ αἱ σπονδαὶ φυλάττοιντο. — "They asked whether the treaty was being kept." (passive optative in indirect question)
- ὁπότε ἄγοιντο αἱ παρθένοι, πᾶσα ἡ πόλις ἑώρταζεν. — "Whenever the maidens were led in procession, the whole city kept festival." (past general condition)

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Potential statements with ἄν** — the commonest environment: φαίνοιτο ἄν, γίγνοιτο ἄν, λέγοιτο ἄν "one might say".
2. **Past general conditions:** εἰ/ὁπότε + present optative for repeated circumstances in the past.
3. **Indirect discourse and questions in past sequence:** a present middle/passive indicative of the original words becomes a present optative.
4. Choose **present vs. aorist** optative by aspect, not by time: present = process or repetition; aorist = single event.
