# Lesson: The Imperfect Indicative Middle/Passive (ω-Verbs)

*Target level: Beginner–Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Greek alphabet, basic noun declension, present indicative active endings, the concept of voice.*

---

## 1. What Is the Imperfect Indicative Middle/Passive?

The **imperfect** describes an action that was **ongoing, repeated, or habitual** in the past (or a past state).
In the **middle/passive** voice the subject either:

* **Middle** — acts for itself, in its own interest, or reflexively ("he was washing himself"); or
* **Passive** — receives the action ("the house was being built").

In the imperfect (as in the present) middle and passive have **identical forms**; context determines the meaning.

> **Key idea:** the imperfect middle/passive says that the subject *in the past* was **in the middle of** an action or was **undergoing** one.

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns

### 2.1 Construction

**Augment + present stem + thematic vowel + secondary middle endings:**

| Person | Active ending | Middle/Passive ending |
|--------|---------------|-----------------------|
| 1 sg. | ‑ον | **‑όμην** |
| 2 sg. | ‑ες | **‑ου** (from *‑ε-σο) |
| 3 sg. | ‑ε(ν) | **‑ετο** |
| 1 pl. | ‑ομεν | **‑όμεθα** |
| 2 pl. | ‑ετε | **‑εσθε** |
| 3 pl. | ‑ον | **‑οντο** |

### 2.2 Full Paradigm: **λύω** "to loose"

| Person | Form | Gloss |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| 1 sg. | **ἐλυόμην** | I was loosing for myself / was being loosed |
| 2 sg. | **ἐλύου** | you were loosing for yourself / were being loosed |
| 3 sg. | **ἐλύετο** | he/she/it was loosing for himself / was being loosed |
| 1 pl. | **ἐλυόμεθα** | we were loosing for ourselves / were being loosed |
| 2 pl. | **ἐλύεσθε** | you (pl.) were loosing for yourselves / were being loosed |
| 3 pl. | **ἐλύοντο** | they were loosing for themselves / were being loosed |

*2nd singular: ἐλύε(σ)ο → ἐλύου — the σ dropped between vowels and contraction followed.*

### 2.3 Another Example: **πράττω** "to do"

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|------|--------|
| 1st | ἐπραττόμην | ἐπραττόμεθα |
| 2nd | ἐπράττου | ἐπράττεσθε |
| 3rd | ἐπράττετο | ἐπράττοντο |

### 2.4 Contract Verbs

The stem vowel merges with the thematic vowel:

| Verb | 1 sg. | 3 sg. | 3 pl. |
|------|------|------------------|------------------|
| **φιλέω** "to love" | **ἐφιλούμην** | **ἐφιλεῖτο** | **ἐφιλοῦντο** |
| **τιμάω** "to honor" | **ἐτιμώμην** | **ἐτιμᾶτο** | **ἐτιμῶντο** |
| **δηλόω** "to show" | **ἐδηλούμην** | **ἐδηλοῦτο** | **ἐδηλοῦντο** |

## 3. Key Rules & Common Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| **1. The augment is obligatory** | ἐ‑ before a consonant; lengthening of an initial vowel: ἀγγέλλομαι → ἠγγελλόμην. |
| **2. Secondary middle endings** | ‑μην, ‑ου, ‑ετο, ‑μεθα, ‑εσθε, ‑ντο. |
| **3. Loss of σ in the 2nd singular** | *‑εσο → ‑ου; poetry sometimes still shows uncontracted ‑εο. |
| **4. Middle = passive in form** | ἐλύετο can bear either meaning; ὑπό + genitive is the sure sign of the passive. |
| **5. Deponents use the same formation** | ἠρχόμην "I was coming," ἐβουλόμην "I wanted" — active in meaning. |
| **6. εἰμί has no middle/passive** | Only ἦν and its family. |
| **7. It is the partner of the present** | Present λύομαι → imperfect ἐλυόμην; everything you know about the present middle/passive carries over with the augment and secondary endings. |

## 4. Example Sentences

| Greek (imperfect middle/passive) | Transliteration | English |
|----------------------------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **ἡ οἰκία ᾠκοδομεῖτο.** | hē oikía ōikodomeîto | The house was being built. |
| **οἱ στρατιῶται ὡπλίζοντο.** | hoi stratiôtai hōplízonto | The soldiers were arming themselves. |
| **ἐλύετο ὁ ἵππος ὑπὸ τοῦ παιδός.** | elýeto ho híppos hypò tou paidós | The horse was being untied by the boy. |
| **ἐπαιδευόμην ὑπὸ σοφοῦ διδασκάλου.** | epaideuómēn… | I was being educated by a wise teacher. |
| **ἐβουλόμεθα εἰρήνην.** | eboulómetha eirḗnēn | We wanted peace. (deponent) |

---

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Narrative background** — "while the soldiers were arming themselves, the general was speaking": ὡπλίζοντο + ἔλεγε.
2. **Habitual action** — "he used to wash (himself) every morning": ἐλούετο.
3. **Continuous state** — "the city was being guarded": ἐφυλάττετο.
4. **Telling the voices apart** — a reflexive pronoun (ἑαυτόν) or the subject's benefit → middle; ὑπό + genitive or an external agent → passive.

---

## 6. Historical Development & Etymology

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Origin** | The secondary middle endings come from Proto-Indo-European and have parallels in Sanskrit. |
| **Loss of σ** | ‑εσο → ‑εο → ‑ου is a sound change within Greek; uncontracted forms appear in Homer. |
| **Unity of middle and passive** | In the present system (present and imperfect) Greek never separated middle from passive; the formal distinction arose only in the aorist and future. |
| **Link to the present** | The imperfect is in every respect "the past of the present system" — which is what makes it easy to learn. |
