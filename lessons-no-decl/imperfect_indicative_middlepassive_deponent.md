# Lesson: The Imperfect Indicative – Middle/Passive (Deponent)  

*Target level: Beginner‑Intermediate*  

---

## 1. What the Imperfect Indicative Middle/Passive Is  

| Term | Meaning in English | Greek grammatical label |
|------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| **Imperfect** | A past‑time action that was ongoing, repeated, or incomplete. | *παρατατικός* |
| **Indicative** | The “statement” mood – used for ordinary facts or questions. | *ἐνδεικτική* |
| **Middle / Passive** | Two voices that share the same set of endings in the imperfect. The **middle** shows the subject acting on itself or for its own benefit; the **passive** shows the subject being acted upon. | *μέσος / παθητικός* |
| **Deponent verb** | A verb that has only middle‑ or passive‑form endings, but its meaning is active (the subject does the action). | *δέπονεν* |

When we combine these, we get **the imperfect indicative middle/passive** – a past‑tense form that looks middle/passive but (for deponent verbs) means “the subject was doing something.”  

---

## 2. The Building Blocks of the Form  

1. **Augment** – a prefixed ε‑ (or lengthened vowel) that marks past time.  
2. **Stem** – the part of the verb that stays constant through the paradigm.  
3. **Middle/Passive endings** – the set of six endings used for the imperfect.  

### 2.1 The Augment  

| Original stem begins with… | Augment added | Resulting form (example) |
|----------------------------|---------------|--------------------------|
| Consonant (e.g., *λυ‑*) | ε‑ before the first consonant → *ἐ‑* | *ἐλύ‑* (from *λυ‑*) |
| Vowel (e.g., *ἀ‑*) | Lengthen the vowel → *η‑* | *ἤ‑* (from *ἀ‑*) |
| Vowel *ε* or *ο* | Lengthen to *η* or *ω* respectively | *ἠ‑* (from *ε‑*), *ὠ‑* (from *ο‑*) |

> **Exception:** Some verbs that already begin with a vowel (especially many deponents) may omit the augment in the imperfect, e.g. **ἔρχομην** “I was coming” (instead of the expected *ἠρχομην*). This is a “weak augment” or “no augment” phenomenon and is simply memorised.

### 2.2 The Middle/Passive Endings of the Imperfect  

| Person | Ending | Transliteration |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| 1st sg. | **‑μην** | ‑mēn |
| 2nd sg. | **‑σο** | ‑so |
| 3rd sg. | **‑το** | ‑to |
| 1st pl. | **‑μεθα** | ‑metha |
| 2nd pl. | **‑σθε** | ‑sthe |
| 3rd pl. | **‑ντο** | ‑nto |

These endings are attached **after** the augment‑plus‑stem.
