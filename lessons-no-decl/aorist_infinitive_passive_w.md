# Lesson: The Aorist Passive Infinitive of ω-Verbs

*Target level: Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Aorist indicative passive, present passive infinitive, aorist active infinitive.*

---

## 1. What This Form Is

The **aorist passive infinitive** expresses a single, complete action **undergone by** its subject:

> **λυθῆναι** "to be loosed" · **πεισθῆναι** "to be persuaded" · **γραφῆναι** "to be written"

It has no person or number, and — outside indirect discourse — no reference to past time. What it carries is **aspect** (a single act) and **voice** (the subject is acted upon).

Unlike the present, where middle and passive share one form, the aorist keeps the voices apart:

| Voice | Aorist infinitive of λύω | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Active | λῦσαι | to loose |
| Middle | λύσασθαι | to ransom (for oneself) |
| **Passive** | **λυθῆναι** | to be loosed |

This is one of the places where Greek is more precise than the present system allows.

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns

Every aorist passive infinitive ends in **‑ναι** with a **circumflex on the preceding η**. There are two types, exactly matching the two aorist passive indicatives.

### 2.1 First (θη-) Aorist Passive — ending **‑θῆναι**

The great majority of verbs. Take the aorist passive stem in ‑θη‑ and add ‑ναι:

| Verb | Aorist passive indicative | Infinitive | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| λύω "loose" | ἐλύθην | **λυθῆναι** | to be loosed |
| παιδεύω "educate" | ἐπαιδεύθην | **παιδευθῆναι** | to be educated |
| πείθω "persuade" | ἐπείσθην | **πεισθῆναι** | to be persuaded |
| ἄγω "lead" | ἤχθην | **ἀχθῆναι** | to be led |
| τιμάω "honour" | ἐτιμήθην | **τιμηθῆναι** | to be honoured |
| ποιέω "make" | ἐποιήθην | **ποιηθῆναι** | to be made |
| λέγω "say" | ἐλέχθην | **λεχθῆναι** | to be said |

*Note the consonant changes before ‑θη‑: πειθ‑ → πεισ‑, ἀγ‑ → ἀχ‑, λεγ‑ → λεχ‑. These are regular and worth learning as a set.*

### 2.2 Second (η-) Aorist Passive — ending **‑ῆναι**

A smaller group forms the passive without θ. The stem often shows a changed vowel:

| Verb | Aorist passive indicative | Infinitive | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| γράφω "write" | ἐγράφην | **γραφῆναι** | to be written |
| φαίνω "show" | ἐφάνην | **φανῆναι** | to appear, be shown |
| βλάπτω "harm" | ἐβλάβην | **βλαβῆναι** | to be harmed |
| στέλλω "send" | ἐστάλην | **σταλῆναι** | to be sent |
| φθείρω "destroy" | ἐφθάρην | **φθαρῆναι** | to be destroyed |
| σῴζω "save" | ἐσώθην¹ | **σωθῆναι** | to be saved |

¹ σῴζω is in fact a first (θη) passive — listed here because σωθῆναι is so frequent that it is worth memorising alongside the others.

> **The two types mean the same thing.** The difference is purely morphological, like "strong" and "weak" verbs in English.

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **1. No augment** | The augment belongs to the indicative. Strip ἐ‑ before forming the infinitive. | ἐλύθην → λυθῆναι |
| **2. Accent always on the η** | λυθῆναι, γραφῆναι, φανῆναι — a circumflex on the penult, without exception. This makes the form instantly recognisable. | |
| **3. ‑θῆναι vs. ‑ῆναι** | Determined by the verb, not by meaning. Learn it with the sixth principal part. | λυθῆναι but γραφῆναι |
| **4. Consonant adjustments before θ** | Labials → φ (γραφθ‑), velars → χ (ἀχθ‑), dentals → σ (πεισθ‑). | πείθω → πεισθῆναι |
| **5. Aspect, not time** | βούλεται λυθῆναι "he wants to be released" — no past sense. | |
| **6. In indirect discourse it is past** | ἔφη λυθῆναι "he said that he had been released." | |
| **7. Many "passives" are intransitive** | φανῆναι means "to appear," not "to be shown," in most contexts; likewise σωθῆναι "to get away safely." Do not force a passive English rendering. | |

---

## 4. Example Sentences

| Greek | Translation |
|---|---|
| οἱ αἰχμάλωτοι ἠξίουν **λυθῆναι**. | "The prisoners asked to be released." |
| οὐκ ἤθελον ὑπὸ τῶν Ἀθηναίων **ἀχθῆναι**. | "They were unwilling to be led by the Athenians." |
| ταῦτα ἄξια ἦν **γραφῆναι**. | "These things were worth writing down." |
| ἐβούλοντο **σωθῆναι** εἰς τὴν πόλιν. | "They wished to get safely to the city." |
| δεῖ τοὺς λόγους ὑπὸ πάντων **ἀκουσθῆναι**. | "The speeches must be heard by everyone." |

---

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Complement of verbs of wishing, asking, being able** — βούλομαι, ἀξιόω, δύναμαι: the commonest environment.
2. **With impersonals** — δεῖ, χρή, ἀνάγκη ἐστί: δεῖ σωθῆναι "we must be saved."
3. **Indirect discourse** — after verbs of saying and thinking, representing an aorist passive indicative.
4. **After ὥστε** — result clauses: ὥστε τὴν πόλιν διαφθαρῆναι "so that the city was destroyed."
5. **Articular infinitive** — τὸ σωθῆναι "salvation, deliverance"; τὸ ῥηθῆναι "the being said." Thucydides uses such abstractions freely.
6. **The agent** — expressed by ὑπό + genitive: λυθῆναι ὑπὸ τοῦ στρατηγοῦ "to be released by the general."

---

## 6. Historical Development & Etymology

- The **‑θη‑** passive is a Greek innovation with no exact Indo-European parallel; it grew out of an old stative/intransitive formation, which explains why so many ‑θη‑ aorists are intransitive rather than truly passive (φανῆναι "appear," ἡσθῆναι "be pleased").
- The **‑η‑ (second) passive** is the older layer, a bare stative suffix added to the root — the same element seen in ἐμάνην "I went mad" and ἐρρύην "I flowed."
- Both types spread at the expense of the **aorist middle**, which in earlier Greek had carried much of the passive load; by Attic the division of labour is largely settled.
- In Koine the ‑θη‑ type continued to expand, and its descendant is still visible in the Modern Greek passive aorist (λύθηκα).
