# Lesson: The Aorist Optative Middle of ω-Verbs

*Target level: Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Aorist indicative middle, present optative, middle voice basics*

---

## 1. What This Form Is

The **aorist optative middle** of an ω-verb expresses a wish or possibility about a single complete action, where the subject acts **for itself or on itself** (the middle voice): ἑλοίμην ἄν "I would choose (for myself)".

Remember the two jobs of the middle with ω-verbs:
- **True middle:** the subject acts in its own interest — αἱροῦμαι "I choose", πείθομαι "I obey / let myself be persuaded".
- Many verbs shift meaning in the middle: αἱρέω "take" → αἱροῦμαι "choose"; πείθω "persuade" → πείθομαι "obey".

## 2. Morphology

As always in the aorist, the formation follows the verb's **aorist stem type**:

### 2.1 First (sigmatic) aorist — -σαίμην, -σαιο, -σαιτο

**παύω "stop"** (aorist middle ἐπαυσάμην "I ceased"):

| Person | Form | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| 1 sg | **παυσαίμην** | may I cease |
| 2 sg | **παύσαιο** | may you cease |
| 3 sg | **παύσαιτο** | may he cease |
| 1 pl | **παυσαίμεθα** | may we cease |
| 2 pl | **παύσαισθε** | may you (pl.) cease |
| 3 pl | **παύσαιντο** | may they cease |

Same pattern: τίνω "pay" → mid. "punish, avenge": **τισαίμην, τίσαιτο**; πειράω → πειράομαι "try": **πειρησαίμην** (Ionic/epic) / **πειρασαίμην** (Attic), 3 sg **πειρήσαιτο / πειράσαιτο**.

### 2.2 Second (thematic) aorist — -οίμην, -οιο, -οιτο

**αἱρέω "take", middle "choose"** (aorist middle εἱλόμην, stem ἑλ-):

| Person | Form | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| 1 sg | **ἑλοίμην** | may I choose |
| 2 sg | **ἕλοιο** | may you choose |
| 3 sg | **ἕλοιτο** | may he choose |
| 1 pl | **ἑλοίμεθα** | may we choose |
| 2 pl | **ἕλοισθε** | may you (pl.) choose |
| 3 pl | **ἕλοιντο** | may they choose |

Same pattern: πείθω, 2nd aor. mid. ἐπιθόμην "I obeyed" → **πιθοίμην, πίθοιο, πίθοιτο**; λαμβάνω → λάβοιτο "may he take hold of".

## 3. Key Rules & Common Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Stem type decides the vowel** | Sigmatic aorists take -αι- (παύσαιτο); thematic aorists take -οι- (ἕλοιτο). | |
| **2. No augment** | εἱλόμην (indicative) but ἑλοίμην (optative). | |
| **3. 2 sg -αιο / -οιο** | σ is lost between vowels: παύσαι(σ)ο → παύσαιο. | |
| **4. Beware παύσαι** | παύσαι can be aorist middle imperative or infinitive/optative forms of similar shape; accent and context decide. | |
| **5. Epic 3 pl -αίατο / -οίατο** | In Homer: πειθοίατο = πείθοιντο. | |

## 4. Example Sentences

- θᾶττον ἂν θάνατον ἕλοιτο ἢ δουλείαν. — "He would sooner choose death than slavery." (potential optative)
- εἰ παύσαιντο τοῦ πολέμου, εὐδαίμονες ἂν εἶεν. — "If they should cease from the war, they would be happy."
- πίθοιο ἂν τοῖς σοφωτέροις; — "Would you obey those who are wiser?"
- ηὔχετο τίσαιτο τοὺς ἀδικοῦντας. — "He prayed that he might punish the wrongdoers."
- ἐβουλεύοντο ὅπως τὴν πόλιν ἕλοιντο. — "They deliberated how they might take the city (for themselves)."

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Potential optative with ἄν** is the commonest use in prose: ἑλοίμην ἄν "I would choose" — the polite, softened future.
2. **Future-less-vivid conditions:** εἰ + aorist optative in the if-clause pictures a single hypothetical event.
3. **Past-sequence subordinate clauses:** purpose (ἵνα ἕλοιντο), indirect questions (ἤροντο εἰ παύσαιντο "they asked whether they would stop").
4. The **aspect** is what distinguishes this from the present optative: αἱροῖτο = "may he keep choosing" (process), ἕλοιτο = "may he choose" (single act).
