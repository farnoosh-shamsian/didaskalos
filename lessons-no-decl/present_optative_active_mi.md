# Lesson: The Present Optative Active of μι-Verbs

*Target level: Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Present indicative of μι-verbs, present optative active of ω-verbs*

---

## 1. What This Form Is

The **present optative active** of μι-verbs expresses a wish, a polite possibility (with ἄν), or a reported thought in past-tense contexts — exactly like the optative of ω-verbs, but built on the athematic present stem.

In real texts one verb towers over all the others here: **εἰμί "to be"**. Its optative **εἴη** ("may it be / it would be") is one of the most frequent optative forms in all of Greek. Learn εἰμί's optative first; the rest of the class follows the same pattern.

## 2. Morphology

μι-verbs form the present optative with the mood suffix **-ιη-** (singular) / **-ι-** (plural) added directly to the short present stem, plus the secondary endings:

### 2.1 εἰμί "to be" — learn this cold

| Person | Form | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| 1 sg | **εἴην** | may I be / I would be |
| 2 sg | **εἴης** | may you be |
| 3 sg | **εἴη** | may he/she/it be |
| 1 pl | **εἶμεν** (also εἴημεν) | may we be |
| 2 pl | **εἶτε** (also εἴητε) | may you (pl.) be |
| 3 pl | **εἶεν** (also εἴησαν) | may they be |

### 2.2 φημί "to say"

| Person | Form |
|---|---|
| 1 sg | **φαίην** |
| 2 sg | **φαίης** |
| 3 sg | **φαίη** |
| 1 pl | **φαῖμεν** |
| 2 pl | **φαῖτε** |
| 3 pl | **φαῖεν** |

### 2.3 δίδωμι "to give" (same pattern: τίθημι → τιθείην, ἵστημι → ἱσταίην)

| Person | Form |
|---|---|
| 1 sg | **διδοίην** |
| 2 sg | **διδοίης** |
| 3 sg | **διδοίη** |
| 1 pl | **διδοῖμεν** |

## 3. Key Rules & Common Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Suffix -ιη- in the singular, -ι- in the plural** | εἴην/εἴης/εἴη but εἶμεν/εἶτε/εἶεν; διδοίην but διδοῖμεν. | The longer plurals (εἴημεν, εἴησαν) also occur. |
| **2. The stem vowel stays short** | διδο- (not διδω-), τιθε- (not τιθη-), ἱστα- (not ἱστη-). | διδοίη, τιθείη, ἱσταίη |
| **3. Compounds of εἰμί follow εἰμί** | πάρειμι → παρείη, μέτειμι → μετείη, σύνειμι → συνείη. | εἰ μετείη μοι — "if I had a share" |
| **4. Accent falls on the -οι-/-ει-/-αι- of the suffix** | διδοίην, τιθείην — the optative diphthong counts as long. | |
| **5. εἶεν as an idiom** | εἶεν on its own means "very well then, so far so good" — a discourse particle grown out of the optative. | εἶεν· τί οὖν; — "Well then, what now?" |

## 4. Example Sentences

- εἴη μοι τοῦτο. — "May this be mine!"
- οὐκ ἂν εἴη τοῦτο ἀληθές. — "This would not be true." (potential optative with ἄν)
- φαίης ἂν αὐτὸν σοφὸν εἶναι. — "You would say that he is wise."
- ἤρετο εἰ ἡ χώρα εἰρήνην ἄγοι καὶ εἰ σῖτος παρείη. — "He asked whether the land was at peace and whether grain was available." (optative in indirect question after a past main verb)
- διδοῖεν οἱ θεοὶ ὑμῖν τὴν νίκην. — "May the gods grant you victory."

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Potential optative:** εἴη ἄν "it would be" is the standard polite assertion in Attic prose — softer than ἐστί "it is".
2. **Indirect discourse after past tenses:** after a main verb in a past tense, an indicative of the original speech may become optative: ἔλεγεν ὅτι χαλεπὸν εἴη "he said that it was difficult".
3. **Wishes:** with or without εἴθε / εἰ γάρ: εἴθε φίλος ἡμῖν εἴης "if only you were our friend".
4. In **Herodotus and Xenophon**, clauses of the type εἰ + optative ("if ever ...", "in case ...") are everywhere; εἴη and its compounds do much of that work.
