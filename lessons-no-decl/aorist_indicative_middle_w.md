# Lesson: The Aorist · Indicative · Middle (ending ‑ω)

---

## 1. What the term means  

| Term | What it tells us |
|------|------------------|
| **Aorist** | A past‑time aspect that presents an action as a simple, completed whole ( “once”, “at once”, “the fact of doing”). |
| **Indicative** | The “real” mood: statements of fact, questions, or ordinary narration. |
| **Middle** | A voice that shows the subject acting **for** or **with** himself/herself, often with a sense of “self‑interest”, “benefit”, “reflexivity”, or “passive‑like” meaning. In many verbs the middle has the same meaning as the active (deponent verbs). |
| **‑ω** | The first‑person singular ending of the aorist indicative middle in **contract** verbs where the regular ‑μην has contracted to ‑ω.  (In the regular paradigm the ending is ‑μην; the contracted ‑ω is a later phonological development that appears in many Attic and later texts.) |

So **aorist‑indicative‑middle ‑ω** = “I (once) did something to/for myself (or in a self‑directed way)”.

---

## 2. Formation of the aorist indicative middle  

### 2.1 The regular (non‑contract) paradigm  

| Person | Ending | Example with **πράττω** (to do) – aorist stem **πραξ‑** |
|--------|--------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| 1 sg | ‑μην | **ἐπράξμην** “I did (for myself) / I acted” |
| 2 sg | ‑σο | **ἐπράξσο** “you did (for yourself)” |
| 3 sg | ‑το | **ἐπράξτο** “he/she/it did (for himself/her/it)” |
| 1 pl | ‑μεθα | **ἐπράξμεθα** “we did (for ourselves)” |
| 2 pl | ‑σθε | **ἐπράξσθε** “you (pl.) did (for yourselves)” |
| 3 pl | ‑ντο | **ἐπράξντο** “they did (for themselves)” |

*The aorist stem is formed by adding **‑σ‑** (or **‑θ‑** for some verbs) to the verb root, then attaching the middle endings.*

### 2.2 The contracted ‑ω ending  

In many **contract verbs** (verbs whose stem ends in a vowel) the sequence **‑μην** contracts to **‑ω** after the loss of *m* before a vowel. The result is a single‑syllable ending **‑ω**.

| Verb (present) | Aorist stem | Regular middle 1 sg | Contracted middle 1 sg (‑ω) |
|----------------|-------------|----------------------|-----------------------------|
| **δείκνυμι** “to show” | **ἐδείξ‑** | **ἐδείξμην** | **ἐδείξω** |
| **ἀκούω** “to hear” | **ἤκουσ‑** | **ἤκουσμην** | **ἤκουσω** |
| **πράττω** (contracted form **πράττω**) | **ἐπράξ‑** | **ἐπράξμην** | **ἐπράξω** (rare, poetic) |
| **ἔρχομαι** (deponent) | **ἦλθ‑** | **ἦλθον** (active) → **ἦλθον** (middle) – no ‑μην, but the same idea of a contracted middle form appears in many deponents. |

> **Why does ‑ω appear?**  
> Historically, the sequence *‑μ‑* before a vowel was lost in Attic Greek, and the preceding vowel lengthened, giving the simple *‑ω* ending. The form is especially common in poetry and in later prose where the contracted shape feels more fluid.

## 3. Full paradigm (contracted) for a regular **‑ω** verb  

Below is the complete aorist indicative middle paradigm for a typical contract verb **ἀκούω** “to hear”. The 1 sg form shows the contracted **‑ω**; the other persons keep the regular endings.

| Person | Form | Gloss |
|--------|------|-------|
| 1 sg | **ἤκουσω** | “I heard (for myself / I listened)” |
| 2 sg | **ἤκουσο** | “you heard (for yourself)” |
| 3 sg | **ἤκουτο** | “he/she/it heard (for himself/her/it)” |
| 1 pl | **ἤκουσμεθα** | “we heard (for ourselves)” |
| 2 pl | **ἤκουσθε** | “you (pl.) heard (for yourselves)” |
| 3 pl | **ἤκουσντο** | “they heard (for themselves)” |

*Note:* The aorist stem **ἤκουσ‑** is formed by adding **‑σ‑** to the root **ἀκου‑** (the *s* is the augment‑plus‑σ‑ marker of the aorist). The augment **ἠ‑** marks past time.

## 4. Key rules & common exceptions  

| Rule | Explanation | Example / Exception |
|------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Augment** | All aorist forms receive a **temporal augment** (ε‑ or η‑) at the beginning of the stem. | **ἤκουσ‑** (from **ἀκου‑**) |
| **‑σ‑ vs. ‑θ‑** | Most verbs use **‑σ‑** to form the aorist; a few use **‑θ‑** (e.g., **τίθημι → ἔθηκα**). The middle endings are the same. | **ἔθηκα** (aorist active) → **ἔθημην** (middle) |
| **Contracted ‑ω** | Appears only when the regular **‑μην** follows a vowel and the *m* is lost. More frequent in Attic poetry and later prose. | **ἤκουσω** (instead of **ἤκουσμην**) |
