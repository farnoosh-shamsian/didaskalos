# Lesson: The Aorist · Indicative · Middle (ω-Verbs)

---

## 1. What the Terms Mean

| Term | What it tells us |
|------|------------------|
| **Aorist** | A past aspect that presents the action as a simple, complete whole ("once," "at a stroke," "the fact of doing"). |
| **Indicative** | The "real" mood: statements, simple questions, ordinary narration. |
| **Middle** | A voice showing the subject acting **for itself or with self-involvement** — self-benefit, reflexivity, or (in deponents) a practically active meaning. |
| **‑ω** | In this formation, the **second person singular** ends in ‑ω: **ἐλύσω** "you loosed (for yourself)." Do not confuse this ‑ω with the ‑ω of the first person present active (λύω)! |

So **aorist indicative middle** = "I did something (once) for myself / with reference to myself — stated as a fact."

---

## 2. Forming the Aorist Indicative Middle

### 2.1 The Sigmatic Conjugation (First Aorist)

**Augment ἐ‑ + aorist stem (‑σα‑) + the secondary middle endings.**

| Person | Ending | Example with **πράττω** "to do" — aorist stem **πραξ‑** |
|--------|--------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| 1 sg | ‑μην | **ἐπραξάμην** "I did (for myself)" |
| 2 sg | ‑ω (from *‑σο) | **ἐπράξω** "you did (for yourself)" |
| 3 sg | ‑το | **ἐπράξατο** "he/she did (for himself/herself)" |
| 1 pl | ‑μεθα | **ἐπραξάμεθα** "we did (for ourselves)" |
| 2 pl | ‑σθε | **ἐπράξασθε** "you (pl.) did (for yourselves)" |
| 3 pl | ‑ντο | **ἐπράξαντο** "they did (for themselves)" |

*The aorist stem is root + ‑σ(α)‑; in πράττω the root πραγ‑ combines with σ to give πραξ‑.*

### 2.2 Where Does the 2nd-Person Ending ‑ω Come From?

The old second person singular middle ending was **‑σο**. Between two vowels the σ dropped and the vowels contracted:

**ἐλύσα-σο → ἐλύσα-ο → ἐλύσω**

That is why the second person of the aorist middle ends in ‑ω — a shape that looks like a "present active" but is betrayed by its augment and its σα-stem.

## 3. Full Paradigms

### 3.1 First Aorist Middle: **λύω** "to loose"

| Person | Form | Gloss |
|--------|------|-------|
| 1 sg | **ἐλυσάμην** | I ransomed / loosed for myself |
| 2 sg | **ἐλύσω** | you ransomed |
| 3 sg | **ἐλύσατο** | he/she ransomed |
| 1 pl | **ἐλυσάμεθα** | we ransomed |
| 2 pl | **ἐλύσασθε** | you (pl.) ransomed |
| 3 pl | **ἐλύσαντο** | they ransomed |

*In a war context λύομαι means "to ransom (a captive) for oneself" — the classic illustration of middle meaning.*

### 3.2 Second Aorist Middle: **γίγνομαι** "to become"

| Person | Form | Gloss |
|--------|------|-------|
| 1 sg | **ἐγενόμην** | I became |
| 2 sg | **ἐγένου** | you became |
| 3 sg | **ἐγένετο** | he/she/it became |
| 1 pl | **ἐγενόμεθα** | we became |
| 2 pl | **ἐγένεσθε** | you (pl.) became |
| 3 pl | **ἐγένοντο** | they became |

*In the second aorist the 2nd singular ends in ‑ου (ἐγένε(σ)ο → ἐγένου).*

---

## 4. Key Rules & Common Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Augment** | All aorist indicative forms take the augment: ἐ‑ before a consonant, vowel lengthening in vowel-initial verbs. | ἀκούω → **ἠκουσάμην** (poetic) |
| **Loss of σ between vowels** | In the 2nd singular: ‑σασο → ‑σω; ‑ε(σ)ο → ‑ου. | ἐλύσω, ἐγένου |
| **Keep the three ‑ω's apart** | λύω = present active 1st person; λύσω = future active 1st person; **ἐλύσω** = aorist middle **2nd person** (with the augment!). | The augment is the sure sign |
| **Deponents** | Verbs without active forms take the same endings and translate actively. | ἐδεξάμην "I received" |
| **Irregular stems** | The second aorist middle is built on the altered stem. | λαμβάνω → **ἐλαβόμην** "I took hold of" |
| **Middle ≠ passive in the aorist** | The passive has its separate ‑θην formation: ἐλυσάμην "I loosed for myself" but ἐλύθην "I was loosed." | — |

## 5. Example Sentences

| Greek (aorist indicative middle) | Transliteration | English |
|-----------------------------------|----------------|---------|
| **ἐλυσάμην τὸν αἰχμάλωτον.** | *elysámēn ton aikhmálōton* | I ransomed the captive. |
| **ἐπράξατο χρήματα.** | *epráxato khrḗmata* | He collected money (for himself). |
| **ἐγένετο βασιλεύς.** | *egéneto basileús* | He became king. |
| **ἐδέξασθε τοὺς ξένους.** | *edéxasthe tous xénous* | You (pl.) received the guests. |
| **τί ἐλύσω τὸν ἵππον;** | *tí elýsō ton híppon?* | Why did you untie your horse? |

---

## 6. Usage & Context

1. **Self-benefit** — the middle often says the subject profits from the action: ἐπραξάμην "I exacted (for myself)."
2. **Narrative** — the aorist middle is as fundamental to storytelling as the active: ἐγένετο "it happened" is everywhere.
3. **The second-person trap** — any ‑ω form that carries an augment is a 2nd-person aorist middle, not a present active; internalize this.
4. **Versus the passive** — for "this was done to me" Greek uses ‑θην; the middle always keeps the subject as the doer.
