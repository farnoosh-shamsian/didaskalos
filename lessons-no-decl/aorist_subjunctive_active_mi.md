# Lesson: The Aorist Subjunctive Active of ‑μι Verbs

*Target level: Intermediate*
*Prerequisites: Aorist indicative active of ‑μι verbs, present subjunctive active of ‑μι verbs.*

---

## 1. What This Form Is

The **aorist subjunctive active** of a ‑μι verb expresses a single, complete action in a clause of purpose, probable condition, or indefinite reference:

> ἐὰν **δῷ** — "if he gives"
> ἵνα **θῶμεν** — "so that we may establish"

These are among the shortest words in Greek — often a single syllable — and beginners regularly fail to recognise them.

**The defining feature is the absence of reduplication.** Compare:

| Present subjunctive (reduplicated) | Aorist subjunctive (bare root) |
|---|---|
| διδῶ "that I may keep giving" | **δῶ** "that I may give" |
| τιθῶ | **θῶ** |
| ἱστῶ | **στῶ** |
| ἱῶ | **ὧ** (compound ἀφῶ) |

Same endings, same contraction — only the stem differs, and with it the aspect.

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns

### 2.1 δίδωμι "to give" (aorist root δο‑)

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | **δῶ** | **δῶμεν** |
| 2nd | **δῷς** | **δῶτε** |
| 3rd | **δῷ** | **δῶσι(ν)** |

### 2.2 τίθημι (root θε‑) and ἵστημι (root στα‑)

| Person | τίθημι | ἵστημι |
|---|---|---|
| 1 sg | **θῶ** | **στῶ** |
| 2 sg | **θῇς** | **στῇς** |
| 3 sg | **θῇ** | **στῇ** |
| 1 pl | **θῶμεν** | **στῶμεν** |
| 2 pl | **θῆτε** | **στῆτε** |
| 3 pl | **θῶσι(ν)** | **στῶσι(ν)** |

> **ἵστημι note:** these forms belong to the **intransitive root aorist ἔστην** "I stood." The transitive first aorist ἔστησα "I set up" has a sigmatic subjunctive στήσω, στήσῃς — a different verb-form with a different syntax.

### 2.3 Other Root Aorists of the Same Shape

| Verb | Aorist | Subjunctive 3 sg | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| γιγνώσκω "know" | ἔγνων | **γνῷ** | that he may recognise |
| βαίνω "go" | ἔβην | **βῇ** | that he may go |
| ἵημι "send" | ἧκα | **ᾗ** (ἀφῇ) | that he may release |
| ἁλίσκομαι "be caught" | ἑάλων | **ἁλῷ** | that it may be captured |

### 2.4 Compounds — the Usual Reading Situation

| Compound | Aorist subjunctive 3 sg | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| ἀποδίδωμι | **ἀποδῷ** | that he may pay back |
| παραδίδωμι | **παραδῷ** | that he may surrender |
| ἐπιτίθημι | **ἐπιθῇ** | that he may attack |
| ἀφίστημι | **ἀποστῇ** | that it may revolt |
| ἀφίημι | **ἀφῇ** | that he may release |

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **1. No reduplication = aorist** | δῶ, θῶ, στῶ. If you see δι‑, τι‑, ἱ‑, it is present. | δῷ (aorist) vs. διδῷ (present) |
| **2. No augment** | ἔδωκα (indicative) but δῶ (subjunctive). | |
| **3. Contraction to a circumflex** | δῶ, θῶ, στῶ — the root vowel plus the long subjunctive vowel. | |
| **4. ο‑root gives ῷ; ε/α‑roots give ῇ** | δῷς, γνῷς but θῇς, στῇς. | |
| **5. Watch for iota subscript** | δῷ, ἀποδῷ, γνῷ — the subscript is written and is a useful cue. | |
| **6. 3 pl δῶσι(ν) is ambiguous in shape** | Identical to the present indicative 3 pl of contract verbs and near the present subjunctive διδῶσι; read the stem. | |
| **7. Aspect, not time** | ἐὰν δῷ = "if he gives," never "if he gave." | |

## 4. Example Sentences

| Greek | Translation |
|---|---|
| ἐὰν οἱ σύμμαχοι τὴν πόλιν **παραδῶσιν**, παυσόμεθα. | "If the allies surrender the city, we shall stop." |
| ἔπεμψαν ἀγγέλους ἵνα τὰ χρήματα **ἀποδῶσιν**. | "They sent messengers so that they might pay back the money." |
| ὅταν τοὺς νόμους **θῶμεν**, ἀσφαλεῖς ἐσόμεθα. | "When we have established the laws, we shall be safe." |
| φοβοῦνται μὴ ἡ πόλις **ἀποστῇ**. | "They fear that the city may revolt." |
| ἐὰν τὴν αἰτίαν **γνῶμεν**, ῥᾴδιον ἔσται. | "If we discern the cause, it will be easy." |

## 5. Usage & Context

1. **Future more vivid conditions** — ἐάν + aorist subjunctive: the standard construction for a single hypothetical event.
2. **Purpose clauses** — ἵνα, ὅπως + subjunctive after a primary tense.
3. **Indefinite temporal clauses** — ὅταν, ἐπειδάν + aorist subjunctive for a completed act: "when he has given …".
4. **Clauses of fearing** — μή + subjunctive: φοβοῦμαι μὴ ἀποστῇ.
5. **Prohibitions** — μή + aorist subjunctive in the second person: μὴ δῷς "do not give."
6. **Reading strategy** — strip the prefix, then look for a one-syllable root with ῷ or ῇ. παρα‑δῷ, ἀπο‑στῇ, ἀφ‑ῇ. In Thucydides ἀποστῇ ("that it may revolt") is a recurring pivot of the narrative.

---

## 6. Historical Development & Etymology

- The **root aorist** (ἔδων, ἔθην, ἔστην type) is the oldest aorist formation in Indo-European: a bare root with personal endings and no suffix at all.
- In the subjunctive these roots take the **long thematic vowel**, which then contracts with the root vowel — the reason δο‑ + ω yields δῶ and στα‑ + η yields στῶ.
- The **contrast reduplicated/unreduplicated** is the cleanest surviving expression of the Indo-European aspect system in Greek: imperfective stems are reduplicated, aoristic stems are not.
- Because these forms were so short, they were vulnerable; later Greek gradually replaced them with regularised thematic formations, and the whole ‑μι class disappeared by the medieval period.
