## Introduction to ω-Verbs
ω-Verbs, also known as thematic verbs, are a category of verbs in Greek that are characterized by the presence of the thematic vowel ω (omega) in their stem. In this lesson, we will explore the imperfect tense of ω-verbs in the indicative mood and medio-passive voice.

## Forms and Patterns

## Key Rules and Exceptions
When forming the imperfect tense of ω-verbs, the following rules apply:
- The thematic vowel (ω) is always present in the stem.
- The imperfect tense marker (-ον) is added to the thematic vowel.
- The verb agrees with the subject in person and number.
- The augment ἐ- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb stem in the imperfect tense.
- The medio-passive endings are used, which are distinct from the active voice endings.

## Notes on Usage and Context
The imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past. It can also be used to describe a background action or a situation that existed in the past. The medio-passive voice is used to describe an action that is performed on oneself or to describe a state of being. The imperfect indicative medio-passive is commonly used in narrative texts to provide background information or to describe situations that were ongoing at a specific point in the past.
