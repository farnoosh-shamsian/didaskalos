### Key Rules and Exceptions
The key rules for the 3rd declension (ευ-stem) are:
- The nominative singular form typically ends in ευ or another consonant/vowel combination.
- The genitive singular form usually ends in ος or υος.
- The dative singular form often ends in ι.
- The accusative singular form typically ends in ν or υν.
- The vocative singular form is usually the same as the nominative singular form.

Exceptions to these rules do exist, and it is essential to memorize the specific declensions of common nouns.
