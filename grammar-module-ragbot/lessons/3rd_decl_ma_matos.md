### Introduction to 3rd Declension (μάματος)
The 3rd declension in Greek is a fundamental concept for mastering the language. It encompasses a wide variety of nouns, and understanding its patterns is crucial for effective communication. In this lesson, we will delve into the 3rd declension, focusing on the pattern of μάματος (matos), meaning "battle."

### Explanation of the Grammatical Concept
The 3rd declension, often referred to as the consonant declension, includes nouns that end in a consonant in their stem form. This declension is characterized by its diverse set of endings, which can vary significantly from one noun to another. However, despite these variations, there are recognizable patterns that help in predicting the forms of nouns within this declension.

### Forms and Patterns
The 3rd declension nouns can be divided into several subgroups based on the final consonant of their stem. The most common subgroups include those ending in -τ (tau), -ν (nu), -λ (lambda), -ρ (rho), and -σ (sigma), among others. Each subgroup has its own set of endings for the different cases (nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, and ablative).

Here is a simplified table illustrating the basic pattern for a 3rd declension noun ending in -μα (ma), such as μάματος (matos), meaning "battle":

| Genitive | μάματος | μαμάτων |
| Dative | μάματι | μαμάσι(ν) |
| Accusative | μάματα | μάματας |
| Ablative | - | - |

### Key Rules and Exceptions
One of the key rules in the 3rd declension is that the stem of the noun remains relatively consistent across the different cases, with the endings being added directly to this stem. However, there are numerous exceptions and variations, especially concerning the changes in the stem itself due to phonetic or historical reasons.

For example, nouns ending in -τερ (ter) like πατήρ (patēr), "father," have unique forms in the nominative and vocative singular.

### Example Sentences with Translations
1. **Ὁ μάματος ἦν μέγας.** - The battle was great.
2. **Τῷ μάματι ἐστὶν ἡ νίκη.** - The victory belongs to the battle.
3. **Ὁ μαθητής ἔμαθεν τὸ μάθημα.** - The student learned the lesson.
4. **Ἡ γυνή ἔδωκεν τὸ δῶρον τῷ ἀνδρί.** - The woman gave the gift to the man.

### Notes on Usage and Context
Understanding the context in which a noun is used is crucial for determining its correct form. The 3rd declension nouns can appear in various contexts, from historical accounts to philosophical discussions, and their forms can significantly affect the meaning of a sentence.

Many of its patterns and exceptions can be traced back to the Proto-Indo-European roots of the Greek language.
