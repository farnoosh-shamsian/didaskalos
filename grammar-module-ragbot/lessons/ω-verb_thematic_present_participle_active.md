## Introduction to ω-Verbs
The Greek language is composed of several types of verbs, each with its own conjugation pattern. One of the most common types is the ω-verb, also known as the thematic verb. In this lesson, we will explore the present tense, participle, and active voice of ω-verbs, providing a comprehensive understanding of their forms, patterns, and usage.

## Explanation of the Grammatical Concept
ω-Verbs are characterized by the presence of the theme vowel ω (omega) in their stem. This vowel is a key element in identifying and conjugating these verbs. The thematic vowel ω is added to the root of the verb to form the present stem, which is then used to create the various forms of the verb in the present tense.

## Forms and Patterns
The present tense of ω-verbs follows a specific pattern. The verb λύω (I loose) will be used as an example to illustrate the conjugation of ω-verbs in the present tense.

### Present Tense of ω-Verbs
| Person | Singular | Plural |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1st | λύω (I loose) | λύομεν (we loose) |
| 2nd | λύεις (you loose) | λύετε (you loose) |
| 3rd | λύει (he/she/it looses) | λύουσι(ν) (they loose) |

Another example is the verb ἔλπω (hope).

## Key Rules and Exceptions
- The thematic vowel ω is used in the present tense of ω-verbs.
- The verb stem is modified by the addition of the thematic vowel to form the present stem.
- The participle form is created by adding the appropriate suffixes to the present stem.
- The first person singular of the present tense drops the theme vowel ω and adds the ending -ω.
- The second person singular of the present tense adds the ending -εις.
- The third person singular of the present tense adds the ending -ει.

## Example Sentences with Translations

2. Οἱ ἄνθρωποι λύουσι τὰς πόρτας (The people loose the doors).
