# The 3rd‑Declension (wr_oros) in Classical Greek  

> **Audience:** Beginner‑to‑intermediate learners who already know the Greek alphabet.  
> **Goal:** Understand how nouns of the 3rd‑declension change for case and number, and when to use each form.

---

## 1. What Is the 3rd‑Declension?

Greek nouns are grouped by the shape of their stems.  
The **3rd‑declension** (called *wr_oros* in the source material) contains the majority of everyday words.  

* **Stem type:** ends in a consonant or in a vowel that is not the typical *‑α*, *‑η*, or *‑ι* of the 1st and 2nd declensions.  
* **Gender:** not predictable from the ending; must be memorised.  
* **Cases:** nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, vocative.  
* **Number:** singular and plural (dual is rare and omitted here).

Because the stem stays the same, the noun simply receives a case‑specific ending.

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns

Below are the paradigms for the three common gender groups.  
Each table shows the singular and plural forms for every case.

### 2.1 Masculine – **‑ος** (e.g., *ἄνθρωπος* “man”)

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Nominative** | **‑ος** | **‑οι** |
| **Genitive**   | **‑ου** | **‑ων** |
| **Dative**     | **‑ῳ** | **‑οις** |
| **Accusative** | **‑ον** | **‑ους** |
| **Vocative**   | **‑ε** | **‑οι** |

> **Example – *ἄνθρωπος***  
> Nom. sg. = ἄνθρωπος Gen. sg. = ἄνθρωπου Dat. sg. = ἄνθρωπῷ Acc. sg. = ἄνθρωπον Voc. sg. = ἄνθρωπε  
> Nom. pl. = ἄνθρωποι Gen. pl. = ἄνθρωπων Dat. pl. = ἄνθρωποις Acc. pl. = ἄνθρωπους Voc. pl. = ἄνθρωποι

---

### 2.2 Feminine – **‑α** (e.g., *γῆ* “earth”)

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Nominative** | **‑α** | **‑αι** |
| **Genitive**   | **‑ας** | **‑ων** |
| **Dative**     | **‑ᾳ** | **‑αις** |
| **Accusative** | **‑αν** | **‑ας** |
| **Vocative**   | **‑α** | **‑αι** |

> **Example – *γῆ***  
> Nom. sg. = γῆ Gen. sg. = γῆς Dat. sg. = γῆι Acc. sg. = γῆν Voc. sg. = γῆ  
> Nom. pl. = γῆς Gen. pl. = γῆς Dat. pl. = γῆς Acc. pl. = γῆς Voc. pl. = γῆ

---

### 2.3 Neuter – **‑ον** (e.g., *ἔργον* “work”)

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Nominative** | **‑ον** | **‑α** |
| **Genitive**   | **‑ου** | **‑ων** |
| **Dative**     | **‑ῳ** | **‑οις** |
| **Accusative** | **‑ον** | **‑α** |
| **Vocative**   | **‑ον** | **‑α** |

> **Example – *ἔργον***  
> Nom. sg. = ἔργον Gen. sg. = ἔργου Dat. sg. = ἔργῳ Acc. sg. = ἔργον Voc. sg. = ἔργον  
> Nom. pl. = ἔργα Gen. pl. = ἔργων Dat. pl. = ἔργοις Acc. pl. = ἔργα Voc. pl. = ἔργα

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions

| Rule | What It Means | Example |
|------|---------------|---------|
| **1. Stem + ending** | The stem is unchanged; only the ending varies. | *ἄνθρωπος* → stem *ἄνθρωπ-* |
| **2. Masculine nouns ending in *‑ος*** | Most masculine 3rd‑declension nouns have *‑ος* in the nominative singular. | *ἄνθρωπος* |
| **3. Feminine nouns ending in *‑α*** | Many feminine nouns have *‑α* in the nominative singular. | *γῆ* |
| **4. Neuter nouns ending in *‑ον*** | Neuter nouns often end in *‑ον* in the nominative singular. | *ἔργον* |
| **5. Accusative singular ≈ genitive** | For many nouns, the accusative matches the genitive. | *ἄνθρωπος* → *ἄνθρωπον* (acc.) = *ἄνθρωπου* (gen.) |
| **6. Neuter nominative/accusative identical** | The neuter form is the same in both cases. | *δῶρον* → *δῶρον* |
| **7. Vocative = nominative** | The vocative case is identical to the nominative for all 3rd‑declension nouns. | *ἄνθρωπος* → *ἄνθρωπε* |
| **8. Stem changes in the nominative singular** | Some nouns add an extra consonant or vowel in the nominative. | *ἄνθρωπος* vs. *ἄνθρωπον* |
| **9. Irregular nouns** | A few nouns have unique endings. | *ἄνθρωπος* → *ἄνθρωπον* (acc.) |

### Common Exceptions

| Noun | Declension | Why it’s irregular |
|------|------------|---------------------|
| *ἄνθρωπος* | 3rd | Accusative ends in *‑ον* instead of the expected *‑ου* |
| *γῆ* | 3rd | Feminine, but the stem ends in *‑η* |
| *δῶρον* | 3rd | Neuter, ends in *‑ον* in all cases |

## 4. Example Sentences

| Greek | English |
|-------|---------|
| **ἄνθρωπος ἐστὶν σοφός.** | The man is wise. |
| **γῆ ἔχει πολλὰ ὑψηλὰ.** | The earth has many high places. |
| **δῶρον ἐν τῇ χειρὶ ἔχει.** | The gift is in the hand. |
| **ἄνθρωπος ἀνδρὸς ἐν Ἀθήναις ἔσται.** | The man will be in Athens. |

> **Tip:** Notice how the endings shift with case and number:  
> *ἄνθρωπος* (nom. sg.) → *ἄνθρωπου* (gen. sg.) → *ἄνθρωποι* (nom. pl.) → *ἄνθρωποις* (dat. pl.).

---

## 5. Notes on Usage and Context

* **Frequency:** The 3rd‑declension supplies most everyday vocabulary; mastering it unlocks a large portion of the language.  
* **Gender:** Must be memorised; it is not always predictable from the ending.  
* **Accusative singular:** Often matches the genitive; a handy mnemonic.  
* **Neuter nouns:** Share nominative and accusative forms in both singular and plural.  
* **Vocative:** Identical to the nominative for all 3rd‑declension nouns.  
* **Practical tip:** When you see a noun ending in *‑ος*, *‑α*, or *‑ον* in the nominative singular
