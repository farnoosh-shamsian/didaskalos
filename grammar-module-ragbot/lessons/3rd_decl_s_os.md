# Lesson: 3rd‑Declension Masculine Nouns Ending in **‑ος** (s_os)

---

## 1. What Is the 3rd Declension?

The 3rd declension is the group of Greek nouns that do **not** follow the simple vowel‑ending patterns of the 1st and 2nd declensions.  
Masculine nouns that end in **‑ος** in the nominative singular belong to this group.  
They are the most common masculine nouns in Greek, covering a wide range of meanings: *ἄνθρωπος* “man”, *ἱππός* “horse”, *ἔργον* “work”, etc.

---

## 2. Declension Pattern (s_os)

Below is the full paradigm for a typical 3rd‑declension masculine noun ending in **‑ος**.  
The stem is the part that remains after removing the nominative‑singular ending **‑ος**.

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Nominative** | ὁ **ἄνθρωπος** | οἱ **ἄνθρωποι** |
| **Genitive**   | τοῦ **ἄνθρωπου** | τῶν **ἄνθρωπων** |
| **Dative**     | τῷ **ἄνθρωπῳ** | τοῖς **ἄνθρωποις** |
| **Accusative** | τὸν **ἄνθρωπον** | τοὺς **ἄνθρωπους** |
| **Ablative**   | ἐξ/ἀπό **ἄνθρωπου** | ἐξ/ἀπό **ἄνθρωπων** |

> **Tip:** The genitive singular **‑ου** and dative singular **‑ῳ** are the most distinctive endings for this declension.

---

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **1. Stem + ‑ος** | The nominative singular ends in **‑ος**. | *ἱππός* → *ἱππός* |
| **2. Genitive ‑ου** | The genitive singular ends in **‑ου**. | *ἱππός* → *ἱπποῦ* |
| **3. Dative ‑ῳ** | The dative singular ends in **‑ῳ**. | *ἱππός* → *ἱππῷ* |
| **4. Accusative ‑ον** | The accusative singular ends in **‑ον**. | *ἱππός* → *ἱππον* |
| **5. Plural ‑οι / ‑ους** | Nominative/Accusative plural ends in **‑οι**; genitive/dative plural ends in **‑ων**. | *ἱππός* → *ἱπποί* / *ἱππῶν* |
| **6. Stem‑changing nouns** | Some nouns change the stem vowel in the plural (e.g., *ἔργον* → *ἔργα*). | *ἔργον* → *ἔργα* |
| **7. Irregular genitive** | A few nouns have an irregular genitive (e.g., *ἄνθρωπος* → *ἄνθρωπου*). | *ἄνθρωπος* → *ἄνθρωπου* |
| **8. Accent shifts** | The accent may move in the plural (e.g., *ἱππός* → *ἱππῶν*). | *ἱππός* → *ἱππῶν* |

### Common Exceptions

| Noun | Singular | Plural | Note |
|------|----------|--------|------|
| *ἔργον* | ἔργον | ἔργα | Stem vowel changes (ο → α) |
| *ἄνθρωπος* | ἄνθρωπος | ἄνθρωποι | Genitive *ἄνθρωπου* (regular) |
| *ἱππός* | ἱππός | ἱπποί | Accusative plural *ἱππούς* (accent shift) |

## 5. Example Sentences

| Greek | English |
|-------|---------|
| ὁ **ἄνθρωπος** ἀκούει τὸν **ἄνθρωπον**. | The man hears the man. |
| ἐν τῷ **ἱππῷ** ἔπαιζε ὁ **ἱππός**. | In the horse, the horse played. |
| ὁ **ἔργον** ἐστὶν **ἔργα**. | The work is works. |

> In the second sentence, the dative *ἱππῷ* shows the indirect object “in the horse”.

---

## 6. Notes on Usage and Context

- **Common nouns**: *ἄνθρωπος* (man), *ἱππός* (horse), *ἔργον* (work), *ἰχθύς* (fish), *ἰχθύς* (fish).  
- **Proper names**: Many Greek personal names belong to this declension (e.g., *Ἀλέξανδρος*).  
- **Adjectival agreement**: Adjectives must match in case, number, and gender.  
  Example: *ὁ καλὸς ἄνθρωπος* “the good man”.  
- **Pronouns and articles**: The definite article follows the same pattern (e.g., *ὁ ἄνθρωπος*, *τοῦ ἄνθρωπου*).  

---

## 7. Historical Development & Etymology

- **Proto‑Greek**: The 3rd declension stems from the *‑os* ending that was common in early Greek.  
- **Evolution**: Over time, the endings simplified: *‑os* → *‑ος* in nominative, *‑ou* in genitive, *‑ō* in dative, *‑on* in accusative.  
- **Influence**: The pattern influenced later Greek dialects and the development of the Attic form we use today.

---

### Final Thought

Mastering the 3rd‑declension masculine nouns ending in **‑ος** opens the door to a vast portion of Greek vocabulary. Pay close attention to the stem‑changing nouns and accent shifts, and practice by reading simple sentences that use these forms. Happy learning!
