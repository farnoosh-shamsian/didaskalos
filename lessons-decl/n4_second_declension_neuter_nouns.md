# Second Declension Neuter Nouns (o-stems)

> Draft module. Replace or expand this content with the RAG-generated lesson.

Second declension neuters end in **-ον** (δῶρον "gift", τέκνον "child"). As in all neuters, the nominative, accusative, and vocative are identical, and the plural of those cases ends in -α.

## Paradigm

| Case | δῶρον (sg.) | δῶρα (pl.) |
|---|---|---|
| Nominative | δῶρον | δῶρα |
| Genitive | δώρου | δώρων |
| Dative | δώρῳ | δώροις |
| Accusative | δῶρον | δῶρα |
| Vocative | δῶρον | δῶρα |

## Notes

- A neuter plural subject regularly takes a **singular** verb: τὰ δῶρα καλά ἐστιν.
