# Second Declension Neuter Nouns (o-stems)

Second declension neuters end in **-ον** in the nominative singular: δῶρον "gift", τέκνον "child", ἔργον "work". They share the genitive and dative endings with the masculine -ος nouns; only the nominative, accusative, and vocative differ.

## The neuter rule

Two facts hold for **every** neuter noun in Greek, of any declension:

1. **Nominative, accusative, and vocative are always identical** (in both numbers).
2. **In the plural those three cases end in -α**: δῶρα.

Because subject and object forms look the same, word order and context tell you which is which.

## Paradigm

δῶρον "gift":

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| Nominative | δῶρον | δῶρα |
| Genitive | δώρου | δώρων |
| Dative | δώρῳ | δώροις |
| Accusative | δῶρον | δῶρα |
| Vocative | δῶρον | δῶρα |

## Common vocabulary

| Noun | Genitive | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| ἔργον | ἔργου | work, deed |
| τέκνον | τέκνου | child |
| ζῷον | ζῴου | animal |
| ὅπλον | ὅπλου | tool; (pl.) weapons |
| βιβλίον | βιβλίου | book |
| ἱερόν | ἱεροῦ | temple |

## Points to watch

- **A neuter plural subject regularly takes a singular verb**: τὰ δῶρα καλά **ἐστιν** "the gifts are beautiful" (not εἰσίν). This is one of the most distinctive rules of Greek syntax.
- The genitive and dative (both numbers) are identical to λόγος: δώρου = λόγου, δώρῳ = λόγῳ. Only nominative/accusative/vocative are new.
- The article for neuters: τό (sg.), τά (pl.): τὸ ἔργον, τὰ ἔργα.

## Examples

- τὰ τέκνα παίζει — "the children play" (neuter plural + singular verb)
- τὸ τοῦ θεοῦ ἱερόν — "the temple of the god"
- ἐν τοῖς βιβλίοις — "in the books"
