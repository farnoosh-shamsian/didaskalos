# First/Second Declension Adjectives

> Draft module. Replace or expand this content with the RAG-generated lesson.

Adjectives like ἀγαθός, ἀγαθή, ἀγαθόν "good" decline the masculine and neuter like the second declension (λόγος, δῶρον) and the feminine like the first declension (τιμή, or χώρα after ε/ι/ρ: δίκαιος, δικαία, δίκαιον).

## Paradigm (singular)

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nominative | ἀγαθός | ἀγαθή | ἀγαθόν |
| Genitive | ἀγαθοῦ | ἀγαθῆς | ἀγαθοῦ |
| Dative | ἀγαθῷ | ἀγαθῇ | ἀγαθῷ |
| Accusative | ἀγαθόν | ἀγαθήν | ἀγαθόν |
| Vocative | ἀγαθέ | ἀγαθή | ἀγαθόν |

## Notes

- Many compound adjectives have only two endings: ἄδικος (m./f.), ἄδικον (n.).
- Regular comparatives and superlatives (-τερος, -τατος) also decline this way.
