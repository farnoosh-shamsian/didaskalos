# Third Declension Consonant Stem Nouns

Third declension nouns attach one shared set of endings directly to a stem that ends in a consonant. The catch: the stem is often hidden in the nominative and only shows clearly in the **genitive singular**. That is why third declension nouns must be learned with both forms:

- φύλαξ, φύλακ-ος "guard" (stem φυλακ-)
- σῶμα, σώματ-ος "body" (stem σωματ-)
- γυνή, γυναικ-ός "woman" (stem γυναικ-)

Gender is not predictable from the endings; learn it with the article.

## The shared endings

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| Nominative | -ς or none | -ες (neuter -α) |
| Genitive | -ος | -ων |
| Dative | -ι | -σι(ν) |
| Accusative | -α (neuter = nom.) | -ας (neuter -α) |
| Vocative | usually = nom. or bare stem | -ες (neuter -α) |

## Paradigms

φύλαξ "guard" (velar stem) and σῶμα "body" (neuter dental stem):

| Case | Singular | Plural | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nominative | φύλαξ | φύλακες | σῶμα | σώματα |
| Genitive | φύλακος | φυλάκων | σώματος | σωμάτων |
| Dative | φύλακι | φύλαξι(ν) | σώματι | σώμασι(ν) |
| Accusative | φύλακα | φύλακας | σῶμα | σώματα |
| Vocative | φύλαξ | φύλακες | σῶμα | σώματα |

## Sound changes before -σ

The nominative singular -ς and the dative plural -σι(ν) fuse with the final stem consonant:

| Stem ends in | + σ gives | Example |
|---|---|---|
| κ, γ, χ (velar) | ξ | φύλακ- → φύλαξ, φύλαξι(ν) |
| π, β, φ (labial) | ψ | Αἰθίοπ- → Αἰθίοψ |
| τ, δ, θ (dental) | dental drops | σωματ- → σώμασι(ν) |

## Common vocabulary

| Noun | Genitive | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| ὄνομα (n.) | ὀνόματος | name |
| πρᾶγμα (n.) | πράγματος | thing, affair |
| χρῆμα (n.) | χρήματος | thing; (pl.) money |
| νύξ (f.) | νυκτός | night |
| παῖς (m./f.) | παιδός | child |
| ἐλπίς (f.) | ἐλπίδος | hope |
| χάρις (f.) | χάριτος | grace, favor |

## Points to watch

- **Neuter dental stems in -μα, -ματος** (ὄνομα, πρᾶγμα, σῶμα) are a very large, very frequent class — recognizing -μα as neuter pays off constantly.
- The neuter rule from the second declension holds here too: nom. = acc. = voc., plural in -α (σώματα).
- Some dental stems take accusative singular -ν instead of -α: χάριν "favor" (from χάρις).
- γυνή is irregular in the nominative but regular on its stem γυναικ-: γυναικός, γυναικί, γυναῖκα; pl. γυναῖκες, γυναικῶν, γυναιξί(ν), γυναῖκας.
- Monosyllabic stems accent the ending in the genitive and dative: νυκτός, νυκτί, νυκτῶν, νυξί(ν).

## Examples

- ἡ τῶν φυλάκων ἀρετή — "the excellence of the guards"
- τὰ πράγματα χαλεπά ἐστιν — "the affairs are difficult" (neuter pl. + sg. verb)
- τῆς νυκτός — "by night" (genitive of time)
