# First Declension Feminine Nouns (α/η-stems)

> Draft module. Replace or expand this content with the RAG-generated lesson.

First declension feminine nouns end in **-α** or **-η** in the nominative singular (χώρα "land", τιμή "honor"). Nouns in -η keep η throughout the singular; nouns in -α keep α after ε, ι, ρ ("pure alpha") and otherwise shift to η in the genitive and dative singular (θάλαττα, θαλάττης).

## Paradigms

| Case | τιμή (sg.) | τιμαί (pl.) | χώρα (sg.) | χῶραι (pl.) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nominative | τιμή | τιμαί | χώρα | χῶραι |
| Genitive | τιμῆς | τιμῶν | χώρας | χωρῶν |
| Dative | τιμῇ | τιμαῖς | χώρᾳ | χώραις |
| Accusative | τιμήν | τιμάς | χώραν | χώρας |
| Vocative | τιμή | τιμαί | χώρα | χῶραι |

## Notes

- The genitive plural of all first declension nouns is perispomenon: -ῶν.
- The plural endings are identical for all first declension types.
