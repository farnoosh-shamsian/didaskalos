# First Declension Feminine Nouns (α/η-stems)

First declension feminine nouns end in **-η** or **-α** in the nominative singular: τιμή "honor", χώρα "land", θάλαττα "sea". They are by far the largest group of feminine nouns, and they all share one set of plural endings — only the singular varies.

## The three singular patterns

Which vowel a noun keeps in the singular follows a simple rule:

1. **η-type** (τιμή): the noun has η in all four singular case endings.
2. **Pure-α type** (χώρα): if the stem ends in **ε, ι, or ρ**, the noun keeps α throughout the singular.
3. **Mixed type** (θάλαττα, δόξα): a short α in the nominative that does **not** follow ε/ι/ρ shifts to **η in the genitive and dative** singular only.

## Paradigms

τιμή "honor" (η-type) and χώρα "land" (pure α):

| Case | Singular | Plural | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nominative | τιμή | τιμαί | χώρα | χῶραι |
| Genitive | τιμῆς | τιμῶν | χώρας | χωρῶν |
| Dative | τιμῇ | τιμαῖς | χώρᾳ | χώραις |
| Accusative | τιμήν | τιμάς | χώραν | χώρας |
| Vocative | τιμή | τιμαί | χώρα | χῶραι |

θάλαττα "sea" (mixed type — watch the genitive and dative singular):

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| Nominative | θάλαττα | θάλατται |
| Genitive | θαλάττης | θαλαττῶν |
| Dative | θαλάττῃ | θαλάτταις |
| Accusative | θάλατταν | θαλάττας |
| Vocative | θάλαττα | θάλατται |

## Common vocabulary

| Noun | Genitive | Meaning | Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| ἀρχή | ἀρχῆς | beginning; rule | η |
| δίκη | δίκης | justice | η |
| εἰρήνη | εἰρήνης | peace | η |
| ἡμέρα | ἡμέρας | day | pure α |
| οἰκία | οἰκίας | house | pure α |
| δόξα | δόξης | opinion; glory | mixed |
| γλῶττα | γλώττης | tongue, language | mixed |

## Points to watch

- **The genitive plural of every first declension noun is accented -ῶν** (τιμῶν, χωρῶν, θαλαττῶν), whatever the accent of the other forms.
- The plural endings (-αι, -ῶν, -αις, -ας) are identical for all three singular types.
- The definite article is your friend: ἡ τιμή, τῆς τιμῆς — the article changes with the noun and confirms the case.

## Examples

- ἡ ἀρχὴ τῆς σοφίας — "the beginning of wisdom" (nominative + genitive)
- ἐν τῇ χώρᾳ — "in the land" (dative after ἐν)
- τὴν θάλατταν ὁρῶμεν — "we see the sea" (accusative object)
