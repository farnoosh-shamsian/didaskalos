# Third Declension ι- and υ-Stem Nouns

Nouns like πόλις "city" (feminine) and ἄστυ "town" (neuter) have stems ending in ι or υ that alternate with ε in most cases. Their hallmarks:

- **Genitive singular in -εως** (πόλεως)
- **Nominative and accusative plural in -εις** (πόλεις)

This class matters far beyond πόλις itself: the enormously productive abstract nouns in **-σις** (φύσις "nature", δύναμις "power", κρίσις "judgment") all decline exactly like it.

## Paradigms

πόλις "city" and ἄστυ "town":

| Case | Singular | Plural | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nominative | πόλις | πόλεις | ἄστυ | ἄστη |
| Genitive | πόλεως | πόλεων | ἄστεως | ἄστεων |
| Dative | πόλει | πόλεσι(ν) | ἄστει | ἄστεσι(ν) |
| Accusative | πόλιν | πόλεις | ἄστυ | ἄστη |
| Vocative | πόλι | πόλεις | ἄστυ | ἄστη |

## Common vocabulary

| Noun | Genitive | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| φύσις (f.) | φύσεως | nature |
| δύναμις (f.) | δυνάμεως | power, ability |
| κρίσις (f.) | κρίσεως | judgment, decision |
| ὕβρις (f.) | ὕβρεως | arrogance, outrage |
| πρᾶξις (f.) | πράξεως | action |
| ἰχθύς (m.) | ἰχθύος | fish |

## Points to watch

- **The accent of πόλεως looks wrong but isn't.** An acute on the antepenult before a long final syllable normally isn't allowed; this genitive is a fixed historical exception (from earlier πόληος). Learn it as it is: πόλεως, πόλεων.
- The accusative singular ends in **-ν** (πόλιν), like the first and second declensions — not in -α like most of the third.
- Nominative and accusative plural are identical: πόλεις. Context and the article (αἱ vs. τάς) decide.
- Simple υ-stems keep υ throughout and take the ordinary third-declension endings: ἰχθύς, ἰχθύος, ἰχθύι, ἰχθύν, pl. ἰχθύες, ἰχθύων, ἰχθύσι(ν), ἰχθῦς.

## Examples

- ἡ τῆς πόλεως δύναμις — "the power of the city"
- ἐν ταῖς πόλεσιν — "in the cities"
- κατὰ φύσιν — "according to nature"
