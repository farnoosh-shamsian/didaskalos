# First Declension Masculine Nouns (-ης / -ας)

A group of **masculine** nouns also follows the first declension: nouns in **-ης** (πολίτης "citizen") and in **-ας** (νεανίας "young man"). Most name male persons — agents, professions, proper names — which is why they are masculine despite their first-declension endings.

They differ from the feminine nouns of the previous lesson in only three places:

1. **Nominative singular** adds -ς: πολίτη-ς, νεανία-ς.
2. **Genitive singular** is **-ου**, borrowed from the second declension: πολίτου, νεανίου.
3. **Vocative singular** is its own short form: πολῖτα, νεανία.

Everything else — including the entire plural — matches the feminine paradigm exactly. As with χώρα, the -ας type appears when the stem ends in ε, ι, or ρ.

## Paradigms

πολίτης "citizen" and νεανίας "young man":

| Case | Singular | Plural | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nominative | πολίτης | πολῖται | νεανίας | νεανίαι |
| Genitive | πολίτου | πολιτῶν | νεανίου | νεανιῶν |
| Dative | πολίτῃ | πολίταις | νεανίᾳ | νεανίαις |
| Accusative | πολίτην | πολίτας | νεανίαν | νεανίας |
| Vocative | πολῖτα | πολῖται | νεανία | νεανίαι |

## Common vocabulary

| Noun | Genitive | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| ποιητής | ποιητοῦ | poet, maker |
| κριτής | κριτοῦ | judge |
| στρατιώτης | στρατιώτου | soldier |
| ναύτης | ναύτου | sailor |
| δεσπότης | δεσπότου | master |
| μαθητής | μαθητοῦ | student, disciple |

## Points to watch

- **The genitive -ου is the key signal.** It distinguishes first-declension πολίτης (gen. πολίτου) from third-declension nouns that also end in -ης, like Σωκράτης (gen. Σωκράτους).
- Nouns in -της take the vocative in -α with recessive accent: ὦ δέσποτα "O master!"
- The article resolves any doubt about gender: **ὁ** πολίτης is masculine even though the endings look like τιμή's.
- The genitive plural is -ῶν here too: πολιτῶν, νεανιῶν.

## Examples

- ὁ ποιητὴς λέγει — "the poet speaks"
- οἱ τοῦ στρατιώτου ἵπποι — "the soldier's horses" (genitive)
- ὦ πολῖτα — "citizen!" (vocative)
