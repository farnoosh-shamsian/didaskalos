# Second Declension Masculine Nouns (o-stems)

> Draft module. Replace or expand this content with the RAG-generated lesson.

Second declension nouns in **-ος** are mostly masculine (λόγος "word", φίλος "friend"); a few are feminine but decline identically (ἡ ὁδός "road", ἡ νῆσος "island").

## Paradigm

| Case | λόγος (sg.) | λόγοι (pl.) |
|---|---|---|
| Nominative | λόγος | λόγοι |
| Genitive | λόγου | λόγων |
| Dative | λόγῳ | λόγοις |
| Accusative | λόγον | λόγους |
| Vocative | λόγε | λόγοι |

## Notes

- The article, not the ending, signals the gender of feminine -ος nouns: ἡ ὁδός.
- Contract nouns like νοῦς (νόος) also belong here: νοῦς, νοῦ, νῷ, νοῦν.
